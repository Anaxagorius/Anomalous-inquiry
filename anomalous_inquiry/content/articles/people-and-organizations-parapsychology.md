This reference article catalogues individuals and institutions whose work is materially relevant to the history and current state of parapsychological research. The list spans foundational historical figures, laboratory researchers, affiliated statisticians and philosophers, sceptical investigators, and the primary institutional bodies through which formal inquiry has been conducted. Inclusion does not imply endorsement of any particular interpretation of the phenomena under study.

## Foundational Figures

The late nineteenth century produced the first concerted attempts to apply systematic empirical methods to anomalous phenomena. The individuals below were central to that founding moment.

**Frederic W. H. Myers** (1843–1901) was a classicist and poet who co-founded the Society for Psychical Research in 1882. His two-volume work *Human Personality and Its Survival of Bodily Death* (1903) synthesised the early SPR case archive and proposed a theoretical framework centred on the subliminal self — a domain of mental activity extending beneath ordinary waking consciousness. Myers introduced the term "telepathy" into scientific and public discourse.

**Henry Sidgwick** (1838–1900), the moral philosopher and first President of the Society for Psychical Research, brought to the organisation an emphasis on rigorous evidential standards and sceptical scrutiny. His sustained engagement with survival research reflected his belief that questions about consciousness and personal identity were philosophically irreducible to physiology.

**William James** (1842–1910), American philosopher and psychologist, was a founding member of the American Society for Psychical Research and a long-term correspondent of the SPR. James argued that the investigation of anomalous mental phenomena was a legitimate scientific undertaking and conducted personal investigations into the medium Leonora Piper over many years. His writings on consciousness, particularly *The Varieties of Religious Experience* (1902), remain influential in adjacent fields.

**Edmund Gurney** (1847–1888) was a co-founder of the SPR and the principal author of *Phantasms of the Living* (1886), a large-scale census study of spontaneous cases of apparent telepathy and apparitions compiled from firsthand testimonies. Gurney developed early statistical approaches to evaluating the significance of coincidental correspondences.

**Frank Podmore** (1856–1910) was an SPR member and sceptical investigator who made systematic contributions to the documentation of early psychical research while consistently advocating conventional explanations for reported phenomena. His *Apparitions and Thought-Transference* (1894) and critical biography of Robert Owen remain important historical documents.

## Academic Parapsychologists

The following researchers have conducted or substantially contributed to experimental or field-based investigation of psi-related phenomena within academic or laboratory contexts.

**J. B. Rhine** (1895–1980) established the first dedicated parapsychology laboratory at Duke University in the 1930s. Rhine developed the forced-choice Zener-card paradigm, coined the term "extrasensory perception," and accumulated the first large-scale statistical database in parapsychological research. His book *Extra-Sensory Perception* (1934) brought the field to wide scientific attention. Rhine later founded the Foundation for Research on the Nature of Man, subsequently renamed the Rhine Research Center.

**Joseph Gaither Pratt** (1910–1979) was Rhine's principal collaborator at Duke and contributed significantly to the design and critique of ESP experiments, including the landmark Pearce-Pratt distance series. Pratt later worked at the Division of Parapsychology at the University of Virginia.

**Louisa Rhine** (1891–1983) built and systematically analysed the largest archive of spontaneous case reports in parapsychology, cataloguing thousands of first-person accounts of apparent telepathy, clairvoyance, and precognition. Her *Hidden Channels of the Mind* (1961) brought this material to a general audience and established a framework for classifying spontaneous psi experiences.

**Gertrude Schmeidler** (1912–2009) was a psychologist at City College of New York best known for identifying the "sheep-goat effect" — the observed tendency for participants who believe in ESP to score above chance on forced-choice tasks while disbelievers score at or below chance. Her work introduced personality and attitudinal variables into experimental parapsychology.

**Hans Bender** (1907–1991) was a German psychologist who founded the Institut für Grenzgebiete der Psychologie und Psychohygiene (IGPP) in Freiburg and produced influential investigations of poltergeist cases and mediumship. He was the dominant figure in European academic parapsychology for several decades.

**Ian Stevenson** (1918–2007) was a Canadian-American psychiatrist who chaired the Department of Psychiatry at the University of Virginia before founding the Division of Perceptual Studies. Over four decades Stevenson documented more than 2,500 cases of children who claimed memories of previous lives, producing the most extensive field-based dataset bearing on survival research. His major works include *Twenty Cases Suggestive of Reincarnation* (1966) and the four-volume *Cases of the Reincarnation Type* (1975–1983).

**Jim Tucker** is a psychiatrist at the University of Virginia who continued Stevenson's reincarnation research after the latter's retirement. Tucker's work has extended the dataset to include American cases and has explored connections between the reincarnation case literature and quantum mechanical models of consciousness. His books include *Life Before Life* (2005) and *Return to Life* (2013).

**Charles Honorton** (1946–1992) was a parapsychologist who developed the Ganzfeld technique as an experimental paradigm for free-response ESP research and conducted the autoganzfeld series at the Psychophysical Research Laboratories in Princeton. His 1994 meta-analysis with Daryl Bem reported a cumulative hit rate significantly above chance across multiple independent replications. Honorton was a leading methodological innovator in the field.

**Raymond Moody** (born 1944) is a philosopher and psychiatrist whose 1975 book *Life After Life* introduced the term "near-death experience" (NDE) to scientific and public discourse. Moody's interviews with individuals who reported experiences during clinical death documented a recurrent phenomenological pattern — tunnel, light, life review, border — that has since been investigated systematically by numerous clinical researchers.

**Stanley Krippner** (born 1932) is a psychologist and former director of the Maimonides Medical Center Dream Laboratory, where he conducted a series of controlled experiments on dream telepathy with Montague Ullman during the 1960s and 1970s. Krippner has published extensively on consciousness, shamanism, and anomalous experiences and has contributed to cross-cultural approaches to parapsychology.

**Brian Josephson** (born 1940) is a British physicist and Nobel laureate (Physics, 1973) who has publicly supported the scientific investigation of psi phenomena and proposed connections between quantum mechanics and mind. While not a practising parapsychologist, Josephson has been a recurring presence in debates about the scientific status of the field and has contributed to theoretical discussions of mind-matter interaction.

**Jessica Utts** (born 1952) is a statistician at the University of California, Irvine, who provided a formal statistical evaluation of the US government's remote viewing programme as part of the 1995 American Institutes for Research review. Utts concluded that the statistical evidence for anomalous cognition in the Stargate programme was robust and comparable to other accepted areas of psychology research. Her work has contributed to the methodological literature on effect size and replication in parapsychology.

**Dean Radin** (born 1952) is Chief Scientist at the Institute of Noetic Sciences and the author of *The Conscious Universe* (1997), *Entangled Minds* (2006), and *Real Magic* (2018). Radin has conducted research on presentiment, the Global Consciousness Project, and double-slit interference modulation, and has been a prominent advocate for the meta-analytic case for psi effects.

**Rupert Sheldrake** (born 1942) is a biologist whose theoretical framework of morphic resonance and morphic fields, outlined in *A New Science of Life* (1981), posits collective memory fields operating across organisms and species. Sheldrake has conducted experimental work on claimed anomalies such as the sense of being stared at and telephone telepathy. His work is contested both within and outside parapsychology, and is sometimes characterised as adjacent to rather than within the mainstream of the field.

**Daryl Bem** (born 1938) is a social psychologist at Cornell University who co-authored the influential 1994 Ganzfeld meta-analysis with Charles Honorton and published a highly debated series of experiments on apparent retroactive influence in *Feeling the Future* (2011) in the *Journal of Personality and Social Psychology*. The replication controversy surrounding Bem's 2011 paper contributed to broader discussions of statistical methodology and replication in psychology.

**Etzel Cardeña** is a psychologist and the Thorsen Professor at Lund University, Sweden, who has published widely on hypnosis, dissociation, altered states of consciousness, and parapsychology. His 2018 article "The experimental evidence for parapsychological phenomena: A review," published in the *American Psychologist*, provided one of the most comprehensive recent reviews of the empirical literature.

**William Braud** (1942–2012) was a psychologist and Research Director at the Mind Science Foundation in San Antonio and later at the Institute of Transpersonal Psychology. Braud was a principal developer of the DMILS (distant mental interaction with living systems) paradigm, which measures apparent anomalous influence on the physiological states of isolated participants.

**Michael Persinger** (1945–2018) was a neuroscientist at Laurentian University, Canada, who proposed that geomagnetic and electromagnetic field fluctuations could account for anomalous experiences including apparitions, sensed presences, and out-of-body experiences. His "God helmet" experiments, which used transcranial magnetic stimulation to induce unusual experiences, generated substantial attention and debate. Persinger also conducted research on apparent correlations between geomagnetic activity and reported psi experiences.

**Erlendur Haraldsson** (1931–2020) was a psychologist at the University of Iceland who conducted extensive field research on apparitions, survival cases, and deathbed visions. His collaboration with Karlis Osis produced *At the Hour of Death* (1977), a cross-cultural survey of deathbed observations by medical personnel, and he investigated claimed phenomena associated with the Indian spiritual teacher Sathya Sai Baba.

**Dick Bierman** is a Dutch psychologist affiliated with the University of Amsterdam and the University of Groningen who has conducted research on presentiment — apparent physiological anticipation of emotionally significant stimuli before their occurrence — and on quantum approaches to consciousness and psi. Bierman has also contributed to the methodological literature on unconscious psi effects.

## Sceptical and Critical Figures

The following individuals have engaged substantively with parapsychological claims in a sceptical or critical capacity, contributing to methodological rigour and to the public scientific debate.

**Susan Blackmore** (born 1951) is a British psychologist who conducted parapsychological research for approximately two decades before concluding that the evidence did not support genuine psi phenomena. Her path from investigator to sceptic is documented in *In Search of the Light* (1996). Blackmore has subsequently written extensively on consciousness, memes, and the psychology of anomalous belief.

**Richard Wiseman** (born 1966) is a psychologist at the University of Hertfordshire who has conducted controlled investigations of claimed psychic phenomena, including remote viewing, precognition, and psychic mediumship, consistently obtaining null results. Wiseman has also contributed to the study of the psychology of luck, deception, and mass participation experiments.

**Ray Hyman** (born 1928) is a psychologist and prominent sceptic who contributed the critical half of the joint communiqué with Charles Honorton on the Ganzfeld evidence, acknowledging methodological improvements while contesting the interpretation of the results. Hyman's analyses of cold reading and the psychology of anomalous belief have been influential in both sceptical and parapsychological literature.

**James Alcock** (born 1942) is a psychologist at York University, Canada, and a Fellow of the Committee for Skeptical Inquiry. Alcock has argued that parapsychology lacks a coherent theoretical framework and that its positive results are better explained by methodological artefacts and wishful thinking. His critiques have focused on statistical and design issues in experimental parapsychology.

**Paul Churchland** (born 1942) is a philosopher of mind associated with eliminative materialism who has argued that folk-psychological concepts, including those underlying parapsychological claims, will ultimately be replaced by mature neuroscience. While not a laboratory investigator of psi, Churchland's philosophical work has informed the theoretical critique of survival and psi hypotheses.

## Key Institutional Bodies

**Society for Psychical Research (SPR)** — Founded in London in 1882, the SPR is the oldest continuing institutional body dedicated to the scientific investigation of psychical phenomena. It publishes the *Journal of the Society for Psychical Research* and the *Proceedings of the Society for Psychical Research* and maintains an extensive historical archive.

**American Society for Psychical Research (ASPR)** — Founded in 1884, the ASPR is the American counterpart to the SPR, located in New York City. It maintains a library and archives and has historically supported research programmes in experimental parapsychology.

**Parapsychological Association (PA)** — Founded in 1957 and affiliated with the American Association for the Advancement of Science since 1969, the PA is the primary professional organisation for parapsychological researchers. It publishes the peer-reviewed *Journal of Parapsychology* and organises an annual international conference.

**Rhine Research Center** — Located in Durham, North Carolina, the Rhine Research Center is the successor institution to J. B. Rhine's Duke University laboratory and the Foundation for Research on the Nature of Man. It continues laboratory-based research and outreach in parapsychology.

**Division of Perceptual Studies (DOPS)** — Based at the University of Virginia School of Medicine, DOPS was founded by Ian Stevenson and continues research on survival-related phenomena, including reincarnation cases, near-death experiences, and deathbed visions.

**Institute of Noetic Sciences (IONS)** — Founded in 1973 by Apollo 14 astronaut Edgar Mitchell, IONS conducts and funds research on consciousness and psi phenomena and maintains a membership-based community of interest. Dean Radin serves as its Chief Scientist.

**Institut für Grenzgebiete der Psychologie und Psychohygiene (IGPP)** — Based in Freiburg, Germany, the IGPP was founded by Hans Bender in 1950 and is the primary European academic institution dedicated to research on the psychology of unusual experiences and claimed paranormal phenomena.

**Princeton Engineering Anomalies Research (PEAR) Laboratory** — Operated at Princeton University from 1979 to 2007 under the direction of Robert Jahn and Brenda Dunne, PEAR conducted extensive research on micro-psychokinesis using random event generators and on remote perception. Its accumulated database constituted one of the largest collections of controlled psi data prior to its closure.

**Koestler Parapsychology Unit (KPU)** — Established at the University of Edinburgh following a bequest by Arthur Koestler, the KPU has produced academic research in parapsychology and contributed to meta-analytic and methodological literature.

## Assessment

The individuals and institutions catalogued here span a wide range of epistemological positions, methodological approaches, and interpretive commitments. The archive presents their contributions as part of the documented history of inquiry into anomalous phenomena, without asserting the validity of any specific claim or theoretical framework.
