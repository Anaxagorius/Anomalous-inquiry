On the evening of March 13, 1997, thousands of residents across the state of Arizona — and into Nevada and Sonora, Mexico — reported observing anomalous aerial objects over a corridor running from Henderson, Nevada through Phoenix and Tucson to the Mexican border. The event produced an extraordinary volume of witness testimony, substantial amateur video documentation, and a protracted official response that became a case study in institutional management of mass UAP sightings.

## The Two Distinct Phenomena

Analysis of the Phoenix Lights evidence indicates that the March 13, 1997 event involved at least two distinct phenomena that have been conflated in popular accounts:

### The V-Formation Flyover

Between approximately 7:30 and 10:00 p.m. MST, witnesses across a corridor several hundred miles long reported a formation of lights — variously described as a solid V-shaped or arrowhead-shaped craft, or a triangular formation of individual lights — moving silently from northwest to southeast at low altitude and high apparent angular size.

In Phoenix, the object (or formation) was observed by thousands of witnesses including Dr. Lynne Kitei, a prominent Phoenix physician who photographed and later filmed the lights over a period of months. Multiple witnesses described a craft with a solid outline occluding stars behind it, suggesting a physical structure rather than a formation of independent vehicles.

The formation was reported moving at an estimated speed of 30 mph or less at altitudes ranging from hundreds to thousands of feet, depending on the witness account and estimated size.

No National Guard or Air Force flight operations were officially acknowledged for this time window.

### The Flare Drop over the Estrella Mountains

At approximately 10:00 p.m., a second set of lights appeared over the Estrella Mountain Range southwest of Phoenix. These lights — stationary and arranged in an arc — were widely photographed and filmed, and became the image most associated with "the Phoenix Lights" in subsequent media coverage.

The Arizona National Guard subsequently acknowledged that an A-10 aircraft from the 104th Fighter Squadron dropped high-altitude flares over the Barry Goldwater Range south of Phoenix at approximately 10:00 p.m. that evening. The flares, descending slowly by parachute, would have been consistent with the stationary arc of lights seen from Phoenix.

This explanation, while plausible for the 10:00 p.m. stationary lights, does not address the V-formation flyover reported by thousands of witnesses earlier in the evening over a 300-mile corridor.

## Official Response

Arizona Governor Fife Symington initially held a press conference in April 1997 in which he produced an aide dressed in an alien costume, trivialising public concern. In 2007, Symington reversed his position, publicly stating that he himself had witnessed an enormous structured craft over Phoenix on March 13 and that the event deserved serious investigation. He stated his earlier press conference was an attempt to defuse public anxiety while he sought information from federal officials.

The Air Force maintained that the flare exercise explanation was sufficient. No formal federal UAP investigation of the Phoenix Lights was conducted.

## Scale of the Event

The Phoenix Lights remains, by witness count, one of the largest mass UAP sightings in recorded history. The National UFO Reporting Center and MUFON databases received several hundred reports in the hours and days following the event. Amateur video footage, including recordings by multiple independent witnesses who did not know each other, shows broadly consistent imagery.

The event generated substantial local political pressure. The Phoenix City Council passed a resolution requesting a federal investigation; the request was declined.

## Significance for UAP Research

The Phoenix Lights case illustrates several recurring dynamics in mass UAP sightings:

1. **The contamination problem**: Conflation of two distinct phenomena (the V-formation and the flares) complicates analysis and provides a partial explanation that is sometimes applied to the whole event
2. **Institutional deflection**: Official responses aimed at managing public concern rather than conducting investigation
3. **Witness calibre**: The event was observed by physicians, pilots, law enforcement, and former military personnel, as well as the general public
4. **Video evidence limitations**: Amateur video from the era provides corroboration of the light pattern but insufficient data for photogrammetric analysis of the earlier V-formation

The V-formation flyover component remains without a satisfactory official explanation.
