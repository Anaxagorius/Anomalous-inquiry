A distinctive feature of a significant subset of UAP reports is the presence of physical effects on the surrounding environment, on witnesses, and on nearby technological systems. These effects distinguish CE-2 cases (Close Encounters of the Second Kind) from purely visual sightings, and provide a class of evidence that is independent of testimony reliability.

Documentation of physical effects is particularly significant for UAP research because it can be preserved, measured, and subjected to laboratory analysis — in contrast to eyewitness accounts, which are inherently subject to the limitations of human memory and perception.

## Electromagnetic Interference

The most frequently reported physical effect associated with close-range UAP encounters is electromagnetic interference (EMI) with nearby electronic and mechanical systems. Reported phenomena include:

- **Vehicle engine failure**: Cars, trucks, and motorcycles stalling or cutting out as a UAP passes overhead, then resuming normal operation after the object departs. This pattern was systematically documented by researcher Mark Rodeghier in a 1981 analysis of 441 vehicle interference cases from the CUFOS database
- **Compass anomalies**: Magnetic compasses spinning, deflecting, or performing erratically
- **Radio and communications failure**: Radios cutting out, producing interference, or failing entirely
- **Avionics malfunctions**: Aircraft instrument failures reported during close UAP encounters, including the Tehran 1976 case in which an F-4's weapons system, navigation system, and UHF radio failed sequentially at close range
- **Power grid disruptions**: Local and regional power outages temporally correlated with UAP activity, most notably in a series of events in Brazil in 1999

The electromagnetic interference pattern is consistent across cases spanning multiple decades and countries, involving witnesses with no connection to each other. The mechanism producing the effect is unknown.

## Radar Effects

UAP have been associated with anomalous radar behaviour beyond simple target tracking:

- **Radar jamming**: Reports of radar systems being actively jammed or degraded during UAP encounters, particularly in military aviation cases
- **Velocity anomalies**: Returns displaying instantaneous velocity changes or impossible accelerations on radar displays
- **Simultaneous multi-radar confirmation**: In high-quality cases, the same object is independently tracked by multiple radar systems with consistent characteristics — the strongest form of objective radar UAP evidence

## Ground Trace Evidence

When UAP are reported to land or to hover at very low altitude, physical ground traces have been documented in a substantial number of cases:

- **Indentations**: Circular or triangular patterns of depressions consistent with landing gear contact
- **Scorched or desiccated vegetation**: Vegetation in the affected area showing thermal damage, desiccation, or accelerated death without chemical contamination
- **Soil analysis anomalies**: Laboratory analysis of soil from trace sites has in some cases revealed characteristics attributed to intense heat treatment: increased silicon content, unusual crystalline structures, or reduced organic material

The 1964 Socorro case documented by Project Blue Book showed three pad indentations and scorched vegetation. The Rendlesham Forest case in 1980 showed similar ground features with elevated radiation readings. Physical trace evidence was systematically catalogued by Ted Phillips of CUFOS, who compiled over 3,000 cases with ground traces by the 1980s.

## Radiation-Related Effects

Some UAP cases have been associated with radiation-like physiological effects in witnesses. These include:

- **Skin burns**: Erythema or thermal burns on exposed skin areas, occasionally in patterns consistent with radiation exposure
- **Eye inflammation**: Conjunctivitis or temporary vision impairment in witnesses after close-range encounters
- **Hair loss**: Reported in a small number of close encounter cases
- **Nausea**: Acute nausea and vomiting following close encounters

The most extensively documented radiation case in the UFO literature is the Cash-Landrum incident of 1980, in which three witnesses in Texas reported severe burns, hair loss, nausea, and long-term health consequences following a close encounter with a large, fire-emitting aerial object escorted by military helicopters. Betty Cash subsequently required extended hospitalisation and died in 1998 from injuries her family attributed to radiation exposure. Radiation surveys of the site produced ambiguous results.

John Burroughs, one of the primary Rendlesham Forest witnesses, has pursued compensation through military medical channels for health conditions he attributes to radiation exposure during the 1980 encounter, with partial success.

## Biological Effects on Fauna

Animal behavioural responses have been documented in UAP cases:

- **Dog and livestock disturbance**: Animals in close proximity to UAP encounters typically display signs of extreme distress, including vocalisation, escape behaviour, and trembling
- **Bird silence**: The sudden cessation of nocturnal bird and insect sounds at the approach of anomalous objects has been reported across many case types
- **Cattle mutilation**: A specific and controversial category in which cattle are found dead with precise surgical excisions of specific organs and tissue, without surrounding blood or soil disturbance. Hundreds of cases have been documented in the western United States since the 1970s. Official explanations involving scavengers are not consistently supported by the observed characteristics.

## Physiological Effects on Witnesses

Beyond radiation-type effects, witnesses in close encounter cases have reported:

- **Paralysis**: Temporary inability to move during the encounter
- **Time distortion**: Loss of time disproportionate to the duration of the observed encounter
- **Anomalous burns or marks**: Physical marks appearing on the body after the encounter, the origin of which the witness cannot account for
- **Chronic health effects**: Including sleep disturbance, anxiety, altered sensory sensitivity, and in some cases reported improvement of pre-existing medical conditions

The psychological and physiological impact on witnesses is a research area in its own right, with implications both for witness assessment methodology and for understanding the nature of the encounters themselves.

## Methodological Notes

Physical effects evidence is compelling precisely because it is independent of witness reliability. However, several methodological cautions apply:

1. **Chain of custody**: Physical trace evidence has forensic value only if it is documented, collected, and analysed by qualified investigators promptly after the event
2. **Baseline data**: Assessments of elevated radiation or anomalous soil chemistry require baseline measurements from undisturbed comparison sites nearby
3. **Alternative causes**: Ground traces, burnt vegetation, and even some electromagnetic effects have conventional explanations that must be systematically excluded before anomalous conclusions are drawn
4. **Documentation lag**: Many cases are investigated weeks or months after the event, by which time trace evidence has degraded or been disturbed

The strongest physical effects cases in the literature combine contemporaneous official documentation, multiple independent witnesses, secured physical samples, and laboratory analysis — a standard met by only a small fraction of the total case database.
