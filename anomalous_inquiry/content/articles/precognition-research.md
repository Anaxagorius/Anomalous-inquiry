Precognition refers to reported cases in which information about a future event appears to be acquired before that event occurs through means that cannot be explained by inference, extrapolation, or chance. Retrocausation (sometimes retrocognition) extends this to include the apparent acquisition of information about past unknown events inaccessible to the percipient. Both represent a challenge not only to conventional accounts of sensory function but to standard physical models of causality.

## Historical Background

Reports of precognitive experiences appear across recorded history in the form of prophetic dreams and oracular traditions. The scientific treatment of such claims began in earnest with the SPR's late nineteenth-century surveys, which collected and classified spontaneous cases. J.B. Rhine introduced laboratory precognition paradigms at Duke University in the 1930s, employing forced-choice card-guessing tasks in which participants attempted to identify the order of a shuffled deck before it was randomised.

## Forced-Choice Precognition Studies

Rhine's laboratory reported significant above-chance scores in precognition conditions, with participants performing at comparable levels whether target randomisation occurred before or after their responses. Early methodological criticisms focused on randomisation quality, experimenter handling, and recording procedures. Later experiments employed mechanical shufflers and electronic random event generators to address these concerns.

## The Presentiment Effect

A physiological line of evidence for precognition involves the "presentiment" or "pre-stimulus response" paradigm, originally developed by Dean Radin in the mid-1990s. In this design, participants view a series of randomly interspersed calm and emotionally arousing images while their electrodermal activity (skin conductance) is continuously recorded. Standard event-related designs show elevated skin conductance following presentation of arousing stimuli.

The presentiment finding is that measurable physiological differences between the pre-stimulus baseline for calm versus arousing images appear in the one to three seconds *before* the image is displayed — before the participant or experimenter can know which category will appear. A 2012 meta-analysis by Mossbridge, Tressoldi, and Utts across 26 studies reported a significant effect (combined effect size p < 10⁻¹¹), which they termed the "predictive anticipatory activity" effect.

## Daryl Bem's "Feeling the Future" Studies

In 2011, social psychologist Daryl Bem published a set of nine experiments in the *Journal of Personality and Social Psychology* under the title "Feeling the Future," reporting significant evidence for anomalous retroactive influences on cognition and affect. The most replicated result was a time-reversed priming effect: participants showed faster response times for words that they would later practice — effects that in a conventional direction would indicate recent priming, but in the temporal direction of Bem's design pointed to a future event influencing a past response.

The publication generated substantial controversy, prompted debate about publication bias and replication methodology in psychology more broadly, and produced a range of replication attempts with mixed results. A preregistered 2016 meta-analysis found an overall effect consistent with Bem's original findings (Bem, Tressoldi, Rabeyron, and Duggan, 2016).

## Remote Viewing and Retrocognition

Remote viewing protocols (developed at Stanford Research Institute; see the Stargate Programme article) have in some series incorporated "associative remote viewing" designs in which targets are not disclosed until after the viewer's response — creating a structural precognition element. A subset of remote viewing trials have also targeted past events unknown to the viewer, falling under retrocognition.

## Theoretical Frameworks

Proposed theoretical frameworks for precognition include:

- **Time-symmetric quantum mechanical models** — interpretations in which retrocausal influence is permitted by formalisms such as the transactional interpretation (Cramer) or the two-state vector formalism (Aharonov)
- **Block universe/eternalism** — a philosophical position in which all times exist equally, potentially permitting access to future states
- **Signal nonlocality** — extensions of quantum nonlocality to macroscopic or biological systems

No theoretical framework has achieved consensus or generated independently verified predictions.

## Assessment

The experimental literature on precognition is more extensive than is commonly acknowledged in mainstream science. The methodology has improved substantially from early Rhine protocols. The core challenge remains replication consistency across laboratories and the absence of a mechanistic account compatible with established physics.
