Psychokinesis (PK) denotes the claimed direct influence of mental states, intentions, or volition upon physical systems without any known physical intermediary. The phenomena divide broadly into macro-psychokinesis — large-scale physical effects allegedly observable to multiple witnesses under everyday conditions — and micro-psychokinesis — statistically detectable deviations in the output of random physical systems, typically measurable only across large datasets.

## Historical Background

Macro-PK claims appear extensively in historical accounts of religious phenomena, folk traditions, and the nineteenth-century Spiritualist movement. Physical mediumship — a practice in which purported spirit intermediaries were held responsible for table levitation, materialisation, and object movement — attracted both popular attention and systematic investigation by the SPR from the 1880s onward. Many well-known physical mediums of the Victorian era were subsequently exposed as fraudulent, though a subset of cases remained ambiguous after investigation.

## Experimental Micro-PK

Laboratory interest shifted decisively toward micro-PK following J.B. Rhine's identification of the technical challenge of controlled large-scale effects. Rhine and colleagues in the 1940s conducted dice-throwing experiments in which subjects attempted to influence which face of a rolled die would land uppermost. Meta-analyses of this early literature reported modest but persistent above-chance results.

The most systematic body of micro-PK laboratory research emerged from the Princeton Engineering Anomalies Research (PEAR) laboratory (founded 1979) and from investigations using electronic random event generators (REGs) — devices whose output is derived from quantum or thermal noise sources and is therefore theoretically immune to mechanical bias. Dean Radin and Roger Nelson conducted large-scale analyses of REG data across many operators.

## Macro-PK Case Investigations

Several individuals have attracted sustained scientific investigation for alleged macro-PK abilities:

**Uri Geller** (b. 1946) rose to international attention in the early 1970s. Laboratory investigations at SRI by Targ and Puthoff, and at Birkbeck College by physicist John Taylor, reported anomalous metal-bending effects under controlled conditions. Subsequent investigators, including James Randi, attributed the observed effects to conjuring methods. The scientific status of Geller's claims remains contested.

**Nina Kulagina** (1926–1990), a Soviet citizen, was investigated by Soviet military researchers in the 1960s–1980s. Filmed sessions purportedly showed her causing small objects to move without contact and affecting a frog's excised heart. Western researchers who visited Kulagina in Moscow reported similar observations. Independent scientific replication was never conducted under fully blinded conditions.

**Felicia Parise**, investigated by parapsychologist Charles Honorton in the early 1970s, was reportedly observed moving small objects without contact in informal settings. Laboratory protocols were in early development before she ceased cooperation with researchers.

## Anomalous Perturbation and Intention-Based Research

Beyond individual cases, a line of research has examined whether group intention can produce measurable deviations in random systems. The Global Consciousness Project (GCP), initiated by Roger Nelson in 1998, maintains a global network of REGs and analyses their output for non-random correlations during periods of large-scale collective human attention. The project reports statistically significant deviations during events such as the September 11, 2001 attacks and major broadcast events.

Mind-matter interaction research also includes studies of plant physiology, cell cultures, and water crystallisation, though the latter have attracted strong methodological criticism.

## Mechanistic Proposals

Proposed physical frameworks for PK have included:

- Quantum mechanical observer effects extended to macroscopic scales
- Torsion field theories advanced in Soviet-era research
- Stochastic electrodynamic models linking zero-point field fluctuations to biological systems

None of these proposals has achieved acceptance within mainstream physics, nor have they generated independently verified predictions.

## Assessment

The experimental micro-PK literature — particularly the PEAR REG database and Dean Radin's consolidated analyses — presents a statistically non-trivial anomaly that has not been fully accounted for by mainstream science. Macro-PK claims remain methodologically more problematic, given the difficulty of excluding conjuring and the paucity of fully controlled investigations. This archive documents the existing research record without adjudicating the underlying mechanism.
