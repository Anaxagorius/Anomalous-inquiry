Between December 26 and 28, 1980, a series of anomalous events occurred in Rendlesham Forest, Suffolk, England — adjacent to RAF Bentwaters and RAF Woodbridge, two NATO air bases jointly operated by the United States Air Force. The incidents involved multiple trained military personnel, included reported physical contact with an unidentified craft, and generated an official memorandum that was later declassified and widely studied. The Rendlesham Forest incident is frequently cited as one of the most compelling documented UAP cases in the United Kingdom.

## Night of December 26–27: First Contact

In the early hours of December 26, USAF security personnel at RAF Woodbridge reported observing strange lights descending into the forest. Airman First Class John Burroughs and Staff Sergeant Jim Penniston, responding to investigate what was initially assumed to be a downed aircraft, entered the forest and approached a structured, glowing object resting on the ground.

Penniston described the object as a triangular craft approximately nine feet across, five feet high, bearing embossed hieroglyphic-like symbols on its hull. He reported touching the craft and observing it rise silently and move away through the trees. No sound, exhaust, or propulsion was apparent.

Burroughs corroborated the presence of an illuminated object but described his perception differently, consistent with the perceptual distortions that frequently characterise close-range encounters.

Physical trace evidence was found the following day: three indentations in the ground consistent with landing gear, scorched vegetation, and elevated radiation readings measured with a Geiger counter by Sergeant Nevill.

## Night of December 28: Lt. Col. Halt's Investigation

On the night of December 28, Deputy Base Commander Lieutenant Colonel Charles Halt led a team into the forest to investigate reports of a renewed anomalous presence. Halt carried a tape recorder and documented the investigation in real time — a recording that was subsequently preserved and released.

Halt's team observed a red, sun-like object that pulsed, moved silently through the trees, and at one point split into five separate white objects before reconverging. At a cleared area, the objects appeared to direct beams of light toward the ground and — according to Halt and other witnesses — toward the base itself.

Halt's radiation measurements at the physical trace site from December 26 showed levels he described as significantly elevated. Soil samples from the indentation sites were subsequently reported by independent analysts to contain anomalies consistent with heat treatment.

## The Halt Memorandum

On January 13, 1981, Lt. Col. Halt filed a one-page memorandum to the UK Ministry of Defence describing "Unexplained Lights" in Rendlesham Forest. The memo summarises the key elements of the December 26 and December 28 encounters and records the radiation measurements and trace evidence.

The document was released under the U.S. Freedom of Information Act in 1983 and became the primary official record of the incident. Halt has subsequently stated that the memo deliberately understated the events, as it was intended to inform without causing concern.

## Official and Alternative Assessments

The UK Ministry of Defence investigated the incident and ultimately concluded it presented no threat to national security and had no identified explanation. The MoD's UAP files, released in tranches between 2008 and 2013, confirmed the assessment of unexplained status.

Alternative explanations that have been proposed include the Orfordness Lighthouse (whose rotating light is visible from the forest under certain conditions) and a bright meteor fireball on the night of December 25 that was independently reported across southern England. Neither explanation is fully consistent with the physical trace evidence, radiation measurements, Penniston's account of direct contact, or the December 28 structured-object observations.

## Witness Credibility and Later Testimony

The Rendlesham incident is unusual in the quality and consistency of its primary witnesses. Charles Halt remained credible and consistent in his testimony over more than four decades. Jim Penniston and John Burroughs have both advocated publicly for government disclosure of the full case files, and Burroughs subsequently pursued a campaign to access classified medical records related to radiation exposure he believes occurred during the encounter.

In later interviews, Penniston introduced the claim that he received a binary code during his contact with the object, which he transcribed from memory and which, when decoded, produced a message. This claim is additional testimony of uncertain evidential weight and is noted here for completeness.

## Classification

The Rendlesham Forest incident qualifies as CE-2 under the Hynek system: a close encounter with physical environmental effects, including ground traces, elevated radiation, and multiple independent witnesses at close range. The December 26 encounter additionally meets CE-3 criteria by Penniston's account of direct craft contact.
