On the morning of April 6, 1966, in the suburb of Clayton South, Melbourne, Victoria, Australia, more than 200 students and teachers at Westall High School and Westall State School reported observing an unidentified aerial object descend into an adjacent field, come to rest briefly, and then depart at high speed. The incident was witnessed in broad daylight by a large number of people in close proximity. Subsequent official and unofficial responses — including apparent military attendance and instructions to witnesses to remain silent — have kept the case in active discussion for decades.

The Westall incident is the largest mass-witness daytime UAP sighting in Australian history.

## The Sighting: Morning of April 6, 1966

### Initial Observation

At approximately 11:00 AM, during a school morning break, students and teachers at Westall High School (grades 7–12) and the adjacent Westall State School observed an object or objects in the sky to the south of the school grounds. The observation occurred under clear daytime conditions.

Witnesses described a round or oval disc-shaped object, approximately the size of two family cars in diameter, silver or grey in colour with a slight purple hue. The object was observed descending toward the Grange Reserve — a grassy paddock area behind the school — and appeared to land or hover very close to the ground.

### Ground-Level Observations

A number of students broke from the school grounds and ran across the oval toward the Grange Reserve to investigate. Several reported approaching to within close range of the object. Their accounts describe:

- A circular craft resting on or very near the ground
- A dome or raised centre section
- No visible windows, seams, or propulsion mechanisms
- A depression or swirl in the grass where the object had been
- The object departing rapidly, rising at speed, and disappearing within seconds

One student, Jacqueline Argent, described running toward the object and observing it at close range before it shot upward. She and several other witnesses reported feeling unwell in the aftermath, describing dizziness and lightheadedness that they attributed to proximity to the craft.

### Multiple Objects

Several witnesses described more than one object. Some accounts include a second, smaller object, described as a more conventionally shaped aircraft — possibly a light plane — that appeared to circle the area. Whether these two objects were related, and what the second craft was, has never been established.

### The Departure

Witnesses who reached the reserve reported that the main object rose rapidly and departed at high speed to the northwest, leaving a circular impression in the long grass of the field. The impression was later examined by some witnesses and reported to show swirled or flattened vegetation in a roughly circular pattern.

## The Aftermath: Official Response

### Physical Trace Evidence

In the hours following the sighting, the circular area of flattened grass was visible in the reserve. Several students and at least one teacher observed it before it was obscured or removed. Attempts by students to return to the site in subsequent days found the grass had been cut or the area otherwise altered. The physical trace evidence was not formally documented by any official body before it disappeared.

### Military and Official Presence

Multiple witnesses have described the arrival of uniformed military personnel and civilians at the school in the hours following the sighting. Witnesses report that:

- Aircraft or vehicles arrived at or near the reserve and the school
- Official personnel spoke to the school principal and teachers
- Students were subsequently told — by teachers and in some cases by the visitors directly — not to discuss what they had seen
- The school principal, Frank Samblebe, reportedly instructed the science teacher, Andrew Greenwood, who had also witnessed the event, to keep quiet

Greenwood later confirmed that he had been instructed to say nothing and had complied with this at the time, though he subsequently described his sighting on record.

### Press Coverage and Suppression

The local Dandenong Journal published a brief report on the incident. Further press coverage was limited. Several witnesses have stated in later interviews that there was an active effort to suppress the story in the days following the event.

## Witnesses: Recorded Accounts

The Westall incident is notable for the volume and consistency of witness testimony accumulated over decades. Key witnesses include:

**Andrew Greenwood** — Science teacher at Westall High School. Observed the object from the school grounds. Described it as a silver, disc-shaped craft. Was instructed not to discuss the sighting and complied for many years before giving recorded interviews.

**Jacqueline Argent** — Student who ran to the reserve and reported observing the craft at close range. Her account includes the closest reported approach distance and describes a grass impression where the craft had been.

**Joy Clarke** — Student witness who described seeing the object descend and hover near the ground from the school oval.

**Terry Peck** — Student witness who described seeing two aircraft-type objects, one of which he identified as a conventional light plane, near the larger object.

Multiple other former students have given recorded testimony, particularly in connection with the 2010 documentary *Westall '66: A Suburban UFO Mystery* directed by Rosie Jones.

## Conventional Explanations

The following conventional explanations have been proposed and assessed:

**Weather or research balloon**: No balloon descent matching the described characteristics — a rapidly moving, disc-shaped object landing and departing at high speed — has been identified for this date and location. The Bureau of Meteorology records for April 6, 1966, do not show any balloon activity consistent with the sighting.

**RAAF or military aircraft**: No declassified records have been produced identifying a RAAF or U.S. military aircraft operating over Clayton South on the morning of April 6, 1966. The RAAF subsequently denied knowledge of any official activity at the site, though witnesses describe uniformed personnel arriving at the school.

**Experimental aircraft**: Proposed but unsupported. No classified experimental programme has been identified that accounts for the described characteristics.

**Mass suggestion or hoax**: Given the number of independent adult witnesses — including trained teachers observing under good conditions — and the consistency of accounts over decades, a mass suggestion or hoax explanation requires a higher evidential threshold than is supported by the available record.

No official investigation was ever conducted, and no official explanation has been issued.

## Institutional Response and Subsequent Research

The incident received no formal investigation from the Royal Australian Air Force or any Australian government agency at the time. RAAF records released under subsequent freedom of information requests have not produced documentation relevant to April 6, 1966, in the Clayton South area.

Researcher Keith Basterfield investigated the case and collected witness statements in the 1990s, contributing to the academic UAP literature on the incident. The Westall incident was included in Vallée's *Dimensions* (1988) as a significant documented case.

The 2010 documentary *Westall '66: A Suburban UFO Mystery* reunited numerous witnesses and presented their accounts on record. The documentary contributed substantially to the public preservation of witness testimony.

In 2021 and subsequent years, researchers continued to attempt to locate any surviving official documents relating to military activity on April 6, 1966, in the Clayton South area.

## CE Classification

**CE-1**: The primary classification. An unidentified aerial object was observed at close range by more than 200 witnesses under good daytime conditions.

**CE-3 (alleged)**: A subset of witnesses, including Jacqueline Argent, report approaching the craft at very close range. No entity or occupant is reported; the CE-3 element refers to the reported ground contact and proximity of the craft rather than observed intelligent entities. Some researchers classify the case as CE-2 due to the physical trace evidence (ground impression). Classification is CE-1 / CE-2 by most formal analyses, with CE-3 notes reflecting the claimed close-contact proximity.

## Disclaimer

This article documents the Westall School incident as a historical case record. Witness accounts are preserved as reported and do not constitute validated factual claims about the nature of the observed object. CE classification reflects reported characteristics. The presence of uniformed personnel and reported suppression of witness accounts are documented claims; no official confirmation has been obtained.
