🧠 Altered States of Consciousness

← Home

Research into non-ordinary states of consciousness, their phenomenology, and intersection with anomalous experience. Across reports, the Phenomenon appears correlated not to fixed external conditions but to shifts in consciousness, perception, and meaning-processing.

I. Physiological / Neurological States

🌫️ Hypnagogic States

Sleep-onset imagery, sleep paralysis, entity perception at the waking threshold.

🌙 Lucid Dreaming

Conscious dreaming, sleep laboratory verification, false awakening, psi research.

💭 Dream Telepathy

Maimonides Dream Lab, shared dreaming, visitational and teaching dreams.

🔥 Trauma-Induced States

Acute dissociation, derealization, missing time, and the Phenomenon as traumatic encounter.

II. Pharmacologically Induced States

🔆 Visionary / Psychedelic States

DMT, psilocybin, LSD, mescaline — autonomous entities, hyperdimensional spaces.

🪞 Dissociation

Depersonalization, derealization, ego dissolution, fantasy-proneness and psi.

Summary Frame: Across reports, the Phenomenon appears correlated not to fixed external conditions, but to shifts in consciousness, perception, and meaning-processing. Researchers including Vallée, Keel, Strieber, Pasulka, and Persinger suggest that altered states may be a feature of contact — not a side effect — and that the boundary between observer and event may be non-separable.
