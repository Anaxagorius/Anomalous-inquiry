📁 CE Archive

J. Allen Hynek's classification system organizes UAP encounters by proximity and type. Close Encounters of the First Kind involve visual sightings within 500 feet; CE2 involves physical effects on the environment or witnesses; CE3 involves reported occupants; CE4 (added later) covers reported abduction.

Physical trace evidence cases catalogued by Ted Phillips (over 4,000 cases as of 2020) include soil compression, anomalous radiation readings, dehydration of organic matter, and electromagnetic effects. Some cases have been independently verified by soil chemists and physicists.

Notable CE2 cases include the Ubatuba, Brazil fragments (1957), Delphos, Kansas ring (1971), and Trans-en-Provence, France (1981 — investigated by GEPAN/SEPRA, the French government UAP office). Physical evidence in most cases has not been conclusively explained.
