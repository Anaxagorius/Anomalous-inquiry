The Hynek Classification System

J. Allen Hynek (1910–1986) was an astrophysicist and professor at Northwestern University who served as scientific consultant to the U.S. Air Force's Project Blue Book from 1948 to 1969. Initially a skeptic hired to debunk reports, Hynek's long engagement with the evidence led him to conclude that a residual category of genuinely unexplained cases warranted serious scientific attention. He founded the Center for UFO Studies (CUFOS) in 1973 and developed the classification system that remains the standard framework for close encounter research.

The Hynek scale describes UAP reports by proximity and type of interaction:

  • CE1 (Close Encounter of the First Kind): visual sighting within approximately 500 feet, with sufficient apparent size and detail to exclude conventional explanation
  • CE2 (Second Kind): physical effects on the environment or witnesses — ground traces, interference with engines or electrical systems, radiation effects, physiological symptoms
  • CE3 (Third Kind): reported occupants or entities associated with a UAP
  • CE4 (Fourth Kind): reported abduction or transportation of witnesses (this category was added by later researchers, not Hynek himself)
  • CE5 (Fifth Kind): claimed human-initiated contact or communication with UAP occupants (a later extension, associated with the CSETI protocols of Steven Greer)

Physical Trace Evidence

CE2 cases involving physical traces on the environment represent some of the most objectively documentable UAP evidence. Ted Phillips catalogued over 4,000 physical trace cases globally as of the 2010s. Documented effects include:

  • Soil compression and dehydration of organic matter at alleged landing sites
  • Anomalous radiation readings in soil samples above normal background levels
  • Magnetic anomalies detectable weeks or months after reported events
  • Vegetation damage patterns inconsistent with conventional mechanical causes

The Delphos, Kansas case (November 1971) produced one of the most extensively analysed physical traces. A teenager reported a glowing mushroom-shaped craft hovering close to the ground, leaving a luminous ring in the soil. Laboratory analysis at the University of Kansas found the soil inside the ring had anomalously low water absorption rates, elevated protein and carbohydrate concentrations, and chemical properties inconsistent with known natural causes. The MUFON team that investigated the case found no conventional explanation.

The Trans-en-Provence, France case (1981) was investigated by GEPAN (Groupement d'Étude des Phénomènes Aérospatiaux Non-identifiés), the French government's official UAP investigation office. Physical traces on the ground and on nearby alfalfa plants showed chemical and biological changes that the investigating scientist, Dr. Michel Bounias, attributed to powerful electromagnetic or other energetic effects. The GEPAN report remains one of the most rigorous government analyses of physical trace evidence.

Notable CE Cases

Socorro, New Mexico (April 1964): Police officer Lonnie Zamora reported a white egg-shaped object with landing legs on the ground, with two humanoid figures nearby. As he approached, the object departed with a roar and blast of flame. The case was investigated by Project Blue Book and by Hynek himself, who considered it one of the most credible and puzzling cases in the official record. Physical traces — scorched ground and disturbed soil — were documented and photographed.

Pascagoula, Mississippi (October 1973): Charles Hickson and Calvin Parker reported being levitated into an object while fishing. Both men were separately interviewed (to prevent collusion), and their accounts showed consistency. Sheriff Fred Diamond found both men genuinely traumatised. A hidden recording made while the men were left alone in the interrogation room — expecting not to be observed — showed them continuing to discuss the experience in terms inconsistent with deliberate hoax.

Travis Walton, Arizona (November 1975): A logging crew of seven witnessed a crew member apparently struck by a beam of light from a hovering disc and apparently disappear. Walton reappeared five days later in a nearby town, disoriented. Six of the seven crew members passed polygraph examinations. The case remains among the most investigated in the American close encounter literature.

Kecksburg, Pennsylvania (December 1965): A large fireball was observed across multiple U.S. states and Canada. Witnesses in Kecksburg reported a metallic acorn-shaped object in the woods, with military personnel arriving shortly afterward and cordoning the area. Object retrieval was reported by multiple witnesses. Official explanations have been disputed by researchers, and NASA's response to a FOIA request produced missing or incomplete records.

International Cases

Westall, Australia (April 1966): Over 200 students and staff at Westall High School in Melbourne observed a silver disc descend, briefly land, and depart at high speed in front of multiple witnesses. Some students reported running toward the landing site and observing a flattened circle in the grass. Despite the scale of the sighting, media coverage was suppressed and witnesses reported being told not to discuss the event.

Ariel School, Zimbabwe (September 1994): Approximately 62 children aged 5–12 at Ariel School reported a craft landing near their playground during recess. Several children described small dark figures near the object. Researcher John Mack (Harvard) and journalist Cynthia Hind interviewed the children separately; their accounts showed remarkable consistency. The children's drawings produced independently showed consistent features of the object and figures.

Varginha, Brazil (January 1996): Multiple residents of Varginha reported seeing humanoid beings with brown oily skin, large red eyes, and prominent heads in the days following an observed light in the sky. Brazilian military units were observed in unusual deployment patterns. The Brazilian government has not officially acknowledged the incident, but local testimony from military and medical personnel has been extensively documented by Brazilian researchers.

Research Organisations and Databases

The Mutual UFO Network (MUFON), founded in 1969, maintains the largest civilian UAP case database, with over 120,000 reports as of the 2020s. MUFON trains field investigators and applies standardised reporting forms to maintain case comparability.

The National UFO Reporting Center (NUFORC), directed by Peter Davenport, operates a 24-hour telephone hotline and online reporting system for UAP witnesses. The NUFORC database contains over 170,000 reports and is frequently referenced by journalists and researchers.

NARCAP (National Aviation Reporting Center on Anomalous Phenomena), founded by aviation safety specialist Richard Haines, focuses specifically on UAP reports from professional pilots and aviation personnel. NARCAP has documented hundreds of aviation-context cases where anomalous objects presented potential airspace safety concerns, and has advocated for formal reporting channels within civil aviation.

CE5 and CSETI (Controversial)

Steven Greer's Center for the Study of Extraterrestrial Intelligence (CSETI) advocates a protocol he terms CE5 — human-initiated contact using meditation, light signals, and coherent intention directed at UAP. CSETI has documented hundreds of alleged responses to these protocols. The methodology is regarded sceptically within the broader UAP research community, which notes the difficulty of distinguishing genuine anomalous responses from confirmation bias in ambiguous sky observations. Greer's broader claims about extraterrestrial agreements and classified programmes are disputed.

Physical Effects on Witnesses

CE2 and CE3 cases frequently involve physiological effects on witnesses, including radiation burns, eye inflammation, anomalous skin lesions, and psychological trauma. Aviation-context CE cases have documented electromagnetic interference with aircraft instruments at close range. Some researchers, including nuclear physicist Stanton Friedman and GEPAN investigators, have argued that the consistency of these physiological and physical effects across unconnected witnesses worldwide constitutes evidence that the phenomenon has genuine physical reality, whatever its ultimate origin.
