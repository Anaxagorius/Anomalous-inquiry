🦶 Cryptozoology

← Home

A documentary archive of alleged unknown animals drawn from field investigation records, indigenous testimony, physical trace evidence, photographic and film evidence, zoological analysis, and comparative folklore. All categories represent claims and hypotheses rather than confirmed biological taxa. Entries follow the principle of zoological neutrality: what has been reported or documented, by whom, and under what conditions of observation. Cryptozoology is defined here as the study of hidden or unknown animals reported across cultures and regions, distinct from paranormal phenomena.

Entity Categories

🦴 I. Hominid / Humanoid Cryptids

Bigfoot, Yeti, Yowie, Almas, Orang Pendek, and other bipedal hominid-morphology reports worldwide.

🐺 II. Canid-Type Cryptids

Oversized or morphologically anomalous wolf/dog-type cryptids: Beast of Gévaudan, Dogman, Waheela, Onza.

🐆 III. Feline Cryptids

British Big Cats, Queensland Tiger, Marozi, Ngoloko, sabertooth survival hypotheses.

🦎 IV. Reptilian & Amphibian Cryptids

Mokele-Mbembe, Kasai Rex, giant monitor variants, Loveland Frogman, giant salamanders.

🌊 V. Aquatic & Semi-Aquatic Cryptids

Loch Ness Monster, Ogopogo, Champ, sea serpents, Kraken, deep-sea unknowns.

🦅 VI. Avian Cryptids

Thunderbird, Kongamato, Ahool, Haast's Eagle survival hypothesis, giant condor variants.

🕷️ VII. Insectoid / Arthropod Cryptids

Giant spiders (J'ba Fofi), giant ants, Mothman (morphological classification).

🦉 VIII. Hybrid / Chimera Cryptids

Owlman, Mothman, Goatman, bat-beast variants — treated zoologically, not paranormally.

🦕 IX. Fossil-Survivor Cryptids

Thylacine, Megalodon, plesiosaur survival hypotheses, Denisovan-like hominids, Moa.

🌍 X. Regional / Indigenous-Reported Cryptids

Chupacabra, Nandi Bear, Grootslang, Bunyip, Black Shuck — zoological framings of traditional reports.

✈️ XI. Aerial Anomalous Animals

Flying rods, sky serpents, atmospheric jellyfish, winged serpent variants — non-avian flight anomalies.

❓ XII. Unknown Classification / Miscellaneous

Rakes, Pale Crawlers, tunnel dwellers, hairless humanoid cryptids, unclassified regional entities.

This archive presents reported phenomena and zoological hypotheses without endorsement. All categories represent claims derived from witness testimony, indigenous oral tradition, physical trace evidence, and field investigation. No conclusions about biological validity, taxonomic status, or evolutionary origin are made herein. The term "cryptid" denotes an animal whose existence is alleged but not confirmed by mainstream zoology.
