🪞 Dissociation

Dissociative experiences range from mild absorption and derealization to full dissociative identity disorder (DID). The relationship between dissociation and anomalous experience is complex — heightened dissociativity correlates with fantasy-proneness and anomalous belief, but also with genuine reports of anomalous events in case literature.

Michael Persinger's work with the Koren helmet and weak magnetic field stimulation induced a range of reported experiences including sensed presences, OBE-like feelings, and anomalous perceptions. Critics noted placebo effects; Persinger's findings remain contested.

The study of dissociation in trauma-related contexts intersects with anomalous experience research in areas including recovered memory, poltergeist cases centered on traumatized individuals, and claimed past-life memories in DID patients.
