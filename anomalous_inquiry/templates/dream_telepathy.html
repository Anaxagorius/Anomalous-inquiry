💭 Dream Telepathy

The Maimonides Medical Center Dream Laboratory, operating 1962–1978 under Montague Ullman and Stanley Krippner, conducted systematic studies of psychic influences on dream content. A sender viewed a randomly selected art print while a receiver slept in a nearby room under EEG monitoring.

Blind judges rated correspondence between dream transcripts and target images. The overall hit rate significantly exceeded chance across multiple series. Honorton's 1977 meta-analysis of the Maimonides data supported above-chance effects.

Independent replications produced mixed results. The Dream Laboratory eventually closed due to funding constraints. Theoretical frameworks proposed to explain dream-state psi include reduced sensory noise during REM sleep and altered states facilitating psi reception.
