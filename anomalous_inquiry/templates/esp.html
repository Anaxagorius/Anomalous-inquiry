🔮 Extrasensory Perception

Extrasensory perception (ESP) refers to the claimed reception of information not gained through recognized physical senses. Laboratory research has examined three principal forms: telepathy (apparent mind-to-mind communication), clairvoyance (perception of remote objects or events), and remote sensing.

Meta-analyses of forced-choice ESP experiments by Honorton and Ferrari (1989) found effect sizes significantly above chance. Critics note methodological concerns including optional stopping, inadequate randomization, and file-drawer effects.

Key figures include J.B. Rhine (Duke University), who coined the term ESP and developed Zener card-guessing protocols. Rhine's parapsychology laboratory, founded 1930, ran thousands of trials using strict double-blind conditions for the era.
