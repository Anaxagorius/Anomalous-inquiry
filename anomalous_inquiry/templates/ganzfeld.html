🎭 Ganzfeld Experiments

The Ganzfeld technique (German: "complete field") creates mild sensory deprivation — white noise, halved ping-pong balls over the eyes, diffuse red lighting — to reduce internally generated mental noise, theoretically improving psi signal detection.

In the standard protocol, a "receiver" in Ganzfeld isolation attempts to identify which of four images or clips is being mentally "transmitted" by a remote sender. Chance hit rate is 25%. Meta-analyses by Honorton (1985) and Bem & Honorton (1994) reported approximately 35% hit rates.

The autoganzfeld studies (Honorton et al., 1990) used automated randomization and computer-assisted judging to address procedural criticisms. Subsequent meta-analyses have yielded inconsistent results.
