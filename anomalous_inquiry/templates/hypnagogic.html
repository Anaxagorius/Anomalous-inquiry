🌫️ Hypnagogic States

Hypnagogic states occur at the threshold between wakefulness and sleep. They are characterized by vivid imagery, phosphenes, sounds, and occasionally tactile sensations that arise without external stimulus. Hypnopompic states are their mirror image on waking.

Reports of anomalous content in hypnagogic states include apparent visitations, voices with information unknown to the experiencer, and imagery matching future events. Andreas Mavromatis (1987) provided the most comprehensive academic survey of hypnagogia across history, cultures, and experimental literature.

The hypnagogic state has been deliberately cultivated by inventors and artists, including Thomas Edison's "key method" and Salvador Dalí's purported use of a spoon balanced over a plate. Neurologically, it corresponds to Stage 1 NREM sleep with mixed alpha/theta EEG activity.
