🌙 Lucid Dreaming

Lucid dreaming — dreaming with awareness that one is dreaming — was verified in a sleep laboratory by Keith Hearne (1975) and Alan Worsley, and independently by Stephen LaBerge at Stanford (1980–81), using pre-agreed eye-movement signals to communicate from within REM sleep.

LaBerge's MILD (Mnemonic Induction of Lucid Dreams) and WILD (Wake-Initiated Lucid Dream) techniques, along with the Novadreamer device, became widely used induction methods. Lucid dreaming intersects with parapsychology through proposed shared dreaming and attempts to meet designated deceased individuals.

Research has also explored therapeutic applications for nightmare disorder (PTSD) and motor skill rehearsal, with neuroimaging studies identifying prefrontal reactivation as a correlate of lucidity onset.
