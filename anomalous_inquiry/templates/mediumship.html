📡 Mediumship Research

Mediumship research investigates ostensible communication between living individuals and deceased persons, primarily through scientific assessment of medium accuracy, information specificity, and conditions ruling out cold reading or other mundane explanations.

The Society for Psychical Research (SPR), founded 1882, produced extensive cross-correspondence studies with Frederic Myers and others. Contemporary work by Gary Schwartz at the Human Energy Systems Laboratory (Arizona) used triple-blind protocols comparing medium responses against controls.

Criticisms center on blinding failures, rater subjectivity, and the difficulty of ruling out statistical noise in ambiguous statements. Julie Beischel at the Windbridge Research Center developed standardized protocols for medium research with multiple control conditions.
