Methodology

Anomalous Inquiry documents reported anomalous phenomena using a neutral, journalistic standard. We neither validate nor debunk claims.

Principles

  • Documentary neutrality — reports are logged as reported, not interpreted.
  • Primary source priority — original testimony, official documents, and contemporaneous records preferred.
  • Strict anonymity — comments and suggestions carry no identifying information.
  • CE classification — encounters follow the Hynek Close Encounter scale (CE1–CE5) where applicable.
  • Citation standards — APA and Chicago formats available on every article.

Classification Key

  • UAP — Unidentified Aerial/Aerospace Phenomenon
  • CE1 — Visual sighting within 500 ft, no physical effects
  • CE2 — Physical trace evidence
  • CE3 — Occupants observed
  • CE4 — Abduction or direct contact
  • CE5 — Human-initiated contact
