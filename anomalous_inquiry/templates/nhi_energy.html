⚡ Energy / Non-Corporeal Entities

The energy and non-corporeal entity category encompasses reported intelligences that appear to lack a fixed biological or mechanical body — entities that manifest as light, electromagnetic fields, energy patterns, shadows, or non-physical consciousness structures. These phenomena bridge the physical sciences, parapsychology, and ontological philosophy: they are among the most frequently reported anomalous entities worldwide yet remain the least tractable to conventional scientific frameworks. The category is unified not by morphological similarity but by the apparent absence or irrelevance of physical substrate: whatever these phenomena are, they do not appear to be made of matter in any straightforward sense.

What Defines a Non-Corporeal Entity?

Across the reported phenomena gathered here, several properties recur as markers of the non-corporeal type:

  • No stable physical body: The entity does not maintain consistent mass, form, or material substrate. It may appear as light, as absence (shadow), as field, or as a non-visual presence felt rather than seen.
  • Apparent intelligence or intentionality: The phenomenon exhibits responsive, directed, or purposeful behavior not explained by known physical processes — approaching, retreating, communicating, or interacting in ways that suggest agency.
  • Traversal of solid matter: Multiple entity types in this category are reported passing through walls, floors, and other solid objects without apparent interaction — a property consistent with very low mass or non-material existence.
  • Electromagnetic signature: Many phenomena in this category produce measurable electromagnetic effects: temperature changes, compass deflections, electrical interference, and photographic anomalies. This distinguishes them from purely subjective experiences.
  • Cross-cultural universality: Every sub-type in this category appears across multiple unconnected cultures and historical periods, often with consistent descriptive features that argue against purely cultural origin.

Documented Sub-Types

🌱 Energyzoa

Self-organising energy-life forms — hypothetical organisms whose structural and reproductive basis is electromagnetic or plasma field organisation rather than biochemistry. The most speculative sub-type; grounded in documented anomalous atmospheric phenomena and theoretical plasma biology.

✨ Light Beings

Luminous humanoid or formless entities reported across NDE, OBE, DMT, and contact experiences. Distinguished from orbs by their apparently humanoid or quasi-humanoid character and the consistent report of benevolent, communicative intent.

⚡ Plasma Entities

Structured plasma phenomena — ball lightning, Hessdalen lights, Marfa lights, and atmospheric electrical manifestations — that exhibit responsiveness, apparent intelligence, and behaviours inconsistent with known plasma physics. The most physically documented sub-type.

🔮 Orbs

Autonomous spherical luminous objects ranging from centimetres to metres in diameter, exhibiting purposeful movement, colour change, and in many accounts apparent responsiveness to human attention. Among the most photographic-evidence-rich categories in anomalous phenomena research.

🌑 Shadow Beings

Three-dimensional dark silhouettes occupying physical space independently of ambient light — including the widely-reported Hat Man figure. Cross-cultural, cross-temporal, reported by children before cultural exposure, and associated with measurable physical effects in documented cases.

🌌 Astral Entities

Beings encountered in out-of-body, near-death, and deep meditative states — the intelligences of the non-physical or subtly-physical planes described across spiritual traditions and increasingly investigated through OBE research and the Monroe Institute's protocols.

🌀 Interdimensional Intelligences

Entities proposed to originate from or primarily inhabit dimensions of reality beyond three-dimensional space-time — as developed by Vallée, Keel, and others. Phenomenologically distinguished by their apparent ability to appear and disappear from our reality at will and their apparent purposeful management of perception.

Theoretical Frameworks

Several theoretical positions attempt to account for the non-corporeal entity category within scientific or scientific-adjacent frameworks:

  • Plasma life hypothesis: Proposed by physicists including Jere Henry Levin and later explored by researchers at the Hessdalen project — that self-organising plasma structures could in principle sustain information processing analogous to biological nervous systems under certain energy-density conditions.
  • Field consciousness models: Frameworks (including Sheldrake's morphic fields, Persinger's geomagnetic consciousness proposals, and aspects of integrated information theory) that locate consciousness in field structures rather than discrete material substrates — opening theoretical space for consciousness-based entities without bodies.
  • Higher-dimensional physics: Kaluza-Klein extra dimensions, Calabi-Yau manifolds in string theory, and Everettian many-worlds all provide geometric frameworks within which entities could exist that interact with our 3+1 dimensional space-time selectively — appearing and disappearing without violating local conservation laws from the perspective of a higher-dimensional observer.
  • Quantum consciousness: Penrose-Hameroff orchestrated objective reduction (Orch-OR) and related theories propose that consciousness is a quantum mechanical phenomenon, implying that consciousness-based entities could in principle operate through non-local quantum channels inaccessible to classical physics.

This section presents documented phenomena and theoretical frameworks without endorsement of any specific hypothesis. All entity descriptions represent reported encounters and researcher categorisation rather than confirmed physical data. No ontological conclusions are made herein.
