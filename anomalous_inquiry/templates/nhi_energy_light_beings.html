✨ Light Beings

Light Beings — luminous, apparently intelligent entities encountered primarily in near-death, out-of-body, deep meditative, and psychedelic states but also in a significant body of waking contact reports — represent one of the most universally described and cross-culturally consistent categories in the non-corporeal entity literature. Distinguished from orbs by their apparently humanoid silhouette or clearly communicative character, and from plasma phenomena by their consistent association with profound affective states (overwhelming love, timelessness, complete understanding), Light Beings occupy a central position in the intersection of the NDE research literature, mystical experience taxonomy, and the contact phenomenon.

Physical Description Across Reported Encounters

Light Being descriptions show a remarkable degree of consistency across independent reports from individuals with no knowledge of the relevant literature:

  • Luminosity: The defining feature — light that appears to originate from within the entity rather than being reflected. The light is frequently described as brighter than any ordinary light source yet not blinding or uncomfortable; several NDE researchers (Pim van Lommel, Kenneth Ring) note that subjects consistently describe this light as having an "impossible" quality — intensely bright yet easy to look at directly.
  • Form variability: Light Beings range from formless radiant presences (most common in NDE accounts) to clearly humanoid figures of light (more common in contact and OBE reports), to what several experiencers describe as "geometric light structures" — complex patterns of light that communicate intelligence without a recognisable form. Some accounts describe a figure that resolves into and out of humanoid form as the experiencer's awareness shifts.
  • Colour: White or golden light is most commonly reported, followed by blue-white. Green and violet light beings appear in a minority of accounts, sometimes described as associated with healing or with specific communicative intent. The colour is frequently described as unlike any observable spectrum colour — "a colour that doesn't exist here."
  • Scale: Extremely variable — from human-sized to vastly larger than any physical object (some NDE accounts describe light presences that seem to encompass the entire perceptual environment). The scale variation may reflect the non-physical context in which they are encountered rather than an intrinsic property.
  • Communication: Almost never described as verbal. Communication is reported as instantaneous, total, and multi-dimensional — conveying meaning, emotional content, history, and ontological understanding simultaneously, in what experiencers universally describe as exceeding anything achievable through language. This feature is so consistent across NDE, OBE, and contact reports that it constitutes a defining marker of the Light Being category.

Light Beings in Near-Death Experience Research

The NDE literature contains the largest systematically collected body of Light Being reports. Key research findings:

Dr. Raymond Moody's foundational Life After Life (1975) introduced the "Being of Light" as one of the core consistent elements of the near-death experience — a luminous presence of overwhelming benevolence that many subjects interpret as divine but that is described in structurally identical terms across religious and non-religious subjects, suggesting the experience is not purely culturally mediated.

Dr. Kenneth Ring (University of Connecticut) conducted systematic research — Life at Death (1980) and Heading Toward Omega (1984) — quantifying the consistency of Light Being encounters across independent NDE subjects. Ring found that approximately 60% of NDEs with deep stages included encounter with a Light Being or luminous presence, and that the phenomenological character was consistent regardless of religious background, cultural origin, or whether the NDE was triggered by cardiac arrest, accident, or intentional near-death situations.

Dr. Pim van Lommel, Dutch cardiologist, published a prospective study of 344 cardiac arrest patients in The Lancet (2001) — the largest controlled NDE study to date. Van Lommel's data confirmed the frequency and consistency of luminous presence encounters and noted that the "impossible" phenomenal properties — perception during verified periods of flat EEG, later verified out-of-body observations, and entity encounters — constituted a serious challenge to purely neurological explanations of the experience.

Light Beings in Out-of-Body Research

Robert Monroe's OBE research produced systematic accounts of Light Being-type entities in non-physical environments. Monroe's taxonomy distinguished between human consciousness patterns encountered in various post-mortem or non-physical states and what he called "Inspecs" (Intelligent Species) — entities of apparently non-human origin encountered in the deepest OBE states, typically manifesting as intensely luminous presences. Monroe described Inspecs as operating at a level of awareness so much greater than human consciousness that direct communication was difficult — requiring them to "step down" their presence to communicate in ways Monroe could process.

The Monroe Institute's ongoing research programme has collected thousands of structured OBE exploration reports from trained practitioners. Light Beings of various types appear consistently in deep Focus states, particularly Focus 27 (Monroe's designation for what he described as a "reception centre" at the boundary of non-physical reality) and beyond. The research framework is explicitly phenomenological rather than ontological: the Institute documents what is encountered without taking a position on its ultimate nature.

William Buhlman, an OBE researcher and Monroe Institute trainer, has collected thousands of OBE reports over three decades and notes that advanced non-physical states consistently involve luminous beings. His analysis, published in Adventures Beyond the Body (1996) and The Secret of the Soul (2001), categorises several sub-types of Light Beings based on behavioural and communicative character — ranging from what appear to be recently deceased humans maintaining a light-body form to beings with no recognisable human history.

Light Beings in Psychedelic and DMT Research

Dr. Rick Strassman's DMT research at the University of New Mexico (1990–1995), documented in DMT: The Spirit Molecule (2001), produced a significant body of entity encounter reports from controlled DMT administration to volunteer subjects. A recurring entity type in Strassman's data is described in terms consistent with Light Beings — luminous, non-material, communicating in the characteristic simultaneous-total manner. These encounters were produced by endogenous neurotransmitter administration in a controlled clinical setting, distinguishing them from more culturally mediated spiritual experiences.

The consistency of the "beings of light" description across Strassman's subjects — who were not told what other subjects had experienced — is particularly relevant. Strassman himself remained agnostic about the ultimate nature of the entities encountered, noting only that the experiences had the "objective" quality of encounters with genuinely external intelligences rather than the subjective quality of dream states.

Research by Andrew Gallimore (neurobiologist) and David Luke (University of Greenwich), exploring the neurological basis and entity phenomenology of DMT states, has noted that light-form entities in DMT are frequently described as conveying specific, verifiable-feeling information rather than the generic emotional content of dream encounters — a property that, if accurate, would distinguish them from neurologically generated hallucinations.

Cross-Cultural and Historical Context

Light Being encounters are documented in virtually every spiritual and religious tradition:

  • Abrahamic traditions: Angels — the primary Light Being category in Jewish, Christian, and Islamic tradition — are described as luminous, overwhelming in presence, and communicating in ways that transcend ordinary language. The Hebrew word for "angel" (malach) means messenger; the Greek "angelos" is the direct etymological source. Biblical angel encounters almost universally begin with the command "fear not" — suggesting an overwhelming initial impact on witnesses consistent with NDE Light Being accounts.
  • Hindu tradition: Devas (literally "shining ones") and the concept of the jyotir (divine light) body are foundational elements of Vedic cosmology. Advanced yogic and meditational traditions describe systematic encounters with luminous beings as progressive stages of spiritual practice — structured phenomenological maps of Light Being encounter that predate modern research by millennia.
  • Tibetan Buddhism: The Bardo Thodol (Tibetan Book of the Dead) provides one of the most detailed pre-modern maps of post-mortem Light Being encounter, including the distinction between the "clear light" of pure awareness and the specific form-possessing luminous deities of the Bardo state. The structural parallels with NDE phenomenology were noted by NDE researchers from the 1970s onward.
  • Indigenous visionary traditions: Luminous beings feature prominently in shamanic journeying traditions across Siberia, the Americas, and Africa — most often described as spirit helpers, ancestors, or non-human intelligences encountered in visionary states induced through drumming, plant medicines, or fasting. The consistency of luminous being descriptions across geographically isolated traditions strongly argues against purely culturally-derived origin.

This page presents Light Being phenomena through the lens of NDE research, OBE investigation, psychedelic research, and cross-cultural spiritual tradition without endorsement of any specific interpretive framework. No claims about the ultimate nature or origin of Light Beings are made by this archive.
