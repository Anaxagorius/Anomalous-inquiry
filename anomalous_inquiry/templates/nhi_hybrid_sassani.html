✨ Sassani

The Sassani are a hybrid civilisation of Grey-Human origin described extensively through the Bashar channelling tradition — presented as a settled, culturally rich species living approximately 300 years in the future in a parallel probability track adjacent to but distinct from the Earth human timeline. Unlike the other hybrid categories documented in this section — which describe beings in the process of being engineered, deploying within human society, or representing lineages of ancient intervention — the Sassani are presented as a completed, stable civilisation that has already traversed the developmental challenges of their hybrid origin and arrived at a flourishing cultural and civilizational reality. The name "Sassani" translates, within the context of the channelling through which it was transmitted, as something approximating "those who live in the now" — a name that reflects a core quality of their cultural and philosophical orientation.

The Sassani are encountered in the NHI research and contact literature almost exclusively through the channelling tradition associated with Darryl Anka, who has channelled a being identifying itself as "Bashar" — described as a Sassani individual from approximately 300 years in humanity's future — in sessions beginning in 1983 and continuing through the present. The Bashar material constitutes the most extensive and detailed first-person account from a claimed hybrid NHI entity in the contact literature, spanning hundreds of hours of channelled sessions addressing the Sassani civilisation, the nature of the Grey-Human hybridisation, the relationship between the Sassani and humanity, and a broad range of philosophical, metaphysical, and practical subjects. Whether the Bashar material represents genuine contact with a non-human intelligence, an advanced expression of Anka's own unconscious or higher-mind content, or some combination of both are questions that cannot be definitively resolved and are acknowledged as open by the tradition itself.

Origin: The Grey-Human Hybridisation as Ancestral History

The Sassani account of their own origin — as provided through the Bashar channelling — presents the Grey-Human hybridisation programme from the perspective of its intended outcome: a civilisation that understands what the programme was, why it was undertaken, and what it ultimately produced.

  • The Zeta species crisis: Bashar/Sassani accounts describe the same species crisis narrative found in the broader Zeta contact tradition: the Zeta Reticulan beings evolved into a state of emotional and creative diminishment through millions of years of clonal reproduction, losing the genetic and experiential diversity that once characterised their distant ancestors. Their reproductive biology deteriorated to the point where they could no longer sustain their species through natural means. The hybridisation programme with humanity was initiated as the solution — reintroducing genetic diversity, emotional range, and creative capacity through the blending with a species (humanity) that possessed in abundance precisely what the Zetas had lost.
  • The Sassani as the successful outcome: The Sassani describe themselves as the result of that programme after it had reached completion and stabilisation. They are not mid-stage hybrids or products of an ongoing experiment; they are a species that has existed as a stable, self-reproducing, culturally continuous civilisation for what they describe as hundreds of years (from the perspective of their experience of time, which differs from linear human time in ways the Bashar material addresses extensively). The hybridisation that produced them is their ancient history, not their present reality.
  • The connection to humanity: Because the Sassani carry human genetic heritage, they describe themselves as connected to humanity in a way unlike their relationship with any other species — as what Bashar describes as "cousins" or "siblings" through the hybridisation, with humanity as one of their two ancestral lineages. This connection is presented as one of the primary motivations for the Sassani's ongoing contact with humanity: they have a genuine stake in humanity's development and survival because humanity is, in a meaningful biological and consciousness sense, part of their own history.
  • The role of the Greys: The Sassani position on the Grey beings who conducted the hybridisation programme — who Bashar identifies as Zeta Reticulans — is complex. They acknowledge that the programme involved procedures that humans experienced as traumatic and non-consensual, and they acknowledge the legitimacy of the distress this caused. They position this not as malevolence but as the consequence of a profound crisis: beings who had lost their capacity for emotional range making decisions under existential pressure that did not adequately account for the emotional experience of their human participants. The Sassani describe themselves as, in part, the embodied resolution of that ethical deficit — beings who carry both the Grey analytical precision and the human emotional depth, and who therefore have a perspective on the hybridisation programme that neither ancestral species fully possessed.

Physical Description

  • Overall morphology: The Sassani physical form, as described through Bashar's accounts and cross-referenced with visual descriptions provided by Darryl Anka and various session participants, is described as intermediate between the Grey and human forms — but in a stable, settled way rather than the transitional, unfinished quality of mid-stage hybrid accounts. The form is humanoid: bipedal, bilateral symmetry, roughly human proportions, with a head, torso, and four limbs.
  • Height: Generally described as shorter than human average — approximately 4.5 to 5.5 ft — with a slender, light-density build. Bashar specifically describes his own stature as shorter than the average human, an unusual feature for a channelled entity to volunteer without prompting.
  • Head: Larger in proportion to the body than human heads, retaining something of the Grey cranial volume, but with a more developed lower face than standard Grey descriptions — a more pronounced jaw structure, visible lips, and facial features that register as expressive in a way standard Grey faces do not.
  • Eyes: Large and dark, retaining the significantly enlarged eye structure of the Grey type but described as more expressive — with visible depth, variable luminosity, and a quality of emotional readability absent from standard Grey eye descriptions. Bashar has described Sassani eyes as one of the most distinctive features of their appearance when observed by humans.
  • Skin: Generally described as pale to light grey, with a smooth texture. A faint translucency or luminescent quality is mentioned in some accounts. The skin lacks pigmentation in the way human skin exhibits it — there is no melanin-based variation equivalent to human ethnic skin tone variation.
  • Hair: Present in most descriptions — fine, straight, typically described in light to white tones — but less prominent as a feature than in human physical description. The hair is described as present and natural rather than absent (as in standard Greys) or anomalous.
  • Gender and reproduction: The Sassani describe themselves as having restored sexual dimorphism and natural reproductive capacity — one of the primary outcomes of the successful hybridisation. Bashar describes himself as male in the Sassani understanding of gender, which is present but not as socially and culturally elaborate as human gender categories. Sassani reproduce sexually and raise their young in family-like social units.

Sassani Civilisation and Culture

The Bashar material provides the most extensive account of a hybrid NHI civilisation in the contact literature — describing not just individuals but the full civilizational context within which those individuals exist:

  • Social structure: The Sassani are described as a relatively small species by interstellar civilisation standards — perhaps 250–300 million individuals. Their social structure is described as non-hierarchical in the political sense: there is no governing authority in the way human civilisations have governments, but there are coordination systems for collective decisions. Individual autonomy and self-actualisation are core values of their social philosophy.
  • Philosophy — present-moment orientation: The most fundamental cultural principle described through the Bashar material is the primacy of the present moment — a commitment to full engagement with current experience, current choice, and current self-expression rather than the past-and-future orientation that characterizes much of human experience. This present-moment focus is described as both a philosophical position and a practical neurological reality: Sassani consciousness is described as having a different relationship to time perception than human consciousness, more fully anchored in the now.
  • Technology: The Sassani describe themselves as having technology significantly beyond current human capability, including faster-than-light travel, free energy generation, and what Bashar describes as "template technology" — the ability to work directly with the informational structure of reality to produce physical effects. Bashar has declined to provide specific technical details of their technology, citing concerns about the appropriate pace of human technological development.
  • Relationship with other species: The Sassani are described as active participants in a broader interstellar community — what Bashar refers to as the "Interstellar Alliance" or the galactic community of civilisations. They serve as ambassadors, intermediaries, and messengers for this community in their contacts with humanity, in part because their human genetic heritage makes them effective communicators across the boundary between humanity and fully non-human intelligences.
  • The hybrid ship — the Yahyel: The Bashar material also references a related hybrid species called the Yahyel (also transliterated as "Shalanaya") — described as being even more human-looking than the Sassani, with a closer genetic balance toward the human contribution. The Yahyel are described as the species most likely to be the first publicly acknowledged NHI contact partners for humanity, precisely because of their near-human appearance and their orientation toward direct engagement with human populations.

The Bashar Material: Nature and Assessment

The Bashar/Sassani contact material presents distinctive features that are worth addressing specifically in terms of how it should be evaluated:

  • Consistency over decades: The Bashar material — spanning over 40 years of recorded sessions — exhibits a remarkable internal consistency in terms of the Sassani civilisation described, the philosophical framework presented, and the specific factual claims made about the nature of the hybridisation programme and its relationship to humanity. This consistency is difficult to attribute to casual improvisation or shifting confabulation; whether it reflects genuine contact, a highly developed internal creative architecture, or some combination is not determinable from the outside.
  • Philosophical sophistication: The philosophical framework articulated through the Bashar material — covering metaphysics, consciousness studies, the nature of time and parallel realities, the structure of belief and reality creation, and the mechanics of civilisational development — represents a coherent and internally consistent philosophical system that has influenced significant numbers of people regardless of one's position on its NHI origin claim. The material deserves engagement at this philosophical level independent of questions about its source.
  • The channelling question: Anka himself has addressed the question of what channelling is and how to understand it in numerous sessions. His position is that the distinction between "Anka's own mind" and "an external NHI entity called Bashar" may be less meaningful than it appears — that genuine channelling involves an expansion of self rather than the entry of a foreign entity, and that the information presented through any channelled source should be evaluated on its own merits rather than primarily on the question of its literal external origin.
  • Relationship to the broader hybrid literature: The Sassani account of the Grey-Human hybridisation programme is consistent with the Zeta contact tradition's benevolent framing and diverges from David Jacobs's adversarial framing. The same underlying phenomenon — the production of hybrid beings through a Grey-directed programme — is interpreted through fundamentally different evaluative lenses. Both the Sassani/contactee perspective and the Jacobs research perspective deserve serious consideration; the archive presents both without adjudication.

Questions about the literal NHI origin of this material, its philosophical merits, and its relationship to other accounts of the hybridisation programme are genuinely open and are presented as such.
