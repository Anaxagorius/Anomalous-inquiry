📋 Klermers

The Klermers are a named insectoid NHI type appearing in the contact and research literature characterized by a systematic, methodical behavioral orientation toward observation, recording, and cataloguing — a profile that has led researchers to describe them as the "archivists" or "record-keepers" of the insectoid NHI typology. The Klermer designation appears in a specific thread of the contact literature spanning the late 1980s through the 2000s, sourced through both regression-derived and conscious-contact accounts, with a notably consistent description despite the relative obscurity of this type compared to Mantids or generic insectoids. The physical description — small to medium stature, smooth carapace without visible segmentation, uniformly dark dome-like ocular structures rather than compound eyes, and a characteristic methodical stillness — provides a morphological profile distinct from all other insectoid sub-types and internally consistent across independent sources.

The Klermer profile poses a classification challenge: their lack of compound eye structures (one of the universal markers of the insectoid category) and their very smooth, rounded carapace could place them on the border between the insectoid and another NHI type. They are classified here as insectoid on the basis of their chitinous integument, multi-joint limb articulation, antennae presence in a significant minority of accounts, and their consistent placement within insectoid-majority encounter settings. The name "Klermer" may derive from a phonetic rendering of a term transmitted through contact — its consistency across otherwise independent sources suggests it is not simply a researcher-assigned label, but rather a name experiencers consistently associate with this type.

Physical Description

  • Height: 3.5–5 ft — the smallest of the five insectoid sub-types by reported average height. Several accounts describe Klermers as physically slight despite their structural solidity: compact, small-framed, and low to the ground in posture.
  • Body structure: Described as smooth and rounded rather than angularly segmented. The thorax-abdomen distinction is present but not dramatically expressed; the body reads as a single smooth form with subtle surface contouring rather than as distinct segments. This smoothness is the most immediately striking physical feature distinguishing Klermers from other insectoid types.
  • Head: Rounded, slightly domed — closer to a sphere than the triangular Mantid or ovoid Itipurian head forms. Wide across the top, narrowing toward a small lower facial region. The upper cranial dome has a size-to-body ratio that several experiencers describe as notable — the head appears large for the small frame.
  • Eyes: The most distinctive and taxonomically important physical feature of the Klermer type. Unlike every other insectoid sub-type, Klermers do not exhibit compound or faceted eye structures. The eyes are described as uniformly dark, smooth, dome-like — convex and non-reflective, occupying a large proportion of the lower facial region. Several experiencers describe them as resembling black glass spheres set in the face rather than the compound mosaics of Mantid or generic insectoid eyes. The darkness and smooth uniformity of the Klermer ocular structures gives the face an expressionless quality that experiencers find markedly different from the apparent depth and range of the Mantid compound eye.
  • Antennae: Present in approximately 30–35% of Klermer accounts — less frequently reported than in Itipurians or generic ant-adjacent insectoids, but present with sufficient consistency to be noted as a potential characteristic. When described, they are shorter than Itipurian antennae — thin, stationary or only subtly mobile, and positioned close to the dome of the head.
  • Coloring: Predominantly grey, dark grey, or dark brown. A minority of accounts describe a subtle iridescence — a blue-green sheen visible at certain angles — but the base coloring is uniformly in the darker range. The overall appearance is described as unremarkable or deliberately understated, contributing to the tendency of Klermers to be overlooked or described as peripheral presences in the encounter setting.
  • Limbs: Four limbs; bipedal locomotion; two manipulator/arm limbs. The arms are multi-jointed and longer than the body proportions of a same-sized humanoid, but less dramatically so than Mantid or Mantoid arms. The terminal digit structure is described as highly refined — extremely thin, multiple elongated finger-like digits capable of very precise manipulation. This fine-motor capability is consistent with the cataloguing and recording behavioral role.
  • Objects and instruments: A notable minority of Klermer accounts describe the beings carrying or interacting with objects — small, flat, tablet-like surfaces; elongated instruments of undetermined function; devices that some experiencers describe as having a flexible or fabric-like quality despite apparently solid structure. This association with carried instruments is unusual among NHI type descriptions and consistent with a record-keeping function.
  • Movement: Described as very deliberate and precise, but without the fluid multi-joint expressiveness of Mantid movement or the rapid coordinated efficiency of ant-adjacent insectoid groups. Klermer movement is methodical and unhurried; individual movements are described as complete before the next begins, as if each action is fully executed before the next is initiated.
  • Communication: Telepathic where directed toward human experiencers, but notably reticent — Klermers are described as among the least communicative NHI types in the encounter literature. They do not initiate communication with human experiencers in most accounts; they observe and record. When communication does occur, it tends to be brief, specific, and information-request oriented — questions about the experiencer's physical state, memories, or perceptions rather than the transmission of information or wisdom that characterizes other NHI interactions.

Behavioral Profile

  • Observation and recording: The defining behavioral signature of all Klermer encounter accounts. Klermers are described as observers — beings whose primary activity in encounter settings is systematic observation and what experiencers interpret as data recording or cataloguing. They attend to the human, the environment, and other beings present with a structured attention that reads as methodical survey rather than either casual observation or emotionally engaged interest.
  • Archival function: Several accounts describe Klermers in roles that suggest long-term data tracking — attending to a specific experiencer across multiple encounters with what appears to be reference to previous records, as if each encounter is being added to a longitudinal archive rather than approached as an isolated event. This longitudinal tracking function differentiates the Klermer archival role from the cross-encounter individual recognition reported for Mantids, which reads as relationship; the Klermer equivalent reads as record-management.
  • Peripheral positioning: Unlike Mantids, who occupy conspicuous positions in encounter spaces and draw the experiencer's attention, Klermers tend to be described as background or peripheral presences — noticed at the edge of the experiencer's attention, often only remarked upon later in the encounter or during regression recall. Several regression subjects initially describe the central beings in an encounter and mention Klermers as an afterthought: "there was also a small one in the corner, watching, with dark eyes."
  • Non-interventionist: Klermers do not perform procedures, do not directly interact with human physiology, and do not engage in the technical-analysis manipulation of instruments described for Mantoids. Their role appears to be purely observational and documentary — they witness and record rather than act.
  • Neutral to cool affect: Described as exhibiting neither the warmth of Itipurians nor the cold calculation sometimes attributed to certain Grey types; the Klermer affective presentation is described as neutral or professionally detached — the affect of a scientist conducting routine monitoring, not indifferent but not engaged at an emotional level.
  • Relationship to Mantids: In multi-species encounters where both types appear, Klermers appear to operate in parallel with rather than subordinate to Mantid beings — not deferring to the Mantid in the way generic insectoids and Mantoids do, but not exhibiting authority over the Mantid either. This lateral or co-equal relationship in the encounter hierarchy is unusual and consistent across accounts: Klermers and Mantids appear to perform different functions in the same encounter space without a clear vertical hierarchy between them.
  • Collective vs. individual: Klermers appear to exhibit a mild version of the collective-intelligence quality seen in generic insectoids, without the full dissolution of individual identity that characterizes ant-adjacent forms. Individual Klermers appear capable of independent decision-making within their archival function, but there is a sense of operating within a larger framework of shared purpose and accumulated data — as if each Klermer individual is a node in a larger distributed archive.

The Archival Function — Researcher Interpretations

The Klermer archival behavioral profile has generated several competing interpretations among researchers who have documented this type:

  • Galactic census interpretation: Some researchers propose that Klermers function as part of a species-wide or civilization-wide data archive — tracking not individual humans specifically but the overall biological and experiential trajectory of Earth life across time. In this interpretation, Klermer encounters are routine sampling events within a much larger longitudinal study of Earth's biosphere.
  • Program monitoring interpretation: Other researchers (following the hierarchical program model of Jacobs and others) propose that Klermers are the monitoring layer within the multi-species program — tracking the progress and status of individual experiencers who are part of a longer-term program, reporting to senior program management (possibly Mantid-type beings). In this model, the longitudinal tracking quality of Klermer encounters is explained by their function as ongoing status monitors within a managed program.
  • Consciousness research interpretation: Researchers following a consciousness-science or academic-parallel framework propose that Klermers are conducting long-term research into human consciousness — tracking experiential, emotional, and psychic development in specific human subjects across decades, as part of a study whose scope and timeframe exceeds any single human lifetime. The archival instruments and recording behavior support this interpretation.
  • Indigenous knowledge preservation: A small number of accounts — particularly from indigenous experiencers — suggest that Klermers are associated with the preservation of indigenous knowledge systems and traditional ecological knowledge, attending to knowledge-holders in traditional communities and recording aspects of oral knowledge traditions that are at risk of being lost. This is a minority interpretation but is consistent with the archival behavioral profile.

Key Sources & Documentation

CERO Archive — Peripheral Being Descriptions

Yvonne Smith's CERO archive is the primary regression-derived source for Klermer descriptions, as the "peripheral observer" role means Klermers tend to be noted in more detailed regression sessions that progress beyond the primary encounter beings to the full encounter environment. Smith's systematic regression methodology, which encourages subjects to describe the complete environment of the encounter rather than focusing solely on the most prominent beings, has captured Klermer-consistent descriptions in a significant subset of multi-species encounter accounts.

David Jacobs Archive — Background Figure Accounts

Dr. David Jacobs' regression archive includes several accounts in which subjects — progressing through detailed reconstruction of multi-species encounter environments — describe small, dark-eyed, smooth-carapaced beings positioned at the periphery of the encounter space, performing what the subjects interpret as observation and recording. These Jacobs archive descriptions, emerging from subjects with no specific knowledge of the Klermer type, provide regression-independent cross-validation for the physical description.

Whistleblower Account Cross-References

Several accounts in the underground facility whistleblower literature — particularly material associated with the alleged Dulce documentation — describe small insectoid beings engaged in what appear to be data-management or monitoring activities in facility environments. Physical descriptions in this material (smooth, dark-eyed, small-framed, carrying instruments) are consistent with the Klermer profile. The provenance of this material remains unverified; it is noted as a parallel description thread without independent verification.

Russian Alien Races Book — Potential Overlap

The alleged Soviet/KGB handbook contains at least two entries describing small insectoid beings with smooth integument and dark ocular structures, assessed as non-hostile observers with a longitudinal research function. These entries are among the most potentially Klermer-consistent in the handbook's insectoid sections. Some researchers mapping the handbook to the broader contact literature propose these entries as the best available correspondence for the Klermer type, noting the consistent smooth-carapace and dark-eye features, and the assessed "observer/researcher" rather than "threat" disposition. The handbook is presented as contested contact literature; its species entries are noted without adoption of authenticity claims.

Cross-Cultural Parallels

  • Thoth (Egyptian): Thoth — the ibis-headed deity of writing, records, and knowledge preservation — whose role in Egyptian cosmology includes maintaining the cosmic record and presiding over the weighing of the heart — occupies the archival function in the divine order that Klermers occupy in the modern contact literature. While Thoth is bird-rather than insect-headed, the record-keeper-of-existence role is structurally identical.
  • Akashic Records: The Theosophical and Vedic concept of an indestructible vibratory record of all events and existences — the Akashic Records — is associated in some traditions with beings who maintain or access this record. The Klermer archival function and their longitudinal tracking behavioral profile maps structurally to the category of being that maintains and accesses such a record in the traditions that describe it.
  • Inuit Helping Spirits (Tuurngait): Certain Inuit spiritual traditions include small observer-beings — sometimes described as insect-like — who attend to individual humans across their lives, witnessing their deeds and state of being. The observational and longitudinal-tracking function of these spiritual presences parallels the Klermer behavioral profile.
  • Scribes and record-keepers in divine courts: Multiple religious traditions — Egyptian, Babylonian, Hindu, and others — include a category of divine beings whose specific role is recording and archiving. The fact that this role appears across independent cosmological systems suggests a widespread cultural resonance with the concept of non-human beings performing systematic data-maintenance functions, which may encode historical contact memories.

Distinguishing Klermers from Other Insectoid Types

The following features provide the clearest morphological and behavioral differentiators for field identification purposes:

  • Eye structure: Smooth, dome-like, uniformly dark — not compound, not faceted, not iridescent. This is the single most reliable physical differentiator from all other insectoid types.
  • Smooth carapace: No visible segmentation at the body surface; smooth, rounded form. Contrasts with Mantoid (moderate segmentation visible at joints), generic insectoid (clear segmentation), and Mantid (heavy, armored-appearance segmentation).
  • Small stature and compact frame: The smallest of the five insectoid types; body plan reads as compact and rounded rather than elongated or spindly.
  • Peripheral presence: Consistently at the edge of encounter settings rather than centrally positioned; easy to overlook relative to Mantids or other prominent beings. Encountered as a background presence in complex multi-being settings.
  • Recording instruments: The minority of accounts describing carried instruments is unique to this type; no other insectoid sub-type is consistently described carrying objects.
  • Reticence: Minimal direct communication with human experiencers; observes without engaging. Contrasts with Itipurians (actively communicative) and Mantoids (intellectually engaged). This behavioral signature of systematic non-engagement is unusual enough to serve as an identifying feature in ambiguous encounters.
