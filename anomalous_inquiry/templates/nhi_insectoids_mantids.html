🦗 Mantids / Mantis Beings

Mantid beings — tall, praying mantis-like entities with large faceted eyes, multi-jointed elongated limbs, and a bearing that experiencers consistently describe as projecting ancient, patient intelligence — constitute the most frequently documented and most thoroughly studied sub-type within the insectoid NHI category. Unlike the generic Insectoids, who tend to appear in procedural or collective contexts, Mantids are almost exclusively reported as individuals: named (by experiencers), recognized across multiple encounters, and exhibiting what researchers describe as clear personality continuity. The emotional dimension of Mantid encounters — experiencers commonly report unexpected calm, awe, and even affection directed toward specific Mantid beings over repeated contact — is among the most striking and consistent features of this sub-type, and places Mantids in a distinct experiential class from virtually all other NHI types.

Physical Description

  • Height: 6–7 ft in the majority of accounts; a minority describe specimens 7–8 ft or taller. The apparent height is amplified by the hunched resting posture common to all mantid arthropods — the centre of gravity sits lower and further back than in a humanoid frame, causing the upper body and head to project forward.
  • Body structure: Narrow torso with a distinctly non-mammalian waist configuration; the thorax and abdomen distinction is apparent in many accounts. No breasts, no navel, no external genitalia reported. The body taper from shoulders to hips is the inverse of the humanoid pattern.
  • Head: Distinctly triangular or elongated, tapering to a point at the top and widening toward the jaw or lower facial region. Proportionately large relative to the body. Set on a thin, highly flexible neck capable of rotation exceeding the human range.
  • Eyes: Large, compound or multifaceted structures occupying most of the front face and wrapping slightly toward the temples. Reported colors: greenish-gold, amber, deep brown, occasionally chartreuse. A subset of accounts describes faint internal luminosity — a low glow rather than active light emission. The compound eye structure is the most consistently reported single physical feature of the Mantid type across all research archives.
  • Antennae: Reported in approximately 40% of detailed Mantid descriptions — small, thin antenna structures at the crown of the head, sometimes described as actively moving during communication. Absent in the remaining accounts, suggesting either individual variation, developmental stage difference, or variability in observational conditions.
  • Arms: Exceptionally long, thin, folding at multiple joints (three visible joint points in most descriptions, compared to two in the human arm). The folded resting position strongly resembles the raptorial foreleg of Mantis religiosa. The terminal "hand" structures vary: some accounts describe elongated multi-digit hands analogous to stretched fingers, others describe a two- or three-digit claw arrangement.
  • Coloring: Predominantly brown, olive, or mottled green-grey. Many accounts note a quality of visual instability — the coloring appears to shift slightly with the light or viewing angle, suggesting either iridescence in the chitinous surface or active chromatophore-like function.
  • Integument: Consistently described as harder than mammalian skin — chitinous or shell-like rather than soft. Visible segmentation at the joints, neck, and abdominal region. No clothing reported in the majority of accounts; the body surface functions as its own protective envelope.
  • Odor: A minority of accounts — roughly 15–20% of detailed descriptions — note a distinctive, faintly sweet or dusty odor associated with Mantid presence, unlike any scent associated with Grey or Reptilian encounters.
  • Movement: Described as fluid but unmistakably non-human — particularly the rotational range of the head and the multi-point articulation of the limbs. Movement appears deliberate and economical; no accounts describe Mantids moving rapidly or erratically.
  • Communication: Almost exclusively telepathic. No accounts consistently attribute audible speech to Mantid beings. A small number of accounts describe a low, resonant hum or vibration that accompanies Mantid communication — perceived as much in the chest as the ears — but this is a minority feature.

Behavioral Profile

  • Supervisory positioning: The defining behavioral signature of Mantid encounters. In multi-species settings, Mantids stand at the periphery, observing procedures performed by Greys or other types without directly participating. Grey beings are consistently described as deferring to the Mantid — looking to it, awaiting some form of non-verbal authorization before proceeding, ceasing activity if the Mantid's posture changes.
  • Ancient quality: Virtually every experiencer who provides a substantive account of Mantid encounter uses language of antiquity — "old beyond anything I can conceive," "like standing in front of something that was alive before our planet formed," "a patience measured in geological time." This quality appears experientially distinct from mere age — it is described as palpable, as something that can be felt rather than merely inferred.
  • Individual identity across encounters: Uniquely among NHI types, Mantids are frequently reported as the same individual recognized across separate encounters, sometimes spanning years or decades. This recognition appears to flow in both directions: experiencers describe the Mantid being as recognizing them as well, with what reads as familiarity. This feature is documented in Dr. Jacobs' archive, Kathleen Marden's cases, and extensively in Simon Parkes' public testimony.
  • Emotional engagement: Mantids are consistently described as engaging experiencers on an emotional level — projecting what reads as care, interest, or concern — rather than the flat affect associated with Grey types. This emotional dimension is reported even by experiencers who were initially terrified of the encounter; the Mantid presence frequently has a calming effect described as imposed rather than merely contextual.
  • Wisdom transmission: A significant subset of Mantid encounters (particularly those documented by John Mack, Barbara Lamb, and the CERO archive) involve information transmission — the experiencer receiving what they describe as direct knowledge downloads, understanding of cosmological or existential concepts, or a sense of being shown something important about the nature of reality. This feature is absent from generic Insectoid encounters.
  • Non-aggressive: No published account in the research literature documents a Mantid being engaging in threatening, violent, or directly harmful behavior. This is a categorical distinction from Reptilian and Maitre types, and notable even relative to Greys, who are associated with distressing medical procedures.
  • Patience and stillness: Described as exhibiting absolute stillness — motionless for extended periods during observation — and an unhurried temporal orientation that several experiencers contrast explicitly with the apparent urgency of Grey beings during the same encounter.

Reported Sub-Types & Variants

Within the Mantid type, researchers have noted several variant forms that may represent sex-based dimorphism, developmental stages, different lineage groups, or entirely distinct species sharing a common morphological template:

  • Brown / olive Mantid: The most commonly reported variant; mottled brown-green coloration, typical height 6–7 ft, full compound eye structures. This is the standard form documented in the majority of regression-derived accounts.
  • Green / jade Mantid: A smaller proportion of accounts describe a distinctly green variant — brighter, more saturated coloration — sometimes associated with higher emotional intensity in the encounter. Some researchers suggest this may represent a different subspecies or a different state of the same being.
  • White / pale Mantid: A rare variant appearing in a small number of accounts, described as near-white or very pale grey in coloration, sometimes with different eye coloration (reported as pale blue or silvery). Simon Parkes' "Mantis mother" description appears to encompass elements of this variant.
  • Large/Senior Mantid: Several accounts in the Jacobs archive and CERO collection describe a larger specimen — 7.5–8 ft or more — present in encounters that also feature standard-height Mantids. This "senior" individual exhibits even greater stillness and appears to command deference from the other Mantids present, suggesting internal hierarchy within the Mantid type itself.

Key Cases & Investigator Testimony

Dr. David Jacobs — Hypnotherapy Archive (1986 onward)

Dr. David Jacobs (Temple University, retired) conducted hypnotic regression with several hundred abduction subjects over more than three decades. His case archive — the most systematically documented single-researcher collection in the field — contains numerous accounts of Mantid beings in supervisory roles during what Jacobs terms "procedures" performed by Grey beings. Jacobs' analysis, published in Secret Life (1992), The Threat (1998), and Walking Among Us (2015), positions the Mantid type as potentially representing the senior tier in a multi-species hierarchical structure, above both standard Greys and Grey-hybrid beings. Jacobs is careful to distinguish between his subjects' descriptions and any conclusions about ultimate intent or origin.

Simon Parkes — Extended Contact Testimony

Simon Parkes — former Labour Party councillor and town council member in Whitby, North Yorkshire, UK — has given extensive public testimony (through Project Camelot interviews from 2010, his own YouTube channel, and published autobiography) claiming lifelong contact with a specific female Mantid being he refers to as his "Mantis mother," in addition to interactions with Grey beings and other NHI types. Parkes describes an ongoing relational history spanning decades, with the Mantid being exhibiting the individual-recognition-across-time quality documented in the research archive. The credibility context of a sitting elected official willing to make these claims publicly — staking his political reputation — gives his testimony an unusual anchor point in the contact literature, regardless of ultimate interpretation.

Kathleen Marden — MUFON Research

Kathleen Marden (Betty Hill's niece, former MUFON Director of Abduction Research) has documented multiple Mantid-involved cases through her investigation and hypnotherapy work. Marden's cases include experiencers who describe building a relationship with a specific Mantid individual over years of repeated contact — the most striking emotional quality of the Mantid sub-type. Her published work, including Captured! (with Stanton Friedman, 2007) and Science Was Wrong (with Friedman, 2010), contextualizes Mantid cases within the broader abduction research literature.

Barbara Lamb — Hypnotherapy Archive (1991 onward)

Barbara Lamb, a certified hypnotherapist and crop circle researcher who has conducted over 2,000 regression sessions with ET experiencers since 1991, documents Mantid presence in approximately 15–20% of multi-species encounter cases in her archive. Her book Alien Experiences (with Nadine Lalich, 2008) includes detailed Mantid case narratives. Lamb has noted that the emotional and relational qualities of Mantid encounters form the most consistently striking aspect of this sub-type across her independently-derived subject base.

Yvonne Smith — CERO Archive

Yvonne Smith, certified hypnotherapist and founder of CERO (Close Encounter Research Organization), has assembled one of the largest single-researcher insectoid encounter collections. Smith's cases include detailed Mantid descriptions from subjects with no prior exposure to the NHI literature, providing some of the cleanest data points for assessing the independence of Mantid descriptions across experiencers.

Dr. John Mack — Harvard Medical School

Dr. John Mack (Professor of Psychiatry, Harvard Medical School, 1929–2004), whose two-volume work Abduction (1994) and Passport to the Cosmos (1999) documented 200+ abduction subject cases from culturally diverse backgrounds, encountered insectoid beings in a subset of his case material. Mack specifically noted the cross-cultural consistency of certain insect and mantis-adjacent forms in his subject accounts as among the features most resistant to explanation through shared cultural templates — his subjects' backgrounds ranging from Western college students to indigenous South American community members.

Mantids & the Hierarchical Program Model

The most significant theoretical implication of the Mantid type — specifically their persistent positioning as supervisory to Grey beings — is what it suggests about the organizational structure of the multi-species NHI presence documented in the abduction literature. Several independent models attempt to account for this:

  • Jacobs' hybrid program model: Jacobs proposes that the multi-species structure (Mantids over Greys, with Grey-hybrids at the base) reflects a long-running biological program oriented toward human genetic material, with Mantids functioning as its most senior overseers. The program's ultimate purpose, in Jacobs' analysis, is not benevolent from a human perspective.
  • Collier's Andromedan cosmology: Alex Collier's contact information (allegedly Andromedan-sourced) positions insectoid beings as among the most ancient species in the local galactic community, with lineages predating the emergence of humanoid-type civilizations by billions of years — consistent with the "ancient beyond measure" quality experiencers report.
  • Mack's participatory model: Mack avoided hierarchical-control interpretations, preferring to understand the insectoid-mantid presence as part of a co-evolutionary process between human consciousness and non-human intelligence. In his framework, the "ancient wisdom" quality is real but not sinister — it represents access to a perspective on existence unavailable to young, embodied consciousness.
  • Vallée's control system hypothesis: Jacques Vallée's broad framework — in which the UFO and NHI phenomenon functions as a control system for human consciousness and civilization development — can accommodate Mantid beings as elements of the system's senior management layer, without requiring literal extraterrestrial origin.

Cross-Cultural Parallels

  • Iktomi (Lakota Sioux): Spider/insect trickster-sage of vast intelligence and age; holds the web of existence and grants access to hidden knowledge. The ancient-wisdom-holder-who-is-not-human structural role closely maps to the Mantid profile.
  • Khepri (Egyptian): Scarab-headed deity of self-creation and dawn, depicted with a full insect head on a human or divine body. The combination of insect morphology with divine authority and cosmological function is structurally consistent with the Mantid encounter profile.
  • Hopi Blue Star Kachina: The Blue Star Kachina — associated in Hopi prophecy with a transformative future arrival from the sky — is depicted in certain Hopi artistic traditions with features that some researchers interpret as mantis-adjacent. The "arrival from above" and transformative-contact function parallel modern Mantid experiential reports.
  • Mesoamerican insect deities: Bee and wasp deities appear across multiple Mesoamerican cultures (Maya Ah-Muzen-Cab; Aztec Xochiquetzal associated with insects; Zapotec bee cult). The positioning of insect beings as cosmological agents rather than mere fauna reflects a traditional understanding broadly consistent with modern contact literature.
  • Tibetan Buddhist Hungry Ghost realm: Some Tibetan iconographic traditions depict beings of the transitional states with insect-adjacent features — multi-faceted eyes, elongated multi-joint limbs — that researchers occasionally compare to modern Mantid descriptions, though the theological context differs substantially.

Russian Alien Races Book

The alleged Soviet/KGB alien species handbook does not contain a specific entry for "Mantids" by that name. This absence is notable given the frequency of the type in Western abduction literature. Researchers cross-referencing the handbook's species catalogue with the broader contact literature have proposed two interpretations:

  • Taxonomic absorption: Several handbook entries describe ancient, hive-intelligence species with colonial cognitive structures — a profile overlapping the Mantid behavioral signature. These entries may represent the same beings the Western literature calls "Mantids" under a different classification or terminology.
  • Documentation gap: The handbook's Soviet-intelligence sourcing may have produced a different encounter emphasis from the American hypnotherapy-derived archive — Soviet encounter documentation may have captured insectoid beings in contexts where the Mantid-specific supervisory role was not apparent or was classified differently.

The Klermers and Itipurians, documented in the separate sub-pages of this section, appear to have partial overlap with specific handbook entries, and are discussed in those profiles.
