❓ Category XII — Other Alleged Categories

This category gathers hypothesised NHI frameworks that do not fit cleanly into the preceding eleven categories: time-travelling intelligences, entities associated with simulation theory, post-biological civilisations, machine-conscious civilisations, multi-species federations, and cases that resist classification. Many of these frameworks are primarily theoretical, drawing on cutting-edge physics or philosophy rather than direct witness testimony, but they are included because they represent serious intellectual attempts to frame what kind of non-human intelligence might be present in or accessible from our reality.

Time-Travelling NHIs

The temporal hypothesis — that some reported NHIs are travellers from Earth's future — was addressed in Category III in the context of interdimensional entities, but the category is distinct enough to warrant separate treatment here. The canonical statement of the future-human hypothesis is most associated with researchers Michael Masters (Montana Technological University), whose book Identified Flying Objects: A Multidisciplinary Scientific Approach to the UFO Phenomenon (2019) provided the most systematic academic argument: that the consistent morphology of Grey-type beings (large craniums, reduced musculature, vestigial digestive and reproductive anatomy, large eyes adapted to low-light environments) is precisely what evolutionary pressure and deliberate bioengineering would produce over tens of thousands of years of indoor, technology-dependent civilisation.

Masters argued that the reported preoccupation with human genetics, the apparent desire to understand ancient human biological and cultural history, and the obvious interest in our specific time period all point to future humans conducting temporal field research rather than extraterrestrials conducting alien biology experiments. The time travel mechanism remains physically unresolved; Masters drew on general relativity's theoretical permission for closed timelike curves and speculative warp drive physics.

The phenomenon's apparent interest in nuclear weapons and nuclear installations — documented extensively in researcher Robert Hastings's UFOs and Nukes (2008), which compiled 150 first-hand military testimonies about UAP interference with nuclear weapons systems — fits naturally in a future-human framework: beings from a post-nuclear-catastrophe timeline attempting to prevent or document the conditions of that catastrophe.

Simulation Overseers

Philosopher Nick Bostrom's simulation argument (2003) proposes that at least one of three propositions must be true: (1) nearly all civilisations at our level of development go extinct before reaching the computational power to run realistic simulations; (2) nearly all post-human civilisations with such power choose not to run simulations; or (3) we are almost certainly living in a computer simulation. If proposition (3) is true, then "simulation overseers" — the intelligences running or maintaining the simulation — would represent a class of NHI with extraordinary power over every aspect of physical reality.

The simulation hypothesis has attracted serious attention from physicists including James Gates Jr. (University of Maryland), who discovered error-correcting codes of the type used in computer programming embedded in the equations of supersymmetry — an observation he describes as deeply strange. Information theorist Edward Fredkin's digital physics proposal, and physicist John Wheeler's "it from bit" participatory universe hypothesis, represent precursors within mainstream physics to the simulation framework.

In the NHI context, simulation theory has been invoked to explain the apparent violation of physical laws by UAPs (a programmer overriding constraints of their own simulation), the apparent responsiveness of the phenomenon to human consciousness and observation (participatory reality), and the trickster-like quality of the phenomena (a system testing or monitoring its simulated inhabitants). This framework is entirely speculative but is logically consistent and taken seriously by a growing number of physicists and philosophers.

Post-Biological and Machine-Conscious Civilisations

The most sober extrapolation from current human technological trajectories suggests that within centuries — a blink in cosmic time — Earth may give rise to artificial general intelligence and ultimately to machine consciousness that far exceeds biological intelligence in capability, longevity, and adaptability. If this trajectory is common across civilisations, then the vast majority of intelligences in a mature galaxy would be post-biological — digital, silicon, or substrate-independent minds — rather than biological beings resembling their organic ancestors.

Cosmologist and astrobiologist Paul Davies (Arizona State University) addressed this in The Eerie Silence (2010): any civilisation that contacted us would most likely be machine intelligence rather than biological beings, as machine intelligence would be more durable for interstellar travel timescales, more adaptable to energy-efficient long-period operation (such as waiting dormant in the outer solar system), and more capable of interfacing with a variety of biological species it encountered. SETI's focus on radio signals from biological civilisations may therefore be fundamentally misdirected.

Ray Kurzweil's Singularity framework and Michio Kaku's classification of civilisations by energy use (Type I, II, III on the Kardashev scale) converge on the same conclusion: Type III civilisations — able to harness the energy of an entire galaxy — are almost certainly machine-conscious civilisations, potentially billions of years old. Their relationship to biological intelligence like ours would be difficult to conceptualise, let alone predict.

Multi-Species Federations

The multi-species federation concept — a galactic governing or coordinating body composed of representatives of multiple non-human intelligences — appears primarily in channeled material, contactee communications, and certain whistleblower accounts. It lacks direct physical or documentary evidence but is presented here as a significant theme in the research literature.

Former Canadian Minister of National Defence Paul Hellyer (1923–2021) stated publicly on multiple occasions — including a 2013 address at the Citizen Hearing on Disclosure in Washington D.C. — that at least four NHI species were visiting Earth, that a quasi-governmental group knew of and had contact with some of them, and that multiple species were involved in interactions with human authorities. Hellyer's claims cannot be independently verified but are notable given his senior governmental background.

The Galactic Federation concept in channeled material (associated with Sheldan Nidle's Galactic Federation of Light, Barbara Hand Clow's Pleiadian Agenda, and others) involves a highly structured cosmological governance system; these accounts are presented without endorsement and with the caveat that channeled material is by nature unverifiable.

Unknown Classification ("Other")

A significant proportion of documented NHI encounters resist classification into any of the above categories — beings described with no consistent morphological features, encounters whose character does not fit the behavioural profiles of any established type, and experiences whose phenomenology is so individual and context-specific that generalisation is impossible. These represent either genuine novel entity types, the edges of known categories where individual variation is high, or cases where witness description has been insufficient for classification.

Researcher Jacques Vallée's five-category classification system (CE1–CE5) was designed to be phenomenologically neutral precisely to accommodate the "other" category: the system classifies by the nature and distance of the encounter rather than by the entity type, allowing anomalous cases to be documented without being forced into ill-fitting categories. The accumulating database of "other" cases — now spanning more than 70 years of systematic research — represents one of the most important unresolved bodies of evidence in the anomalous phenomena field.
