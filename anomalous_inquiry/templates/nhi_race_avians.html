🦅 Avians / Bird-Like Beings

Avian or bird-like NHI — humanoid beings with feathered, winged, or distinctly avian morphological features — represent one of the oldest and most universally attested NHI archetypes in human history. From the falcon-headed gods of ancient Egypt to the feathered serpents of Mesoamerica, winged divine beings appear across every major civilization. In the modern contact literature, avian beings appear in contactee accounts, channeled material, and most prominently in the testimony of whistleblower Corey Goode, whose Blue Avian accounts have reached the widest contemporary audience.

Physical Description

Avian beings present significant morphological diversity across accounts, but share core avian features:

  • Height: 5–8 ft depending on sub-type; most accounts place them in the 6–7 ft range for the Blue Avian type, and larger for raptor/eagle types.
  • Head: Elongated mandible or beak structure (sometimes partial — a pronounced avian lower face with mouth that opens like a beak); prominent brow ridges; crest structures (feathered or bony) reported in a subset of accounts.
  • Eyes: Large, amber, deep blue, or completely solid in color (Blue Avians described with entirely blue eyes with no visible pupil or sclera). Described as deeply intelligent and penetrating.
  • Plumage: Blue Avians (per Corey Goode): intensely, almost iridescently blue feathers covering the entire body including face. Raptor types: mottled brown/gold. White Avians: pure white, appearing angelic.
  • Wings: Variable. Blue Avians described as having vestigial or folded wing structures that appear non-functional in most encounters. "Eagle/Raptor" types described with functional wings of 6–12 ft span. Body otherwise bipedal humanoid.
  • Hands: Humanoid four-fingered hands reported for the Blue Avian type; claw-like appendages for more avian/raptor sub-types.

Sub-Types

  • Blue Avians: Goode's primary contact type; deeply blue feathered, 6–8 ft, communicating through hand gestures and telepathy. Part of the "Sphere-Being Alliance" in Goode's testimony.
  • Raptor/Eagle types: Larger, predatory feature set, described in various contactee accounts as warrior/guardian class.
  • White Avians: Angelic in appearance, associated with healing and high-dimensional contact in channeled material.

Historical & Mythological Context

The avian NHI archetype is among the most richly documented in ancient iconography:

  • Horus (Egyptian): Falcon-headed son of Osiris and Isis; king of the sky, patron of the pharaoh. The Eye of Horus is among the most reproduced symbols in human history.
  • Thoth (Egyptian): Ibis-headed god of wisdom, writing, and the moon; recorded the weighing of the soul in the Hall of Two Truths. In some traditions depicted with a human body and ibis head, in others entirely human.
  • Ra (Egyptian): Falcon-headed solar deity; the supreme creator god of the Heliopolitan tradition.
  • Garuda (Hindu/Buddhist): Enormous divine eagle-being; vehicle (vahana) of Vishnu; enemy of the Nagas (serpent beings) — possibly encoding an ancient NHI interspecies conflict.
  • Quetzalcoatl (Mesoamerican): The Feathered Serpent — a fusion of avian and reptilian iconography unique in world mythology, appearing across Aztec, Toltec, and Maya civilizations.
  • Thunderbird (Native American Pacific Northwest): Enormous supernatural bird capable of generating thunder with its wings; a cultural anchor point for anomalous large avian reports in North America.
  • Birdman (Cahokia Mississippian culture): Warrior-shaman figures depicted with avian features at the Cahokia site (near modern St. Louis, Illinois) — the largest pre-Columbian city in North America.
  • Simurgh (Persian): An enormous, benevolent mythical bird of immense age, said to have witnessed the world's destruction three times; associated with wisdom, healing, and the tree of knowledge.
  • Ahura Mazda (Zoroastrian): The supreme deity of Zoroastrianism is depicted with a winged disc — a human figure emerging from a sun-disc with spread eagle wings, possibly representing an avian NHI in sacred garb.

Contemporary Reports

Corey Goode — Blue Avian Testimony (2015 — present)

Corey Goode, who claims service in a clandestine "Secret Space Program" from approximately 1987 to 2007, began public disclosure of his experiences in 2015 through the Gaia network's Cosmic Disclosure series (hosted by David Wilcock). Goode describes contact with Blue Avian beings as part of what he terms the "Sphere-Being Alliance" — a group of five highly advanced NHI civilizations that arrived in the solar system to oversee a period of energetic transition. The primary Blue Avian contact in Goode's account is a being named Ra Tear Eir, communicating primarily through hand gestures and a form of sign language rather than speech or conventional telepathy. Goode's testimony became the most widely discussed avian NHI account in the modern era; it generated both significant interest and significant skepticism, with researchers noting both compelling details and inconsistencies.

Lisette Larkins

Lisette Larkins, a Los Angeles-based author, describes voluntary contact with beings that include avian-featured entities in her books Talking to Extraterrestrials (2002) and Calling on Extraterrestrials (2003). Her accounts are characterized by positive, communicative interactions and a teaching relationship — consistent with the broader pattern of avian NHI encounters in the literature.

Mary Rodwell — Australian ACERN Cases

Mary Rodwell, founder of the Australian Close Encounter Resource Network (ACERN), has documented avian-type NHI in multiple experiencer accounts gathered through her extensive hypnotherapy practice and support work with Australian and international experiencers.

ECETI Ranch — James Gilliland

James Gilliland of the ECETI (Enlightened Contact with Extra-Terrestrial Intelligence) Ranch in Trout Lake, Washington, has documented avian being sightings as part of the broader NHI contact phenomena at the ranch, which has been observed and filmed by numerous independent visitors over multiple decades.

The Owl Connection

Researcher Mike Clelland has conducted perhaps the most systematic investigation of a peculiar recurring motif in abduction literature: the appearance of owls — real owls, owl imagery, and owl-themed synchronicities — in the lives of people who report contact experiences. Clelland's books The Messengers (2015) and Hidden Experience (2018) document hundreds of cases in which owls appear at pivotal contact-related moments.

Two interpretations dominate: (1) owls function as screen memories — the mind substituting an owl image for the actual (often more disturbing) Mantid or Grey face during the memory encoding phase of an encounter; or (2) owls themselves are in some manner connected to the contact phenomenon, appearing either as messengers, co-opted symbols, or actual manifestations of avian NHI in a form less alarming to the human nervous system. The large, forward-facing eyes and nocturnal silent flight of owls make them compelling screen memory candidates for both Grey and Mantid faces.

Russian Alien Races Book

The Russian Alien Races Book does not specifically document an avian species. Some researchers have suggested that certain "bird-god" adjacent descriptions in the handbook may map to avian categories, but no explicit avian entry has been identified in published analyses of the document. This absence is notable given the universality of avian deity iconography across ancient cultures — an absence that some researchers argue reflects the handbook's focus on modern-era contact species rather than ancient historical presences.
