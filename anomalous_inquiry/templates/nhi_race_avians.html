🦅 Avians / Bird-Like Beings

Avian or bird-like NHI — humanoid beings with feathered, winged, or distinctly avian morphological features — represent one of the oldest and most universally attested NHI archetypes in human history. From the falcon-headed gods of ancient Egypt to the feathered serpents of Mesoamerica, winged divine beings appear across every major civilization. In the modern contact literature, avian beings appear in contactee accounts, channeled material, and most prominently in the testimony of whistleblower Corey Goode, whose Blue Avian accounts have reached the widest contemporary audience.

Overview & Classification

Across the full body of contact testimony, historical iconography, and modern whistleblower accounts, avian NHI can be broadly grouped into three primary sub-types, each with a distinct morphological signature, behavioral profile, and cultural/textual heritage:

  • Blue Avians: Intensely blue-feathered, 6–8 ft humanoids communicating through hand gestures and telepathy. Most prominently documented by Corey Goode in his Secret Space Program testimony (2015–present). Associated with the "Sphere-Being Alliance." Possible connection to the "Ra" social memory complex of the Law of One channeling (1981–84).
  • Avian Humanoids (Raptor/Eagle/White types): A broader category of bipedal beings with pronounced avian features — beaks, talons, feathered bodies, sometimes functional wings — appearing across independent contactee accounts, abduction case files, and ancient visual cultures worldwide. Includes warrior/guardian "Raptor" beings, angelic "White Avian" types, and eagle-headed entities from multiple contact streams.
  • Garuda-Type Beings: The oldest and most textually documented avian archetype, anchored in Hindu and Buddhist sacred literature spanning more than 3,000 years. Garuda is described as an enormous divine eagle-being of supernatural power — vehicle of Vishnu, enemy of the Nagas. The Garuda archetype is the most detailed written description of an avian NHI candidate in any pre-modern tradition, with extensive anatomical and behavioral specification in primary sources.

Physical Description — General

Avian beings present significant morphological diversity across accounts, but share core avian features:

  • Height: 5–8 ft depending on sub-type; most accounts place them in the 6–7 ft range for the Blue Avian type, and larger for raptor/eagle types.
  • Head: Elongated mandible or beak structure (sometimes partial — a pronounced avian lower face with mouth that opens like a beak); prominent brow ridges; crest structures (feathered or bony) reported in a subset of accounts.
  • Eyes: Large, amber, deep blue, or completely solid in color (Blue Avians described with entirely blue eyes with no visible pupil or sclera). Described as deeply intelligent and penetrating.
  • Plumage: Blue Avians (per Corey Goode): intensely, almost iridescently blue feathers covering the entire body including face. Raptor types: mottled brown/gold. White Avians: pure white, appearing angelic.
  • Wings: Variable. Blue Avians described as having vestigial or folded wing structures that appear non-functional in most encounters. "Eagle/Raptor" types described with functional wings of 6–12 ft span. Body otherwise bipedal humanoid.
  • Hands: Humanoid four-fingered hands reported for the Blue Avian type; claw-like appendages for more avian/raptor sub-types.
  • Communication: Predominantly telepathic across all sub-types. Blue Avians additionally use hand gestures/sign language per Goode. Raptor types may vocalize. Garuda-type beings speak audibly in textual sources (Sanskrit, Pali).

Historical & Mythological Context

The avian NHI archetype is among the most richly documented in ancient iconography, with representations on every inhabited continent:

Egypt

  • Horus: Falcon-headed son of Osiris and Isis; king of the sky, patron of the pharaoh. The Eye of Horus is among the most reproduced symbols in human history. Depicted in both fully falcon and falcon-headed human form.
  • Thoth: Ibis-headed god of wisdom, writing, magic, and the moon; keeper of the Akashic records; recorded the weighing of the soul in the Hall of Two Truths. In some traditions depicted with a human body and ibis head, in others entirely human.
  • Ra / Ra-Horakhty: Falcon-headed solar deity; the supreme creator god of the Heliopolitan tradition. The solar barque traversing the underworld each night is captained by a being of avian and divine aspect.
  • Nekhbet: Vulture goddess of Upper Egypt; divine protector of the pharaoh, depicted as a white vulture or a woman wearing a vulture headdress. Among the oldest deities in the Egyptian pantheon.
  • Montu: Falcon-headed god of war; pre-dynastic origins; patron deity of Thebes before Amun. Associated with the solar disc and depicted in combat posture.
  • Sokar: Falcon deity of Memphis; god of the necropolis and guardian of the underworld's first region; merged in later periods with Ptah and Osiris.

South & Southeast Asia

  • Garuda (Hindu): Enormous divine eagle-being; vehicle (vahana) of Vishnu; born fully formed from the cosmic egg with blinding radiance; enemy of the Nagas (serpent beings) — possibly encoding an ancient NHI interspecies conflict. See the Garuda subpage for the full profile.
  • Garuda (Buddhist): One of the five classes of mythical beings in the Buddhist cosmological system; enormous, feeds on Naga serpents; appears in Jataka tales and Pali canon. The Garuda standard is used by Thailand, Indonesia, and other nations as a national emblem.
  • Jatayu & Sampati (Ramayana): Divine vulture-eagle brothers; Jatayu attempts to rescue Sita from Ravana and is mortally wounded; Sampati is described as the older, larger brother who lost his wings to the sun's heat. Presented as ancient, wise beings allied with Rama/Vishnu.
  • Kylin/Fenghuang (China): The Fenghuang (phoenix) is the supreme avian being of Chinese cosmology — associated with the south, fire, and virtue; appears as an omen of a virtuous ruler. Depicted with the neck of a snake, the back of a tortoise, tail of a fish, forehead of a crane, and beak of a cock.

Mesoamerica & North America

  • Quetzalcoatl (Aztec/Toltec/Maya): The Feathered Serpent — a fusion of avian and reptilian iconography unique in world mythology, appearing across Aztec, Toltec, and Maya civilizations. Quetzal feathers (iridescent green) represent the sky; the coatl (serpent) represents the earth — a being of both dimensions.
  • Thunderbird (Pacific Northwest): Enormous supernatural bird capable of generating thunder with its wings and lightning with its eyes; a cultural anchor point for anomalous large avian reports in North America. Appears across Ojibwe, Menominee, Lakota, and Pacific Northwest traditions.
  • Birdman (Cahokia Mississippian): Warrior-shaman figures depicted with avian features at the Cahokia site (near modern St. Louis, Illinois) — the largest pre-Columbian city in North America, ca. 1050–1350 CE. The Chunkey Bird motif shows a being with hawk wings and human body in active flight posture.
  • Huitzilopochtli (Aztec): Hummingbird god of the sun and war; the guiding spirit of the Aztec people during their long migration to Tenochtitlan. Born fully armed, killing his siblings at birth — an echo of Garuda's instantaneous adult emergence from the egg.

Middle East & Central Asia

  • Simurgh (Persian/Zoroastrian): An enormous, benevolent mythical bird of immense age, said to have witnessed the world's destruction three times; associated with wisdom, healing, and the tree of knowledge. In the Shahnameh, Simurgh raises the hero Zal and later aids Rustam. The Sufi Farid ud-Din Attar's Conference of the Birds positions the Simurgh as the ultimate divine reality.
  • Ahura Mazda (Zoroastrian): The supreme deity of Zoroastrianism is depicted with a winged disc — a human figure emerging from a sun-disc with spread eagle wings, possibly representing an avian NHI or avian-associated being in sacred garb. This iconographic motif is shared with the Egyptian winged sun disc and the Akkadian Ashur.
  • Anzu / Zu Bird (Mesopotamian): A lion-headed eagle of enormous size in Sumerian/Akkadian mythology; thief of the Tablet of Destinies from Enlil; associated with storms and chaos. Defeated by Ninurta/Marduk in epic battle. Among the earliest written descriptions of an avian supernatural being.
  • Anzû (Akkadian): The great bird Anzu appears in the Epic of Anzu (Old Babylonian period, ca. 1600 BCE) as a divine creature of storms and fire who steals sacred tablets of cosmic law. Its defeat by Ninurta is depicted on Assyrian palace reliefs.

Contemporary Reports

Corey Goode — Blue Avian Testimony (2015 — present)

Corey Goode, who claims service in a clandestine "Secret Space Program" from approximately 1987 to 2007, began public disclosure of his experiences in 2015 through the Gaia network's Cosmic Disclosure series (hosted by David Wilcock). Goode describes contact with Blue Avian beings as part of what he terms the "Sphere-Being Alliance" — a group of five highly advanced NHI civilizations that arrived in the solar system to oversee a period of energetic transition. The primary Blue Avian contact in Goode's account is a being named Ra Tear Eir, communicating primarily through hand gestures and a form of sign language rather than speech or conventional telepathy. Goode's testimony became the most widely discussed avian NHI account in the modern era; it generated both significant interest and significant skepticism, with researchers noting both compelling details and inconsistencies. Lisette Larkins

Lisette Larkins, a Los Angeles-based author, describes voluntary contact with beings that include avian-featured entities in her books Talking to Extraterrestrials (2002) and Calling on Extraterrestrials (2003). Her accounts are characterized by positive, communicative interactions and a teaching relationship — consistent with the broader pattern of avian NHI encounters in the literature.

Mary Rodwell — Australian ACERN Cases

Mary Rodwell, founder of the Australian Close Encounter Resource Network (ACERN), has documented avian-type NHI in multiple experiencer accounts gathered through her extensive hypnotherapy practice and support work with Australian and international experiencers. Rodwell's case archive includes both Blue Avian-consistent descriptions and more general raptor-type avian accounts.

ECETI Ranch — James Gilliland

James Gilliland of the ECETI (Enlightened Contact with Extra-Terrestrial Intelligence) Ranch in Trout Lake, Washington, has documented avian being sightings as part of the broader NHI contact phenomena at the ranch, which has been observed and filmed by numerous independent visitors over multiple decades.

Alex Collier — Andromedan Testimony

Alex Collier, whose Andromedan contact claims stretch from the early 1990s, describes the avian-type as one of the oldest civilizations in the galactic community. In Collier's framing, avian beings predate most other NHI races and occupy a position of spiritual and structural seniority in the galactic community. They are described as existing in what Collier's contacts term "eighth density" or above — states of being outside ordinary spacetime.

Law of One — Ra Social Memory Complex

The Law of One material (1981–1984), channeled by Carla Rueckert with Don Elkins and Jim McCarty (L/L Research), presents a being or group of beings calling themselves "Ra, a social memory complex" who describe having previously visited ancient Egypt in physical form — teaching, building, and eventually departing when their teachings were distorted. Ra describes itself as sixth-density beings of light who now communicate only through "the narrow band" of telepathic channeling. The name "Ra" (an Egyptian falcon deity) and the group's Egyptian connection have led numerous researchers to identify the Ra social memory complex with the avian NHI archetype — particularly the Blue Avians as described by Corey Goode, who himself acknowledged familiarity with the Law of One material.

The Owl Connection

Researcher Mike Clelland has conducted perhaps the most systematic investigation of a peculiar recurring motif in abduction literature: the appearance of owls — real owls, owl imagery, and owl-themed synchronicities — in the lives of people who report contact experiences. Clelland's books The Messengers (2015) and Hidden Experience (2018) document hundreds of cases in which owls appear at pivotal contact-related moments.

Two interpretations dominate: (1) owls function as screen memories — the mind substituting an owl image for the actual (often more disturbing) Mantid or Grey face during the memory encoding phase of an encounter; or (2) owls themselves are in some manner connected to the contact phenomenon, appearing either as messengers, co-opted symbols, or actual manifestations of avian NHI in a form less alarming to the human nervous system. The large, forward-facing eyes and nocturnal silent flight of owls make them compelling screen memory candidates for both Grey and Mantid faces.

A third interpretation has been advanced by some researchers: that owls, particularly the barn owl's distinctly heart-shaped face, may serve as an intentional "calling card" left by avian NHI in nature — a signature embedded in the contact phenomenon's symbolic vocabulary, known to the beings involved as recognizable to humans across cultures (the owl's ancient associations with wisdom, death, and hidden knowledge make it a universally loaded symbol).

The Avian–Reptilian Polarity

One of the most striking cross-cultural patterns in avian NHI mythology is the consistent opposition between avian and reptilian beings. This polarity appears independently across multiple ancient traditions:

  • Garuda vs. Nagas (Hindu/Buddhist): Garuda is the sworn enemy and predator of the Nagas — cosmic serpent beings who appear across Hindu, Buddhist, Jain, and Southeast Asian traditions. This enmity is cosmological, not merely biological: Garuda and the Nagas represent opposing cosmic principles.
  • Horus vs. Set (Egyptian): Horus (falcon) battles Set (associated with chaos, storms, and the hippopotamus/unknown beast) across 80 years of mythological struggle for the kingship of Egypt. Some researchers interpret Set's association with "foreign" and "red" things as a possible Reptilian NHI encoding.
  • Eagle and Serpent (Mesoamerican): The founding vision of Tenochtitlan — an eagle devouring a serpent on a cactus — is the central symbol of Mexico. This same eagle-serpent opposition appears in Aztec, Olmec, and Maya iconography long before the Aztec migration.
  • Modern NHI testimony: Several whistleblower and contactee accounts describe ongoing tension or conflict between avian and reptilian NHI factions — with avian groups positioned as the more spiritually advanced and reptilian groups as oriented toward control and hierarchy. Corey Goode's testimony positions the Sphere-Being Alliance (including Blue Avians) as entities that have imposed restraint on more aggressive NHI groups, implicitly including reptilian factions.

Russian Alien Races Book

The Russian Alien Races Book does not specifically document an avian species by name. Some researchers have suggested that certain "bird-god" adjacent descriptions in the handbook may map to avian categories, but no explicit avian entry has been identified in published analyses of the document. This absence is notable given the universality of avian deity iconography across ancient cultures — an absence that some researchers argue reflects the handbook's focus on modern-era contact species rather than ancient historical presences. Others propose that the handbook's avian NHI may be classified under a broader category that does not correspond to Western contactee nomenclature.
