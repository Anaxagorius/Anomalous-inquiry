🦉 Avian Humanoids

Beyond the specific Blue Avian profile documented by Corey Goode, the broader category of Avian Humanoids encompasses a wide range of reported bipedal beings with pronounced avian morphology — beaked faces, feathered or scale-and-feather integuments, claw-like hands, and in many cases functional wings. This category includes the Raptor/Eagle types (larger, more predatory, warrior-class beings), White Avians (angelic in appearance, associated with healing), eagle-headed beings documented in various independent contactee streams, and a range of lesser-described avian entities from abduction case files and indigenous contact traditions. Together these represent one of the most consistent non-humanoid NHI categories across the global contact literature.

Raptor / Eagle-Type Avian Humanoids

The Raptor or Eagle-type avian humanoid is the most frequently described sub-category after the Blue Avians, appearing across independent contactee and abduction accounts with striking morphological consistency:

  • Height: 7–10 ft — taller on average than the Blue Avian type. The larger reported height is consistent across accounts from independent researchers with no apparent cross-contamination.
  • Build: Powerfully muscular — a broad-chested, physically imposing body plan unlike the more slender Blue Avian type. The physical presence is described as immediately commanding and potentially intimidating.
  • Plumage/Integument: Variable — mottled brown and gold (eagle-type), dark brown to near-black (raptor-type), or mixed grey-brown. Some accounts describe a mix of feathers and hardened scale-like patches, particularly on the arms, shoulders, and upper torso. The face may be less fully feathered than the body, with exposed skin around the beak base.
  • Face: Robust hooked or curved beak — unmistakably raptor (hawk, eagle, or falcon) rather than the straighter beak of the Blue Avian. Heavy brow ridges. Eyes: amber, gold, or copper-orange — described as piercing, with a predator's intensity of focus.
  • Wings: Functional, large — spans of 10–16 ft described in the most extreme accounts. Wings are held partially or fully folded against the body in most contact settings but can be extended for display or flight. Their presence is physically dramatic even folded.
  • Hands/Feet: Claw-like appendages with 3–4 digits, sharp and curved rather than the flat nails of the Blue Avian type. Some accounts describe taloned feet that are structurally more avian than human.
  • Communication: Mix of vocalization (described as clicks, low tones, or something analogous to a raptor call) and telepathy. Less sign-language-reliant than Blue Avians.
  • Role/Behavior: Consistently described as warrior, guardian, or enforcement class — not teaching or guidance-focused but protective and imposing. Some accounts describe them as policing less cooperative NHI groups in encounter settings.

Key Accounts — Raptor Type

  • Corey Goode — "Anshar" and inner-Earth council meetings: Goode describes large, eagle-like beings attending council-level meetings alongside other NHI races — present as security or witness, not as communicants. Their physical presence reportedly impressed even the SSP representatives who attended these councils.
  • Alex Collier — Andromedan accounts: Collier's Andromedan contacts described avian warrior-type beings among the oldest spacefaring civilizations in the galaxy, playing a role analogous to a galactic constabulary in certain systems.
  • Multiple MUFON case files: MUFON's investigative archive contains several obscure but consistent accounts of large raptor-type beings observed aboard craft or in outdoor encounter settings — typically described by traumatized experiencers who did not seek out avian NHI material before their encounter.
  • Barbara Lamb hypnotherapy sessions: Lamb has documented several experiencer accounts under hypnotic regression involving large eagle-type avian beings, typically in settings where multiple NHI types were present simultaneously.

White Avians

The White Avian sub-type is the least well-documented of the avian NHI categories, appearing predominantly in channeled and spiritually-oriented contact literature rather than in hard abduction case files or whistleblower testimony. Their profile is distinctly angelic in the Western sense:

  • Appearance: Pure white plumage, full-body feathering, often described as luminescent or self-illuminating. Height in the 6–8 ft range. Wings: large and described as genuinely functional but typically held folded. The overall appearance is consistently described as beautiful in a way that transcends aesthetic category — witnesses report emotional responses (tears, peace, awe) to the appearance itself.
  • Face: Less pronounced beak structure than the Raptor type — more refined, almost softened features. Eyes described as pure white, gold, or soft light-emitting.
  • Role: Healing, guidance, spiritual transmission. White Avians appear in accounts associated with near-death experience adjacent encounters, healing events, and high-affect altered states. They are not described in adversarial or procedural contexts.
  • Communication: Pure telepathy. No gesture language, no vocalization. The communication is described as arriving as fully formed knowing rather than language.
  • Cross-cultural parallel: The White Avian description maps closely onto the "angel" archetype across multiple traditions — Judeo-Christian seraphim (six-winged, face-obscuring), Islamic figures described in similar terms, and certain Hindu apsara or deva descriptions. The possibility that "angel" and "White Avian NHI" encode the same contact category is a recurring theme in the research literature.

Angels as Avian NHI

The standard Western depiction of angels — human face with large white feathered wings — is not the original biblical description. The Hebrew Bible's angelology describes some beings in terms far closer to avian NHI than to winged-human figures:

  • Seraphim (Isaiah 6:2–3): "Six-winged: with two he covered his face, with two he covered his feet, with two he flew." The seraphim surround the divine throne, calling out in a sound that shook the temple foundations. These are not gentle helpers but enormous, powerful, multi-winged beings of overwhelming presence.
  • Ophanim (Ezekiel 1): Ezekiel's chariot vision describes "living creatures" (later identified as cherubim) each with four faces — human, lion, ox, and eagle — and four wings. These are covered in eyes. The eagle face is explicitly present in the divine council's guardians.
  • Revelation 4:7: The four living creatures before the throne include "a flying eagle" — the avian representative in the divine council's inner circle alongside the lion (Raptor), the ox (Bovine/Pleiadian?), and the human face.
  • The Zohar's angelology: Kabbalistic tradition describes vast taxonomies of angelic beings, several of which are explicitly avian in character — governing specific celestial spheres and bearing message-transmission functions consistent with the avian NHI communication profile.

Eagle-Headed Beings in Global Contact Traditions

Independent of the Sphere-Being Alliance framework, eagle-headed avian beings appear across multiple contact traditions worldwide:

Native American Traditions

  • Thunderbird (Pacific Northwest, Plains, Great Lakes): A being of supernatural scale, generating thunder with wing-beats and lightning from eyes, Thunderbird is not merely a weather phenomenon — traditions describe it as conscious, communicative, and engaged with human affairs. The Ojibwe, Menominee, Potawatomi, and Lakota all maintain Thunderbird traditions with notably different details, suggesting independent reporting of an actual phenomenon rather than simple diffusion of a single myth.
  • Eagle beings (Zuni, Hopi): Multiple Pueblo traditions describe Sky Beings associated with eagles who descended to Earth in the deep past and left instructions that are still maintained as ceremonial obligations. The kachina system includes multiple eagle-type figures. Some Hopi elders have privately described sky-being contacts consistent with avian humanoids in ways that parallel modern contactee accounts.
  • Wakíŋyaŋ (Lakota): The Thunderbird of the Lakota is one of the most powerful wakan (sacred/mysterious) forces in Lakota cosmology — described as a being of immense size who nests on mountain peaks and maintains a relationship of both conflict and cooperation with underwater water spirits (Uŋktéhi), an echo of the avian-aquatic tension that appears in other traditions.

African Traditions

  • Impundulu (Zulu/Xhosa, South Africa): The "Lightning Bird" — a being of immense size capable of summoning lightning and storms, associated with witchcraft and spiritual power. Descriptions from traditional accounts sometimes describe a being with human and bird characteristics, not simply a large bird.
  • Benu / Bennu (Egyptian): The sacred phoenix-heron of Egyptian cosmology; the first being to stand on the Primordial Mound at the moment of creation; associated with Ra and Osiris and with the soul's journey through the afterlife. The Bennu may represent the avian NHI's role at the moment of cosmic origination in the Egyptian tradition.

East Asian Traditions

  • Karura (Japan, Buddhist): The Japanese equivalent of Garuda — a divine fire-breathing eagle-being of enormous size that feeds on serpents (Naga). Adopted into Japanese Buddhism and depicted in temple sculpture. The Karura appears in the Nihon Shoki as having a fierce appearance that terrifies all who see it but is benevolent to those under its protection.
  • Tengu (Japan): A class of supernatural beings in Japanese folklore ranging from purely bird-like (Kotengu) to bird-humanoid (Dai-tengu — human face, wings, long nose) to primarily human with residual avian features. Tengu are associated with mountainous wilderness, martial skill, and spiritual tests. Buddhist texts describe them as dangerous but capable of imparting wisdom to those who pass their trials.
  • Peng (China, Zhuangzi): The mythical enormous bird described in the Zhuangzi (369–286 BCE) — capable of flight of 90,000 li, carrying storm-clouds under its wings, ascending to the highest heaven. The Peng is used as a metaphor for the scale of Taoist liberation, but the specific physical description suggests a genuine archetype of an immense avian being.

Modern Abduction & Contact Literature

Abduction Case Files

Avian humanoids appear in a smaller but consistent percentage of abduction regression accounts gathered by major researchers. Unlike Greys (dominant), Nordics (common), or Mantids (recurring), avian beings appear in roughly 3–8% of cases documented by researchers who specifically catalog entity type. Their appearance is consistently associated with:

  • Settings involving multiple NHI species simultaneously
  • Experiences with a spiritual or initiatory quality rather than purely procedural
  • Heightened emotional memory — encounters with avian beings are disproportionately described as among the most emotionally significant in multi-encounter experiencers' lifetimes
  • Communication of information rather than physical procedures

Mary Rodwell — ACERN

Mary Rodwell, founder of the Australian Close Encounter Resource Network (ACERN), has documented avian-type NHI accounts across her hypnotherapy practice and direct interview work. Rodwell's case archive is notable for including avian beings described by experiencers with no familiarity with avian NHI literature — particularly significant given Australia's geographic and cultural isolation from the North American SSP disclosure community centered on Corey Goode's testimony. This independent convergence on avian morphology from experiencers who did not encounter avian NHI concepts through prior exposure is one of the stronger arguments for the category's objective reality in the contact phenomenon.

Barbara Lamb — Hypnotherapy Archive

Barbara Lamb, certified hypnotherapist and ET experiencer researcher with over 2,000 sessions since 1991, has documented avian beings in a subset of her cases. Lamb notes that avian beings — particularly large, powerful raptor-type entities — appear in her archive in contexts involving what might be called "spiritual upgrading" or "DNA calibration" events, as distinct from the standard medical/reproductive procedures associated with Grey encounters. The functional distinction between Mantid (supervisory/medical) and Avian (initiatory/spiritual) roles across independent accounts is one of the more intriguing patterns in the entity literature.

Lisette Larkins

Lisette Larkins (Los Angeles) describes voluntary, positive contact with beings including avian-featured entities in her books Talking to Extraterrestrials (2002) and Calling on Extraterrestrials (2003). Her avian encounters are characterized by teaching exchanges — the beings communicate philosophical or cosmological information — consistent with the avian NHI's broader wisdom-transmission profile across accounts.

ECETI Ranch — James Gilliland

James Gilliland of the ECETI Ranch in Trout Lake, Washington has documented avian being sightings at the ranch, a multi-decade contact site observed and filmed by independent visitors. Gilliland's avian being descriptions tend toward the more eagle-like rather than Blue Avian type and appear in conjunction with a range of other NHI observations at the site.

The Owl Connection — Avian Humanoid Dimension

Researcher Mike Clelland has documented the systematic appearance of owls — real owls, owl imagery, and owl synchronicities — in the lives of contact experiencers. From the Avian Humanoid perspective specifically, there are three interpretations worth maintaining:

  • Screen memory: Owls serve as the mind's substitution for avian humanoid faces during memory encoding — particularly the large, forward-facing eyes and generally non-threatening "wise" aspect of the owl maps onto the avian NHI face more naturally than onto a Grey's wrap-around dark eyes.
  • Proxy messenger: Some accounts suggest actual owls may be used as vehicles for avian NHI presence — appearing at significant moments as a form of communication or marking. Whether this involves literal control of owl behavior or something more symbolic/synchronistic is unresolved.
  • Morphological cousin: The barn owl, in particular, has a face structure (heart-shaped, forward-facing, with enormous black eyes) that resonates with multiple Grey-type NHI faces. The owl appearance as a substitute for an avian humanoid would require a different morphological substitution — closer in scale and bearing to the being it masks.
