🔭 EBENs — Exchange Program Beings (Project Serpo)

EBENs (Extraterrestrial Biological Entities, Not [earth-]Native) is the designation used in classified context for the beings involved in the alleged Project Serpo — a claimed secret United States government exchange program in which twelve US military personnel traveled to the EBEN home planet for an approximately 13-year stay (1965–1978), while one living EBEN survivor of the Roswell crash was returned to his home system. If accurate, Project Serpo represents the most consequential event in human history: confirmed interstellar contact and physical exchange between Earth humanity and an extraterrestrial civilization.

Physical Description

EBEN physical description from the Serpo disclosures and related testimony:

  • Height: Approximately 4–5 ft; slightly taller than Short Greys but shorter than typical adult humans.
  • Build: Thin limbs, somewhat broader torso than Short Greys.
  • Eyes: Large but with visible color — described as grey-brown irises, contrasting with the solid-black opaque eyes of the standard Grey type. This is one of the key distinguishing physical features noted in the Serpo disclosures.
  • Skin: Tan-brown coloring — described as warmer than the neutral grey of most Grey-type beings, potentially reflecting adaptation to their binary star system's solar radiation.
  • Mouth: Visible, functional in communication — the EBEN language is described as a clicking/tonal vocalization system; unlike Greys who communicate primarily or exclusively telepathically, EBENs have an audible language.
  • Internal biology: Team medical personnel noted significant differences from human anatomy — no identifiable lung structure; oxygen absorbed through skin and what may be a diffuse respiratory system. Cardiovascular equivalent present but different in structure.
  • Gender: No discernible gender differences visible to human observers.
  • Lifespan: Estimated at several hundred Earth years, possibly longer.

Project Serpo Summary

Origins (1947)

The alleged origins of Project Serpo lie in the Roswell Incident of July 1947. Per the Serpo disclosures, one or two EBENs survived the crash(es) near Roswell. Communication was established with the surviving EBEN through a device recovered from the craft. Over the following years, radio/signal contact was established with EBEN representatives. Negotiations ensued over what would become the exchange program.

The Exchange Agreement

Under what is described as an agreement formalized during the Eisenhower administration (variously dated to 1954 or later; potentially the "Greada Treaty" or a subsequent arrangement), the terms of the exchange were negotiated: twelve human military volunteers would travel to the EBEN home planet (designated Serpo) in exchange for technology transfer and continued communication. One living EBEN would be returned to Serpo. The agreement also specified protocols for ongoing communication and further contact.

The Mission (1965–1978)

  • Departure: July 1965; twelve specially selected military personnel (including doctors, linguists, and scientists) departed on an EBEN craft.
  • Journey time: approximately 9 months in Earth reference frame; EBEN navigational technology allowed travel at speeds approaching relativistic limits.
  • Serpo: a planet approximately 38.42 light-years from Earth (Zeta Reticuli system), described as slightly warmer and dryer than Earth, with two suns producing significantly higher UV radiation. The team required protective eyewear throughout their stay.
  • Duration: approximately 13 Earth years on Serpo.
  • Return: 1978; 8 team members returned (2 died during the mission; 2 remained on Serpo voluntarily).
  • Post-return: all returning team members died within years of return from what was assessed as radiation-related illness, possibly from extended binary-star UV exposure.

The Serpo Disclosures (2005–2006)

Beginning in November 2005, an anonymous source identified only as "Request Anonymous" — later identified by researchers as a high-level intelligence community insider — began releasing documents about Project Serpo to UFO researcher Victor Martinez, who distributed them through an email network of researchers. The documents included mission briefing materials, team member diaries (edited for security), EBEN biological descriptions, and Serpo civilizational data. Bill Ryan of Project Camelot and researcher Robert Collins investigated the disclosures and found significant internal consistency and what they described as credible classified terminology consistent with genuine intelligence documents. Richard Doty, a former Air Force Office of Special Investigations (AFOSI) agent, was identified by some researchers as involved in the disclosures — a connection that both lends and complicates credibility, given Doty's documented history of intelligence-community UFO disinformation operations (the Paul Bennewitz case).

EBEN Civilization on Serpo

Team diaries, as released through the Serpo disclosures, describe EBEN civilization on Serpo:

  • Non-hierarchical society: No apparent leadership structure — decisions described as emerging from community consensus without designated leaders or governing class.
  • Technology: Highly advanced but deployed with economy — technology applied only where necessary; EBEN civilization described as having reached a stable equilibrium rather than pursuing perpetual expansion.
  • No conflict: No evidence of war, crime, or violent conflict found in EBEN society during the team's stay.
  • Communication: The clicking/tonal EBEN language was partially decoded by the team's linguists; telepathic communication was also used, particularly for more complex concepts.
  • Religion/spirituality: Described as a form of deep reverence for the universe as a living entity — no formal religious institutions but a pervasive spiritual orientation to existence.
  • Longevity: Individual EBEN lifespan estimated at 350–400 Earth years.
