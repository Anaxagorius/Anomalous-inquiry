🛸 Egarot

The Egarot are documented in the alleged Soviet/KGB intelligence handbook on alien species — the Book of Alien Races (translated and published in English by Dante Santori and Petro Kichkaev) — as a humanoid species assessed as generally protective or neutral toward Earth. Unlike the more extensively documented species in the mainstream abduction and contactee literature, the Egarot are known almost exclusively through the handbook, making their profile uniquely dependent on that document's credibility.

Russian Alien Races Book Entry

The handbook documents the Egarot as one of several humanoid species with a known protective orientation toward Earth and human populations. Key elements of the handbook entry:

  • Assessment: Neutral to positive; possibly protective role toward humanity.
  • Earth interaction history: Known to have visited Earth across multiple periods; pattern of observation rather than intervention in most documented cases.
  • Concerns: Noted in the handbook as particularly attentive to environmental and nuclear risks facing Earth — consistent with the warning messages characteristic of protective NHI types in contactee literature.
  • Technology: Advanced spacecraft capable of interstellar travel; described as capable of atmospheric entry and departure without the energy signatures associated with some other NHI craft types.

The handbook's provenance remains contested. It is presented here as significant contact literature regardless of ultimate authenticity; the Egarot entry is documented as part of a complete picture of what the handbook claims.

Physical Description

From the Russian Alien Races Book entry:

  • Height: 2.0–2.5 metres (6.5–8.2 ft) — tall by humanoid standards; notably robust in build compared to many reported NHI humanoids.
  • Head: Large but human-proportioned, with pronounced brow ridges that give a slightly heavy-featured appearance.
  • Skin: Brown to olive-toned — one of the warmer skin tones among documented NHI types, which are frequently described as pale or grey.
  • Eyes: Large, described as amber or deep brown — similarly warm-toned to their skin coloring, which some researchers note may indicate a common star-system pigmentation adaptation.
  • Hands: Five fingers — uniquely matching human digit count among frequently reported NHI types (many other types are documented with three or four digits).
  • Build: Broader and more robustly muscled than the typical Grey or Nordic description — described as appearing physically powerful.
  • Hair: No hair, or very fine hair in some accounts.
  • Clothing: Wear form-fitted dark suits in encounter contexts — a utilitarian rather than ceremonial garment style.

Behavioral Profile

Based on the handbook entry and associated contact reports:

  • Protective orientation: The Egarot are described as having a long-standing interest in Earth's wellbeing — particularly regarding environmental destruction and humanity's capacity for nuclear self-annihilation. This places them in the category of "guardian" NHI types alongside certain Tau Cetian and Nordic accounts.
  • Non-interventionist by default: Despite their protective orientation, the Egarot appear to observe more than they intervene — consistent with a policy of non-interference except in circumstances that would constitute an existential risk.
  • Communication style: Accounts associated with Egarot-type beings describe direct, matter-of-fact communication — clear warnings or information delivered without the elaborate theatrical staging of some contactee encounters.
  • Physical presence: Their robust build and direct communication style give Egarot encounters a less unsettling quality than Grey or Maitre encounters — despite their greater physical size, experiencers in associated accounts less frequently describe terror.

Possible Case Connections

South American Contact Cases (1970s–1990s)

Several South American contact cases from this period describe humanoid beings of larger-than-human stature with brown or olive-toned skin who delivered warning or ecological messages — a description that aligns with the Egarot profile in the handbook. The high concentration of contact cases in South America (Brazil, Peru, Colombia, Venezuela) involving this physical type has led some researchers to suggest the Egarot may have had particular operational interest in the South American region — possibly related to the Amazon basin's ecological significance.

Villas-Boas Connection (Brazil, 1957)

The António Villas-Boas case — the first extensively documented abduction case in the world (1957, Minas Gerais, Brazil), predating the Hill case by four years — describes beings that some researchers associate with the Egarot type rather than the Grey type. The beings in the Villas-Boas account were described as wearing white suits with helmets, approximately human height but slightly taller, communicating in sounds unlike any known language. Some researchers note the fit with the Egarot description; others associate the case with Nordic or other humanoid types. The Villas-Boas case is complicated by its sexual encounter component, which introduces behavioral elements not associated with the protective Egarot profile in the handbook.

Context within the Russian Handbook

The Egarot entry occupies a moderate position in the handbook's documentation depth — more detailed than some entries but less extensive than the Greys, Maitre, or Solipsi Rai entries. This moderation of detail may reflect the level of Soviet intelligence contact with, or intelligence about, this species relative to others. The positioning of the Egarot as protective/neutral alongside the Maitre as hostile creates an interesting contrast within the handbook — suggesting the Soviet intelligence framework recognized a spectrum of NHI disposition toward humanity rather than a single unified agenda.
