🌠 Lyrans / Laan

Beings from the Lyra constellation are considered by a broad spectrum of contactee and channeled sources to be among the most ancient humanoid civilizations in the galaxy — and the progenitor race from which most other humanoid species, including Pleiadians, Sirians, Arcturians, and ultimately Earth humans, descend. The Russian Alien Races Book documents this race as "Laan" — one of the foundational entries in the handbook. The Lyran origin story is one of the most developed cosmological frameworks in the contact literature.

Physical Description

  • Height: 6–7 ft; strongly built and robust compared to the more slender Pleiadian type. Some accounts describe particularly elder Lyrans as taller still.
  • Features: Blend of what contactee sources describe as pan-human features — a kind of averaged human that suggests the common root of multiple Earth racial types. Facial structure strong and well-defined. Bearing described as conveying great experience and depth.
  • Hair: Auburn to blonde, with brown and darker hair reported in sub-groups. Typically worn in simple styles; some accounts describe elaborate braiding or arrangements in ceremonial contexts.
  • Eyes: Blue to hazel range most commonly reported; some sub-groups described with deeper brown or green.
  • Skin: Pale to lightly tanned; not the chalky white of the Tall Whites or the blue tint of Arcturians.

Cat-People / Feline Lyran Variant

A distinct sub-type of Lyran is described in multiple independent sources: a feline-featured variant with similar build but with slit-pupil eyes and a very fine fur or textured skin. This "Cat People" or feline Lyran type appears in both Lyssa Royal's channeled material and in separate contactee accounts, and is positioned as a parallel evolutionary branch from the same Lyran root — humanoid intelligence that developed feline rather than purely primate characteristics. Some researchers connect this type to the ancient Egyptian lion-headed deities (Sekhmet, Tefnut) and the Sphinx.

Russian Alien Races Book — "Laan" Entry

The Russian Alien Races Book entry for the Laan describes:

  • Height: 1.9–2.1 metres (6.2–6.9 ft)
  • Origin: Lyra constellation
  • Assessment: Neutral to positive; known to interact constructively with other humanoid civilizations
  • Antiquity: Described as among the oldest spacefaring races in the handbook — positioned as foundational in the galactic community
  • Known activities: Exploration, archiving of civilizational knowledge, interaction with descendant civilizations (Pleiadians, Sirians, others)

The Laan entry is notable for its relatively brief description compared to the detail given to more actively Earth-interacting species — consistent with the characterization of Lyrans as an ancient elder race that has stepped back from direct intervention in younger civilizations.

Galactic History: The Lyran War

Multiple independent contactee and channeled sources describe an ancient catastrophe known as the "Lyran War" or "Lyran cataclysm" — an event said to have occurred approximately 22 million years ago (some sources cite much shorter timescales) in which the original Lyran home worlds were destroyed. The cause varies across sources: an internal civil war, an attack from an external species (the Alpha Draconians in Collier's account), or a catastrophic energy event connected to a Lyran civilization-level experiment.

The consequences described consistently across sources:

  • Lyran survivors dispersed across the galaxy, carrying their civilization's genetics and knowledge.
  • Migration to the Pleiades → Pleiadians (Lyran descendants in Pleiades star cluster)
  • Migration to Sirius region → component of Sirian civilization
  • Migration to Arcturus → component of Arcturian civilization
  • Migration to Procyon, Vega, Tau Ceti, and other systems → further humanoid species
  • Earth → ultimately seeded with Lyran genetic material, making Earth humans the most recently manifested branch of the Lyran lineage

This framework — Lyra as the "cradle of humanoid civilization" — is the closest thing to a creation mythology in the contactee literature and is referenced explicitly or implicitly across dozens of independent source streams.

Key Sources

  • Lyssa Royal & Keith Priest — The Prism of Lyra (1992) is the most comprehensive single treatment of Lyran cosmology. Royal channels material she attributes to the "Sasha" entity and other Lyran/Pleiadian sources. The book covers Lyran physical types, the galactic family tree, the feline variant, and the Lyran war mythology.
  • Barbara Marciniak — Pleiadian channeled material includes extensive treatment of Lyran prehistory as the ancestral foundation of the Pleiadian civilization the Bringers of the Dawn represent.
  • Alex Collier — Andromedan-sourced galactic history positions the Lyrans as the root stock of most galactic humanoid civilizations, consistent with the above.
  • Sheldan Nidle — Sirian High Council material references the Lyran origin of the Sirian civilization.
