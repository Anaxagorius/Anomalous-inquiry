⚠️ Maitre

The Maitre (French: "Master") are documented in the alleged Soviet/KGB intelligence handbook known as the Book of Alien Races as among the most dangerous non-human species catalogued. Their profile in the handbook — along with witness accounts from researchers studying the most traumatic categories of abduction and unexplained biological phenomena — positions the Maitre as an apex-predator NHI operating independently of the Grey hierarchy, engaged in systematic biological exploitation of Earth's biosphere.

Russian Alien Races Book Entry

The Maitre entry in the alleged Soviet KGB intelligence handbook — claimed to have been translated and published in English by Dante Santori and Petro Kichkaev — is one of the most detailed in the document. Key points from the entry:

  • Assessment: Hostile — the Maitre are assessed as the most dangerous species documented in the handbook, actively harmful to human interests.
  • First documented Earth visit: assessed at 1947 or earlier, with possible pre-modern contact.
  • Known activities: abductions, cattle and animal mutilations, non-consensual biological experimentation, territorial behavior over operational areas.
  • Technology: described as significantly advanced relative to early 21st century human capability.
  • Relationship to Greys: described as operating independently, not subordinate to or cooperative with the Zeta Reticulan Grey types.

Physical Description

From the Russian Alien Races Book entry and corroborating witness accounts:

  • Height: 2.0–2.5 metres (6.5–8.2 ft) — substantially taller than Grey types.
  • Skin: Extremely pale to near-translucent white with a slightly bluish tint. Described as almost paper-thin in appearance, with underlying structural features faintly visible. In certain lighting conditions, a slight iridescence is reported.
  • Cranium: Very large, elongated — disproportionately large even relative to Tall Grey types. Elongated posteriorly rather than superiorly.
  • Eyes: Deeply set, completely black (no sclera, iris, or pupil visible — a solid opaque black surface), but positioned differently from Greys — more deeply recessed under prominent brow ridges, giving a more predatory appearance.
  • Facial features: No cartilaginous nose structure — only a vestigial vertical slit for nostrils. No lips — the mouth is described as a nearly invisible thin line. No ears. The overall facial impression is described as deeply alien and unsettling, more so than the Grey type.
  • Neck: Extremely long and thin — disproportionate even relative to the large head it supports.
  • Build: Thin body but with dense, powerful musculature beneath the skin. Not fragile despite the thin frame.
  • Hands: Four long, tapered fingers. No thumb equivalent reported in most accounts.
  • Movement: Described as fluid in an unsettling way — joints appearing to flex in unexpected directions, locomotion faster than the thin build would suggest.
  • Hair: None.

Behavioral Profile

The Maitre behavioral profile, as assembled from the handbook entry and researchers who have studied the most severe abduction and mutilation cases, is strikingly consistent:

  • Apex predator mentality: No apparent concept of consent or reciprocal obligation in interactions with other species. Interactions are extractive and instrumental — other beings, including humans, are treated as biological resources.
  • Territorial behavior: The handbook documents territorial conflict with other NHI species over access to Earth operational areas, suggesting the Maitre view certain regions as proprietary.
  • Independent operation: Unlike Grey types that appear to operate under some form of hierarchical direction, Maitre appear to be autonomous — not subordinate to other NHI groups.
  • Caste structure: Different physical variants within the Maitre species appear to serve different roles — physical size and build varying across what appears to be a functionally differentiated society.
  • No reported communicative interaction: Unlike Nordics (messages), Greys (telepathic communication during procedures), or Mantids (silent emotional connection), Maitre appear to have no interest in communication with human subjects. Accounts describe only clinical procedures and then departure.

Possible Case Connections

The Guarapiranga Reservoir Case (Brazil, 1988)

In September 1988, a male body was discovered near the Guarapiranga Reservoir in São Paulo, Brazil. The official autopsy by Dr. Cuenca documented surgical removals of body parts with unusual characteristics: eyes, ears, lips, tongue, and genitalia removed with precision incisions showing no signs of struggle, hemorrhage, or standard surgical technique. Internal organs removed through small incisions — the abdomen apparently drained of organs through cuts far smaller than would be required by conventional surgical access. Crucially, the wounds showed characteristics that Dr. Cuenca described as inconsistent with any known surgical technique and suggesting an unknown cutting technology. The case was investigated by researcher A.J. Gevaerd and documented in the Brazilian UFO Magazine. The methodology is consistent with what Maitre operations are described as in the Russian handbook.

Cattle Mutilation Pattern (American Southwest, 1970s–present)

The pattern of surgical precision, organ-specific removal, bloodless incisions, and laser-cut precision documented in American cattle mutilations by investigator Linda Moulton Howe (beginning with her 1980 Emmy-winning documentary Strange Harvest) is consistent with the Maitre biological extraction methodology described in the handbook. Howe's investigations produced veterinary and laboratory analysis confirming the precision and anomalous nature of the incisions, and law enforcement documentation from multiple states confirming the phenomenon's reality.

Filiberto Cardenas Abduction (1979, Florida)

Filiberto Cardenas disappeared from a roadside in Hialeah, Florida on January 3, 1979, in front of three witnesses including his family. He returned hours later reporting an abduction by beings described as distinctly taller and more intimidating than the classic Grey type — pale, elongated cranium, deeply unsettling presence. Investigated by researcher Virgilio Sanchez-Ocejo, the case includes multiple witness statements and physical sequelae from Cardenas' experience.

Distinction from Greys

Researchers who have identified a possible Maitre presence in case files consistently note the following distinguishing characteristics from standard Grey encounters:

  • Taller (Maitre 2–2.5m vs. Short Greys 1.1–1.2m)
  • Paler, with a distinctive bluish tint to white skin (vs. neutral grey)
  • No apparent worker class — Maitre appear to perform all functions themselves
  • No communication, no hybrid presentation, no apparent interest in the experiencer's emotional state
  • More violent and physically harmful outcomes in associated cases
  • Not reported in groups with other NHI species — operate independently
