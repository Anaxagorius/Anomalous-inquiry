🦗 Mantids / Insectoids

Mantid or insectoid beings — tall, praying mantis-like entities with large compound eyes, elongated limbs, and a bearing suggesting ancient, patient intelligence — occupy a distinctive position in the NHI literature. Unlike Greys, who typically perform procedures, Mantids are almost exclusively reported in supervisory or observational roles. Experiencers consistently describe them as emanating a sense of profound age and authority, and many report developing unexpected emotional connections with Mantid beings across multiple encounters.

Physical Description

  • Height: 6–7 ft, sometimes reported taller. They appear even larger due to their hunched resting posture and extraordinarily long limbs.
  • Head: Distinctly triangular or elongated, tapering to a pointed top. Set on a thin, flexible neck. Proportionately larger than the head of any Grey type.
  • Eyes: Large, faceted or multifaceted — compound eye structures unlike any mammalian eye. Color: greenish-gold, amber, or deep brown. Some accounts describe a slight internal luminosity. The eyes occupy most of the front of the triangular face and wrap slightly to the sides.
  • Appendages: Extremely long, thin arms that fold at multiple joints, suggesting the folded foreleg structure of a praying mantis. Lower limbs similarly long and jointed. Movement described as fluid but non-human — articulating at angles that human limbs cannot.
  • Antennae: Vestigial or small antenna structures at the top of the head are reported in a subset of accounts; absent in others.
  • Coloring: Mottled brown, green-grey, or olive — often described as difficult to see clearly due to the coloring blending with the environment or appearing to shift slightly.
  • Integument: Skin described as armored or chitin-like in texture — not soft skin but something harder and segmented. No clothing reported in most accounts; the body surface itself appears to function as protection.
  • Communication: Almost exclusively telepathic in reported interactions. No audible sound attributed to Mantids in the majority of accounts.

Behavioral Profile

The behavioral signature of Mantid beings is one of the most consistent aspects of their reported presence across independent case archives:

  • Supervisory positioning: Mantids are overwhelmingly described as standing at the periphery of encounter settings, observing medical procedures being performed by Grey beings without directly participating. Their presence appears to communicate authority — Grey beings are sometimes described as deferring to the Mantid.
  • Ancient wisdom quality: Virtually every experiencer who has reported a Mantid encounter describes feeling in the presence of something profoundly old — a sense of vast accumulated experience that produces both awe and, somewhat surprisingly, calm.
  • Emotional and psychic connection: Unlike Greys, Mantids appear to engage experiencers on an emotional and spiritual level. Multiple researchers (notably Kathleen Marden and Barbara Lamb) document cases where experiencers report a developing relationship with a specific Mantid being over multiple encounters — a sense of being known and recognized that is rarely reported with Greys.
  • Patience: Described as having a quality of stillness and patience unlike any biological entity experiencers have encountered. Simon Parkes describes his Mantid contact (whom he refers to as his "Mantis mother") as exhibiting a patience calibrated in far longer time-scales than human experience.
  • Non-aggressive: No accounts in the research literature describe Mantid beings engaging in threatening or violent behavior. They observe; they do not act directly on the experiencer's body in the accounts available.

Key Cases & Testimony

Dr. David Jacobs Hypnotherapy Cases

Several of Dr. David Jacobs' hypnotic regression subjects described Mantid or insectoid beings in supervisory roles during encounter sequences that began with Grey beings. Jacobs' case archive, documented in The Threat (1998) and Walking Among Us (2015), positions the Mantid-type beings as potentially senior in hierarchy to both Greys and Grey-hybrid beings.

Simon Parkes — Extended Contact Claims

Simon Parkes, a former Whitby Town Councillor and Labour Party member in the UK, has given extensive public testimony claiming lifelong contact with Mantid beings, whom he describes as his "Mantis mother" (a female Mantid entity with whom he claims a familial bond) and with Grey beings. Parkes describes a complex relational history spanning decades. His claimed contact became public through Project Camelot interviews (2010 onward) and his own YouTube channel. His political career gave his claims unusual credibility context — a sitting elected official willing to stake his public reputation on contact testimony.

Kathleen Marden Case Files

Kathleen Marden, Betty Hill's niece, MUFON research director, and abduction researcher, has documented multiple cases involving Mantid presence through her hypnotherapy and interview work. Marden notes the consistent emotional qualities attributed to Mantid beings by independent experiencers as among the most striking aspects of the Mantid sub-category.

Yvonne Smith — CERO Cases

Yvonne Smith, certified hypnotherapist and founder of CERO (Close Encounter Research Organization), has gathered Mantid descriptions through her long-running hypnotherapy practice with abduction experiencers. Smith's case archive represents one of the largest single-researcher collections of Mantid encounter material.

Barbara Lamb — Hypnotherapy Archive

Barbara Lamb, a certified hypnotherapist and crop circle researcher, has conducted over 2,000 hypnotherapy sessions with ET experiencers since 1991 and has documented the Mantid type extensively, noting that in her client base they appear in approximately 15–20% of encounters involving multiple NHI types.

Cross-Cultural Parallels

  • Iktomi (Lakota Sioux): Spider/insect trickster being of great intelligence and antiquity; associated with the web of existence and hidden knowledge.
  • Hopi Blue Star Kachina: Some researchers interpret the Blue Star Kachina prophetic figure — described as arriving from the sky to herald a new age — as potentially insectoid or mantid in appearance based on certain Hopi artistic depictions.
  • Khepri (Egyptian): Scarab-headed deity of dawn and transformation; the rolling of the dung ball as metaphor for the solar disc moving across the sky. Khepri's insect-headed form in Egyptian art is among the most striking NHI candidate images in ancient iconography.
  • Mesoamerican insect deities: Bee and wasp deities appear across multiple Mesoamerican cultures; the Ah-Muzen-Cab (bee god) in Maya iconography is sometimes cited by researchers as potentially reflecting an insectoid NHI presence in Pre-Columbian Mesoamerica.

Russian Alien Races Book

The Russian Alien Races Book does not appear to contain a specific Mantid-type entry. However, the handbook documents several hive-minded species described as ancient and numerous, with colonial intelligence structures — characteristics that overlap with the behavioral profile attributed to Mantid beings. Some researchers mapping the handbook's entries to encountered types have suggested that the insectoid-adjacent entries may represent a taxonomic grouping that includes the Mantid type under a broader "hive intelligence" category. The absence of a specific Mantid entry is itself notable, given the frequency of the type in Western abduction literature.
