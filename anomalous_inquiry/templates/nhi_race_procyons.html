⚡ Procyons

The Procyons are described across multiple independent contact and channeled sources as a humanoid civilization from the Procyon system — a binary star system 11.46 light-years from Earth in the constellation Canis Minor. One of the nearest stellar systems to Earth, Procyon A is a white-yellow main-sequence star slightly more luminous than the Sun, accompanied by the white dwarf Procyon B. In the contact literature, Procyons appear most distinctively as a civilization that has had direct experience of Grey manipulation or control and has actively worked to liberate itself — a background that positions them as potential allies with a uniquely informed perspective on the Grey agenda.

Stellar Context

Procyon (Alpha Canis Minoris) is the eighth-brightest star in the night sky, easily visible in winter/spring skies as the brightest point in the small constellation Canis Minor (the Little Dog). Its name derives from the Greek for "before the dog" — it rises slightly before Sirius (the Dog Star) in the seasonal sky progression. This proximity to Sirius in the sky and in mythological association has led some researchers to propose cultural linkage between Sirian and Procyon NHI types.

Procyon A has an estimated age of 1.87 billion years — younger than the Sun's 4.6 billion years — which, if accurate, would suggest a civilization from this system would be less developmentally advanced than one from a star system comparable in age to our own. This apparent contradiction with contact claims of advanced Procyon civilization is resolved in multiple ways in the literature: through claims of panspermia and multi-system civilization migration, through claims that age-of-civilization is not linearly tied to stellar age, or through the dimensional-state framework used by several contactees.

Physical Description

Procyon physical descriptions in the contact literature:

  • Height: Approximately 5–6 ft; human proportions.
  • Build: Slender but more athletic than the gracile Nordic type; described as capable and physically active. Some accounts describe them as more robustly built than Pleiadians, with a quality of physical readiness or alertness.
  • Skin: Light — pale cream to very light tan. Similar to fair-complected Northern European humans.
  • Eyes: Described variably as blue, grey, or pale hazel; typically normal human proportion, unlike the oversized eyes of Greys or the slightly enlarged eyes of other humanoid types.
  • Hair: Light — blonde to white-blonde; medium length in most accounts.
  • Distinguishing feature: A quality of heightened alertness or watchfulness — multiple accounts note that Procyon beings seem continuously aware of their environment in a way that goes beyond normal attentiveness, possibly a survival adaptation from their historical conflict with Grey forces.

Historical Narrative — Liberation from Grey Control

The most distinctive and consistent element of the Procyon narrative across multiple independent sources is their history of conflict with and liberation from Grey or Zeta Reticulan control. This narrative appears in contactee material, channeled sources, and several alleged insider testimonies in broadly compatible forms:

According to the most detailed versions of this narrative — derived primarily from sources citing private briefings or direct contact — the Procyon civilization at a certain point in its history came under the influence or direct control of Grey/Zeta factions, who exploited Procyon resources and potentially conducted biological experimentation on the population. This is described as occurring through a combination of technological superiority and manipulation of Procyon leadership rather than direct military conquest. The Procyon liberation — achieved with assistance from other galactic civilizations — is described as a relatively recent historical event on a civilizational timescale.

The significance of this narrative in the contact literature is that it positions Procyons as:

  • A civilization with direct experiential knowledge of Grey methodology, motivations, and techniques — making them valuable informants about the Grey agenda on Earth.
  • A civilization with specific motivation to prevent other civilizations from experiencing what they experienced — providing a rationale for their interest in and concern for Earth.
  • A civilization that understands the gap between technological advancement and ethical development from lived experience — a perspective that shapes their communications with Earth contacts.

Behavioral Profile & Agenda

Based on compiled contact and channeled sources, Procyon behavioral characteristics and reported agenda:

  • Anti-Grey orientation: The most consistent behavioral marker. Procyons are described as actively monitoring Grey operations on Earth and providing information to human contacts about Grey methods. This contrasts with the more neutral observation stance of other benevolent NHI types.
  • Tactical intelligence: Unlike the philosophical, consciousness-focused communications of Pleiadians or Arcturians, Procyon communications in the contact literature tend toward the practical — specific information about Grey technology, Grey intentions, how to detect implants, how to resist telepathic control.
  • Human sovereignty advocacy: Multiple sources attribute to Procyon beings an explicit position that humanity should develop genuine technological and social self-sufficiency rather than becoming dependent on benevolent NHI assistance — precisely because dependency creates the vulnerability they experienced.
  • Technology sharing (limited): Several sources describe Procyon willingness to share specific defensive or detection technologies — not advanced weapons, but tools for identifying Grey surveillance, detecting implants, and shielding consciousness from telepathic intrusion.

Key Sources

Lyssa Royal — "The Prism of Lyra" Framework

Channel Lyssa Royal, in The Prism of Lyra (1992) and subsequent channeled work, describes Procyon civilization in the context of the broader galactic family. Royal's material specifically addresses the Procyon history with Grey interaction and their role as a civilization that has "walked the path" that Earth is currently on. The Procyon beings in Royal's framework offer wisdom specifically applicable to the Grey contact situation on Earth from direct historical experience.

Robert Dean — Alleged NATO Briefings

Retired US Army Command Sergeant Major Robert O. Dean (1929–2018) claimed to have read a classified NATO cosmic-level document called "The Assessment" (1964) while serving at SHAPE headquarters in Europe. Dean described the document as cataloguing several NHI species, including beings "virtually indistinguishable from humans" — a description overlapping with Procyon characteristics in the broader Nordic taxonomy. Dean's claims are unverified; no copy of the document has been independently produced.

Sheldan Nidle — Galactic Federation Material

Channel Sheldan Nidle's extensive material on the Galactic Federation of Light (channeled from the Sirian High Council, per Nidle) places Procyons as full members of the Federation and describes their specific contributions to galactic diplomacy and conflict resolution based on their historical experience with external control.

Alex Collier — Galactic Political Context

Contactee Alex Collier's Andromedan-sourced material mentions the Procyon system in the context of the broader galactic political situation, consistent with the liberation narrative present in other sources.

Canis Minor — Ancient Cultural Context

Canis Minor (the Little Dog) has fewer mythological associations than the prominent constellations, but several relevant cross-cultural connections exist:

  • Egyptian connection: Procyon's rise preceded the Nile flooding season in ancient Egypt, giving it agricultural significance; it was tracked alongside Sirius in Egyptian astronomical records. The pairing of Sirius (the greater Dog Star) and Procyon (the lesser) in Egyptian observation may reflect awareness of both systems' significance.
  • Mesopotamian astronomy: Procyon appeared in Babylonian star catalogues as the "Star of the Herald" — a name suggesting a messenger or advance-notice function consistent with the contact literature's Procyon profile as forewarners regarding Grey activities.
  • Greek myth: Canis Minor was associated with Maera, the faithful dog of Icarius, who revealed the body of his murdered master. The theme of faithful revelation of hidden events resonates with the Procyon contact narrative.
