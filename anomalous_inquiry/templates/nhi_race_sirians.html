⭐ Sirians (Sirius System)

Beings alleged to originate from the Sirius star system — home to Sirius A (the brightest star in Earth's night sky, 8.6 light-years distant) and its white dwarf companion Sirius B — appear across multiple independent evidence streams: ancient Egyptian religion and cosmology, the remarkable astronomical traditions of the Dogon people of Mali, channeled contactee material, and structured hypnotic regression accounts. The Sirius connection in human history is among the most well-documented astronomical-mythological convergences available to researchers.

Physical Description

Multiple distinct Sirian sub-types appear across the literature:

Type 1 (Sirian A Origin)

Tall (6–7 ft), powerfully built, dark-featured humanoids. Dark skin tones ranging from deep brown to near-black. Large, dark eyes. Strong facial bone structure. Described in several channeled sources as warriors and protectors in the galactic context. Some accounts describe them as "leonine" in appearance — not literally feline but with a lion-like quality of strength and bearing.

Type 2 (Sirian B Origin)

Shorter and more slender than the Type 1. Lighter skin coloring — olive to tan. Associated in channeled material with healing arts, water, and what some sources describe as cetacean (dolphin/whale) consciousness. Some Sirian B descriptions overlap with the standard Nordic type in appearance.

Dolphin/Cetacean Sirians

A non-humanoid type described primarily in channeled material — cetacean-form beings of advanced consciousness said to have seeded Earth's oceans with cetacean species as "biological libraries." This type appears exclusively in channeled sources (notably Patricia Cori) and has no corroborating physical encounter accounts.

Russian Alien Races Book

The handbook documents a species from the Sirius region described as tall, dark-featured bipeds — consistent with the Type 1 description above. Assessment: generally neutral to positive in orientation toward Earth. The handbook notes their long-standing relationship with human civilizations, possibly predating the modern era.

The Dogon Connection

The most compelling evidence for ancient Sirian contact in the anthropological record is provided by the Dogon people of the Bandiagara Escarpment in Mali, West Africa. French anthropologists Marcel Griaule and Germaine Dieterlen documented the Dogon's cosmological knowledge beginning in the 1930s, publishing their findings in the journal Journal de la Société des Africanistes in 1950 and in Griaule's posthumous Le Renard Pâle (1965).

The Dogon possessed the following astronomical knowledge about the Sirius system:

  • Sirius B (Po Tolo): The Dogon knew of the existence of Sirius B — a white dwarf star invisible to the naked eye and not photographed until 1970 — describing it as the "heaviest star" (white dwarfs have extreme density, approximately one solar mass compressed into an Earth-sized object).
  • Orbital period: The Dogon stated the orbital period of Sirius B around Sirius A as 50 years — the actual period is 49.9 years.
  • Elliptical orbit: The Dogon described the orbit as elliptical, not circular.
  • Sirius C (Emme Ya): The Dogon described a third star in the Sirius system — which Western astronomy did not confirm until 1995 (and remains contested).

Robert Temple, in The Sirius Mystery (1976, expanded 1998), proposed this knowledge derived from ancient contact with Sirian beings — specifically the Nommo, amphibious beings described in Dogon tradition as having come from Sirius to teach humanity civilization. Temple documented not only the Dogon knowledge but extensive Egyptian and Sumerian parallels suggesting a broader ancient Sirian contact tradition.

Academic critique: Walter van Beek's 1991 fieldwork with the Dogon disputed Griaule's findings, arguing the specific astronomical knowledge was not present in the Dogon community he interviewed. This critique itself was disputed; the evidentiary debate continues. Temple's response to van Beek's critique is addressed in the expanded 1998 edition of The Sirius Mystery.

Ancient Egyptian Context

Sirius — known to ancient Egyptians as Sopdet (Sothis in Greek) — held the most important position of any star in the Egyptian religious calendar. Its annual heliacal rising (first appearance on the eastern horizon just before sunrise after a period of invisibility) coincided with the annual Nile flood — the event on which Egyptian agricultural civilization depended. The heliacal rising of Sirius marked the Egyptian New Year.

  • The goddess Isis was identified with Sirius/Sopdet; her tears flooding the Nile at her husband Osiris's death were the mythological explanation for the flood.
  • Osiris was identified with the Orion belt stars — creating the Sirius/Orion dual cosmology that researcher Robert Bauval documented in The Orion Mystery (1994).
  • The air shafts of the Great Pyramid's Queen's Chamber are precisely aligned to Sirius (southern shaft) — documented by Bauval and confirmed by independent survey.
  • Temple pylons at Denderah and Abu Simbel are oriented to Sirius's rising position.

The Nommo of Dogon tradition — amphibious, fish-tailed beings that came from Sirius — have an Egyptian parallel in the Oannes/Apkallu tradition documented by the Babylonian historian Berossus: semi-divine beings who emerged from the sea and taught humanity writing, law, agriculture, and astronomy. The fish-cloak worn by the Apkallu in Sumerian relief sculpture may represent either a literal fish suit (diving equipment?) or a metaphorical amphibious NHI description.

Channeled & Contactee Material

Patricia Cori has channeled material attributed to the "Sirian High Council" since the 1990s, producing the Sirian Revelations trilogy and related texts. The material covers Sirian-influenced aspects of Earth's ancient history including Atlantis, the construction of the Egyptian monuments, and cetacean consciousness as a Sirian seeding project. Cori's material is among the most internally consistent and extensively detailed Sirian channeled corpus.

Sheldan Nidle's Galactic Federation of Light material features Sirian beings prominently — positioning them as among Earth's primary galactic sponsors and advocates within a larger galactic governance structure.

Amorah Quan Yin's Pleiadian-Sirian-Risian connection material explores the interrelationship between Sirian, Pleiadian, and Arcturus star system beings as a cooperative group involved in Earth's spiritual development.
