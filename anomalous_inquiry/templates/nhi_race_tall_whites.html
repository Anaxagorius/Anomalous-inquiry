🤍 Tall Whites

The Tall Whites are a non-human species documented primarily through the extensive testimony of Charles Hall, a former United States Air Force weatherman who claims sustained observation and direct interaction with this species during his service at Nellis AFB's Indian Springs auxiliary field in Nevada from 1965 to 1967. Hall's account is unusual among contact claims for its prosaic, matter-of-fact detail, its specific military-operational context, and its four-volume autobiographical documentation published more than three decades after the events he describes.

Physical Description

Based on Charles Hall's testimony and corroborating accounts from researchers who interviewed Hall:

  • Skin: Chalky white, almost fluorescent — described by Hall as appearing to glow faintly in low light. The skin has a translucent quality; underlying structures are faintly visible in very bright light. Texture smooth and paper-thin in appearance.
  • Eyes: Large, blue, with a cat-like (vertically elliptical) pupil structure. The eyes are described as striking and unsettling in direct contact — larger than human but not the vast wrap-around eyes of the Grey type.
  • Height: Young adult Tall Whites approximately 5–5.5 ft tall. A key characteristic of the species is that they continue growing throughout their lives — elderly individuals reach 7 ft and beyond. This growth-with-age pattern is one of the most distinctive biological features in Hall's account.
  • Hair: White to nearly transparent white-blonde, fine in texture. Adults typically wear it short.
  • Fingers: Long and thin, with claw-like nails — Hall describes these as potentially injurious in close contact.
  • Voice: High-pitched, described as capable of reaching frequencies that cause physical pain or disorientation in humans at will. Hall reports being warned by the beings themselves about standing too close when they vocalized at full volume.
  • Speed and reflexes: Substantially faster than human reflexes. Hall describes instances of Tall Whites disappearing and reappearing within his visual field at speeds he found alarming.
  • Lifespan: Hall was told their lifespan extends to 700–800 Earth years, accounting for the continued physical growth into old age.
  • Uniform: Typically wear a form-fitting white jump-suit that appears to be a technological garment, not merely clothing — likely providing life support, thermal regulation, and possibly communication functions.

Primary Source: Charles Hall Testimony

Charles James Hall served as a weather observer (AFSC 30050, "Weather Specialist") at Nellis Air Force Base, specifically at the Indian Springs Auxiliary Field and the adjacent Gunnery Ranges, from approximately 1965 to 1967. Hall describes an arrangement in which the Tall Whites had an established presence on the range — predating his arrival and known to base personnel — that was maintained through an informal agreement with elements of the United States Air Force.

The arrangement included:

  • Use of a large hangar facility (Hall refers to "Hangar 18" equivalents at the Nellis range) for spacecraft maintenance and storage.
  • A living area or "village" in a remote canyon section of the range where Tall White families resided, including children who played outdoors at night.
  • Access to basic human supplies and goods, including canned food and clothing items from the base commissary.
  • Non-interference from base personnel, enforced by Air Force officers who had knowledge of the arrangement.

Hall reports that most airmen assigned to the range were not briefed on the Tall White presence and instead experienced terror, mental breakdowns, or inexplicable "accidents" when they unexpectedly encountered Tall Whites. Hall himself, by his account, survived through a combination of luck, natural temperament, and eventually developing a functional working relationship with several Tall Whites — particularly with what he describes as a "Teacher" figure (the Tall Whites referred to knowledgeable humans as "teachers" — a term of respect in their culture).

Hall notes that the Tall Whites used translator devices — small technology carried on their persons — to communicate with humans, converting their high-frequency vocalizations into perceptible language.

Behavioral Profile

Hall's observations of Tall White behavioral patterns, drawn across his two-year deployment:

  • Dangerous when threatened or startled: Hall's most consistent warning about the Tall Whites is their lethal potential when they perceive a threat to themselves or their children. On several occasions Hall describes narrowly avoiding death when Tall Whites misread his approach as threatening. The Air Force officers he served under confirmed that previous airmen had been seriously injured in such incidents.
  • Hierarchical society: Tall Whites have a clear internal hierarchy. "Scout craft" pilots are younger, lower-status beings. The "teachers" (older, taller individuals) carry authority. Hall describes generals and admirals among the Tall Whites who interacted with senior Air Force officers.
  • Business arrangement, not friendship: The Tall Whites' relationship with the US military was transactional — Hall describes it as a mutual use arrangement, not an alliance. The Tall Whites obtained supplies, maintenance facilities, and access to Earth's biosphere; the Air Force obtained technology transfer (described by Hall as limited and carefully controlled by the Tall Whites).
  • Long-lived perspective: Their centuries-long lifespan apparently produces a fundamentally different temporal perspective — Hall describes them as patient in ways difficult for humans to conceptualize, willing to wait decades for outcomes they view as eventual certainties.
  • Night preference: Children and adults alike preferred the Nevada desert at night — bright sunlight was apparently uncomfortable, though the white jump-suits appeared to provide some protection.

Connections to Other Testimony

Paul Hellyer, former Canadian Minister of National Defence (1963–67) and Deputy Prime Minister, publicly stated in multiple venues that he believed Charles Hall's testimony was credible and that Tall Whites were a real species with an established Earth presence. Hellyer cited Hall's books as among the most convincing contact accounts he had encountered in his research after leaving government service.

Linda Moulton Howe (Earthfiles) conducted multiple investigations into Hall's testimony, corroborating details of his service record, his weather station assignments, and the geography of the Nellis range as described in his books. Howe has described Hall as among the most credible sources she has investigated.

Researcher Paola Harris conducted extensive interviews with Hall and investigated the Nellis range geography, publishing analysis that supported the consistency of Hall's account with publicly available records of the area.

Russian Alien Races Book

The Russian Alien Races Book documents a species with characteristics closely matching the Tall White description. The handbook's entry describes beings of great height with white skin originating from a star in the Boötes constellation. The assessment in the handbook is cautiously neutral — these beings are noted as having made contact with Earth authorities through established channels, not pursuing the covert biological operations associated with Greys or the overtly hostile posture of the Maitre. The handbook notes their longevity and physical speed as distinguishing characteristics, consistent with Hall's account.
