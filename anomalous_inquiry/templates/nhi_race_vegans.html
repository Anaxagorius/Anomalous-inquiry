💫 Vegans / Vega Beings

The Vegans — beings associated with Vega, the brightest star in the constellation Lyra at 25.04 light-years from Earth — occupy a crucial position in the galactic cosmology presented across the contact literature. In multiple independent frameworks, Vega is described as the specific origin point of the first humanoid expansion from the Lyran system — making Vegans the ancestral or founding branch of a vast tree of humanoid civilizations that includes Pleiadians, Sirians, Andromedans, and ultimately Earth humans. The Vega civilization is thus described as ancient, foundational, and carrying the original genetic and consciousness template of the humanoid form.

Stellar Context

Vega (Alpha Lyrae) is the fifth-brightest star in the night sky and the brightest star in the constellation Lyra, best visible in summer skies in the northern hemisphere. It is a white A-type main-sequence star, significantly more luminous than the Sun, at 25.04 light-years from Earth. Vega forms the topmost vertex of the prominent Summer Triangle asterism (alongside Altair and Deneb).

Vega has particular astronomical significance beyond its brightness:

  • Pole Star past and future: Due to Earth's precession (the slow wobble of its rotational axis over ~26,000 years), Vega was the north pole star approximately 12,000 years ago and will again become the pole star approximately 12,000 years in the future. It served as the pole star during the approximate era of the earliest human civilizations' possible origins.
  • First exoplanet disk detection: In 1983, the IRAS infrared satellite detected an anomalous infrared excess around Vega — later confirmed as a circumstellar disk of dust and debris, the first such disk detected around any star other than the Sun. This marked the beginning of the field of debris disk research and established that Vega has a planetary system in some stage of formation or evolution.
  • Standard candle: Vega served historically as the photometric standard for stellar magnitude — the zero point of the visual magnitude scale. All other stars' brightnesses have historically been measured relative to Vega.
  • SETI candidate: Vega's relative proximity (25 ly), luminosity, and confirmed disk have made it a target for SETI observations on multiple occasions.

Its position as the most prominent star in Lyra connects Vegans directly to the broader Lyran ancestry narrative in the contact literature — in many accounts, the terms "Lyran" and "Vegan" are used interchangeably for the original humanoid stock, while in others Vegans are a specific sub-group of the Lyran civilization.

Physical Description

Two primary morphological types are described for Vegan beings across the literature:

Classic Humanoid Type

  • Height: 5–6 ft; proportions very similar to Earth humans.
  • Skin: Described with greater variation than most NHI types — ranging from pale to medium dark, including olive, light brown, and occasionally described as a pale olive-gold. This variation is attributed to the great age of the Vegan civilization, which developed diverse sub-populations over millions of years on a single world before dispersing.
  • Hair: Dark — black or dark brown — more frequently than the blonde characteristic of most Nordic types. Several sources describe this as distinguishing Vegans from Pleiadians.
  • Eyes: Dark — dark brown to near-black; large but not as dramatically so as Greys; a quality of depth and age noted by multiple contactees.
  • Build: The original Vegan stock is described as somewhat shorter and more compact than the tall, fair Pleiadian variant — reflecting a different evolutionary path from the common Lyran ancestor.

Cat-Like / Feline Type

A distinctive and unusual variant in the Vegan profile: multiple sources, including Lyssa Royal's channeled material, describe a Vegan sub-type with feline characteristics — slightly vertical-pupil eyes, a flatter nasal structure, fine skin texture reminiscent of short-furred cats, and an overall bearing or presence associated with feline alertness and grace. This type is described in Royal's framework as representing the integration of two different evolutionary streams in Vegan history. Some researchers connect this to the global prevalence of feline deities (Sekhmet and Bastet in Egypt, Parvati-as-tigress in Hindu tradition, Jaguar deities throughout Mesoamerica) as potential cultural memory of contact with Vegan feline-type beings.

The Vegan Role in Human Origins

The position of Vegans in the galactic origin narratives found across the contact literature:

In the cosmological framework presented by Lyssa Royal (The Prism of Lyra, 1992) and elaborated across multiple channeled sources, the humanoid form — bipedal, bilaterally symmetrical, tool-using, language-capable — originated in the Lyra/Vega region and spread throughout the galaxy via migration, conflict, and intentional seeding. Earth humans carry the Lyran/Vegan genetic template, with subsequent contributions from Pleiadians, Sirians, and other civilizations that each left genetic imprints through direct intervention in human evolutionary history.

In this framework, the Vegan contribution to Earth's genetic heritage is described as specifically associated with the darker-pigmented, shorter-statured human populations — the Vegan physical template expressed in Earth's diversity. This is a speculative framework presented in channeled material without anthropological verification.

In Alex Collier's Andromedan-sourced material, a compatible framework describes the Lyran/Vegan origin of most humanoid species, including specific civilizations within the Lyran cluster. Collier's material identifies the Vegan contributions to Earth's genetic makeup as among the oldest layers in the human genome, predating Pleiadian involvement.

Contact Cases & Key Sources

Lyssa Royal — The Prism of Lyra

The most detailed modern framework for Vegan civilization comes from channel Lyssa Royal's The Prism of Lyra (co-authored with Keith Priest, 1992, Royal Priest Research). Royal's source material explicitly distinguishes between Lyrans (the broader ancestral civilization) and Vegans (the specific Vega-origin population), describing Vegans as representing a specific "integration challenge" in galactic history — a civilization that had to reconcile divergent evolutionary streams within itself before emerging as a unified culture.

Carl Sagan — Contact (1985)

While not a contact claim, Carl Sagan's 1985 novel Contact (and the 1997 Robert Zemeckis film) specifically chose Vega as the origin point of the first detected extraterrestrial signal — a choice that Sagan made based on Vega's prominent visibility and relative proximity. In the story, the signal from Vega contains instructions for building a transportation device. Sagan's choice of Vega has been noted by researchers as an interesting parallel to the contact literature's identification of Vega as a significant source system, given that Sagan was an astronomer with access to SETI discussions that would not have been fully public at the time of the novel's writing.

Vega in the IRAS / SETI Context

The 1983 IRAS discovery of Vega's debris disk — the first such detection — was made by a team including Dr. Hartmut Aumann. The discovery was presented as routine astronomical research, but coincided with increasing SETI attention to nearby star systems. Project META (Megachannel Extraterrestrial Assay), overseen by Paul Horowitz at Harvard (1985–1995), included Vega as a target system. No confirmed signals were recorded from Vega; however, several unconfirmed narrowband signals from the Vega direction were noted in project logs but not independently verified.

Sheldan Nidle — Galactic Federation Context

Sheldan Nidle's extensive Galactic Federation material places Vegan civilization as a founding member of the Federation, with their long history of civilization-building informing Federation governance structures. Nidle's material describes Vegans as having "stabilized" their civilization millions of years ago and providing a long-duration model of sustainable galactic citizenship that younger civilizations look to as an example.

Ancient Cultural Connections

Vega's role as the historical pole star (~12,000–10,000 BCE) gives it particular relevance in ancient astronomical traditions:

  • Ancient Egypt: The shaft alignment of the Queen's Chamber in the Great Pyramid has been proposed (by researcher Robert Bauval and others) to point toward Vega at specific historical epochs. If the pyramid's construction encoding reflects the precessional alignment circa 10,500 BCE rather than 2,500 BCE, Vega's position as pole star at that epoch becomes architecturally significant.
  • Hindu tradition: Vega (known as Abhijit in Sanskrit — "the victorious one") was the Pole Star in Vedic astronomy and is referenced in the Mahabharata as the "best of stars." The Vedic period's attention to Abhijit/Vega as a primary navigational and cosmological reference connects the ancient Indian sky tradition to this star.
  • Chinese astronomy: Vega is one of the two stars (with Altair) in the romantic Qixi legend — the Weaving Maiden (Zhinü) separated from the Cowherd by the Milky Way. The Weaving connection is notable given the broader mythology of star-beings who taught humanity crafts and civilization skills.
  • Greek mythology: Lyra — Vega's constellation — was the lyre of Orpheus, whose music could move rocks, trees, and all living things. After Orpheus's death, his lyre was placed in the sky by Zeus. Some researchers propose this myth reflects cultural memory of Vegan beings whose technology (described as sound-based in some contact sources) was perceived as magical music.
