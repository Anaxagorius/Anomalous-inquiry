🐉 Draconians

The Draconians — distinguished from the more specific Alpha Draconian (Thuban) ruling caste — refer to the broader Draconian civilization and its multiple constituent races, all described as originating from or associated with the Draco constellation. A multi-species empire organized around strict hierarchy, military power, and long-term strategic domination, the Draconian complex is described in the contactee and whistleblower literature as the most powerful and most problematic non-human civilization currently intersecting with Earth. The Draconian civilization encompasses the winged Alpha Draconian royal caste, the Ciakar warrior nobility, and numerous subordinate reptilian species pressed into service within the empire's structure.

The Draconian Civilization Structure

The Draconian civilization — as described through the primary sources of Alex Collier's Andromedan contact information, Reptoid researcher James Bartley, and multiple whistleblower accounts — is not a single species but a rigidly hierarchical empire with multiple constituent species in defined castes:

  • Alpha Draconian / Royal Caste (Ciakars): The ruling class. Winged, 14–22 ft in height according to Collier; psychically dominant; the original Draconian bloodline. Origin attributed to the Alpha Draconis / Thuban system. Rarely encountered in modern physical contacts — operate primarily at the level of galactic politics and strategic direction.
  • Warrior-Nobility Caste: 7–9 ft; may have vestigial or smaller functional wings; command military operations. The most commonly encountered Draconian type in reported physical contacts and abduction accounts involving commanding presences.
  • Reptoid Soldier Class: The 6–8 ft basic Reptoid type that conducts direct operations, including encounters with humans. These are the beings most frequently described in terrestrial encounter reports — under Draconian authority but not of the royal lineage.
  • Client Species: The Draconian empire, per multiple research sources, incorporates Grey species — particularly certain Orion Grey types — as client or subject species. The relationship varies between alliance and coercion depending on the source.

The caste system within Draconian society is described as rigid and hereditary — determined by bloodline, not merit — and as ancient beyond any human civilization's comparable records. Alex Collier's Andromedan sources describe the Draconian empire as approximately 2.1 billion years old at the time of his 1994 lectures, making it the oldest known spacefaring civilization in the galactic community by a substantial margin.

Physical Description: Draconian Warrior Caste

The warrior-nobility Draconian type, most likely to be encountered in close-proximity reports, is described across multiple independent sources:

  • Height: 7–9 ft (2.1–2.7 m); substantially more imposing than the basic Reptoid type.
  • Build: Extremely muscular and powerful — disproportionately so compared to a human or even to the basic reptoid type. The impression conveyed in accounts is of physical power far beyond human capacity.
  • Scale pattern: More prominently scaled than the basic reptoid type; coarser, darker, with some accounts noting ridge-like scale formations along the spine and shoulders that resemble vestigial dorsal crests.
  • Coloration: Dark — deep charcoal, dark forest green, near-black in some accounts. Some descriptions note bioluminescent or iridescent quality to scale edges under certain lighting.
  • Wings: Vestigial wings are described in the warrior caste — present but not functional for flight; rather like a display structure that may have significance in Draconian social dynamics. Full, functional wings appear in the royal/Alpha caste (see Alpha Draconian profile).
  • Eyes: Larger relative to head than the basic reptoid type; described as particularly intense and penetrating in their gaze; the psychic impact of direct eye contact described as overwhelming.
  • Scent: A strong, distinctive odor — several accounts specifically describe a sulfurous, burning, or ozone-like quality in Draconian encounters that differs from the musty reptile-house scent of basic reptoid encounters.

Behavioral & Psychological Profile

The Draconian behavioral profile is among the most clearly delineated in the contact literature, largely because the consistency of its description across independent sources is striking:

  • Territorial dominance: Earth is described in Draconian cosmology (per Collier's Andromedan briefings) as a territory claimed by the Draconian empire as property — which is the fundamental basis for conflict between pro-human galactic councils and the Draconian political system. The concept of human sovereignty over Earth is not recognized within the Draconian framework.
  • Long-term strategic thinking: The timescale of Draconian planning is described as operating across centuries and millennia — human political and military activity is perceived at the level of short-term local events within a much longer strategic arc.
  • Psychic capacity and weaponry: Described as far exceeding basic reptoid types. Draconian warriors are described as capable of generating intense psychic projections — overwhelming fear, confusion, or paralysis — that function as combat capabilities. The "reptilian stare" phenomenon documented in abduction accounts (a direct, unbreakable eye contact that produces trance or paralysis) is most commonly associated with the Draconian warrior type.
  • No deception protocols: Unlike Greys, who reportedly use complex deception and virtual reality overlays, Draconian beings in encounters are described as operating through direct domination — they do not conceal what they are or what they want; the imbalance of power makes deception unnecessary from their perspective.
  • Consumption of biological material: Among the most disturbing claims in the Draconian literature — multiple independent whistleblower sources describe the Draconian hierarchy as having used humans as a food and biological resource across historical periods. This claim appears in the Branton Files, in Thomas Castello's alleged Dulce testimony, and in several channeled sources. It is presented as claimed and unverified.

Key Sources & Testimony

Alex Collier — Andromedan Contact (1994–ongoing)

Alex Collier is the most detailed and consistently cited source for Draconian civilization structure. Claiming contact since childhood with Andromedan beings (specifically, contact with beings named Vissaeus and Morenae), Collier presented his first public account of Draconian history in 1994 and elaborated it through his book Defending Sacred Ground (1996) and numerous subsequent interviews and lectures. Collier's Draconian material is internally consistent across three decades of interviews and covers: empire structure, caste system, the Draconian claim to Earth ownership, the political relationships with Grey species, historical Draconian interaction with Earth, and the galactic council dynamics around the Earth situation.

Thomas Edwin Castello — Dulce Base Testimony (c. 1990)

Thomas Edwin Castello claimed to be a former security officer at the Dulce, New Mexico underground facility who escaped with photographs, a video, and documents describing reptilian beings — specifically Draconians — occupying the lower levels of the facility. His account, documented in the Branton Files, describes: a 7-level underground structure; reptilian beings on the lower levels with access to human captives; extreme tension between the reptilian occupants and the human military personnel co-habiting the facility. Castello's claims cannot be independently verified; the individual's subsequent whereabouts are unknown. The account is widely cited in underground base and Draconian literature.

Michael Salla — Exopolitics Framework

Dr. Michael Salla (Ph.D., American University) has developed the most academically framed analysis of Draconian political dynamics in the context of galactic politics, drawing on whistleblower testimony, contactee material, and historical records. His Exopolitics series includes extensive documentation of the Draconian empire structure and Earth relations, positioned within a framework that treats these claims as requiring serious geopolitical analysis rather than dismissal.

Dragon Mythology as Draconian Record

The dragon — as distinct from the serpent — appears in mythological traditions specifically associated with the Draconian type: winged, fire-breathing (plausibly re-interpreted as energy weapons or plasma discharge), territorial, hoarders of treasure (re-interpreted as resource extraction and territorial claim), and of immense power. Key dragon traditions:

  • Tiamat (Babylonian): The primordial dragon-sea of chaos; described in the Enuma Elish as the original cosmic entity from whom the gods (Anunnaki) emerged. Some researchers interpret Tiamat as the primordial Draconian presence predating Anunnaki history on Earth.
  • Dragon Kings of China (Lung Wang): The Four Dragon Kings rule the four seas and the subterranean realms; they are shape-shifters who can appear as humans; they are the ultimate authority over weather (potentially: atmospheric technology); they are petitioned by emperors for counsel and occasionally for military support. The Dragon Kings are not evil by default — they are amoral forces of immense power, exactly consistent with the Draconian description.
  • Vritra (Vedic): The great dragon of Vedic cosmology, defeated by Indra with the thunderbolt (vajra) — potentially a record of a conflict between Draconian and other NHI forces over Earth.
  • The Western Dragon: Medieval European dragon mythology consistently depicts territorial, hoard-keeping, powerful beings associated with underground caves — a description that maps with striking fidelity onto the Draconian underground-base territorial pattern in the whistleblower literature.
  • Quetzalcoatl (Mesoamerican): The "Feathered Serpent" — winged, serpentine, associated with the highest level of cosmic authority and the founding of civilization — potentially a record of a Draconian/Alpha Draconian contact in pre-Columbian Mesoamerica.
