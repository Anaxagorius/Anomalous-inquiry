⚓ Nimitz Encounter (2004)

In November 2004, the USS Nimitz carrier strike group operating off the coast of San Diego encountered a series of unidentified aerial objects tracked by multiple radar systems. The objects were described as approximately 40 feet long, white, with no visible means of propulsion or control surfaces.

Commander David Fravor (CO, VFA-41) and Lt. Commander Jim Slaight intercepted an object that descended from 80,000 feet to sea-skimming altitude in seconds, then departed at extraordinary speed. Fravor reported the object appeared aware of his aircraft and mirrored his maneuvers.

The Tic-Tac video, formally released by the U.S. Department of Defense in 2020, shows infrared tracking of the object. Analysis by Luis Elizondo and others at AATIP (Advanced Aerospace Threat Identification Program) flagged the performance as beyond known technology.
