🌐 Out-of-Body Experiences

Out-of-body experiences (OBEs) involve the sensation of being located outside one's physical body, often including a point of view from above or across the room. Surveys suggest 10–20% of the general population report at least one OBE in their lifetime.

Susan Blackmore's 1982 survey work and the AWARE (AWAreness during REsuscitation) study by Sam Parnia at Southampton attempted to verify veridical perceptions during cardiac arrest. AWARE I (2014) found one case of possible veridical perception out of 2,060 cardiac arrests; AWARE II is ongoing.

Charles Tart's 1968 case study used a subject who reported repeatedly leaving her body during sleep and correctly identified a 5-digit number placed on a high shelf. Critics note the lack of multiple independent verification and possible normal explanations.
