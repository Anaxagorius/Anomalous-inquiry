🔬 PEAR Laboratory

The Princeton Engineering Anomalies Research (PEAR) laboratory operated from 1979 to 2007 under Robert Jahn, Dean of the School of Engineering. Its primary focus was human-machine interaction anomalies — specifically, whether human intention could produce statistically measurable deviations in random physical systems.

Over nearly three decades, PEAR collected approximately 1,000 operator-REG (Random Event Generator) experiments. Their meta-analysis reported a mean effect size of roughly 0.0003 bits per trial — small but, they argued, statistically robust over millions of trials.

Criticisms included data handling questions, lack of pre-registration, and failure of independent replication in tightly controlled settings. The laboratory closed in 2007; Jahn and Dunne established the International Consciousness Research Laboratories (ICRL) to continue theoretical work.
