⏳ Precognition Research

Precognition denotes apparent foreknowledge of future events by unexplained means. Closely related is presentiment — a physiological response to a future stimulus before it occurs, measured via electrodermal activity or heart rate.

Daryl Bem's "Feeling the Future" (2011, JPSP) reported nine experiments with combined p = 1.34 × 10⁻¹¹, using standard memory and priming tasks run in reversed temporal order. Multi-lab replications produced a range of results, with a 2012 replication (Ritchie, Wiseman, French) finding null results.

Dean Radin's presentiment studies reported above-chance physiological precursors to randomly selected emotional stimuli. Theoretical frameworks invoked include quantum retrocausality and time-symmetric formulations of quantum mechanics.
