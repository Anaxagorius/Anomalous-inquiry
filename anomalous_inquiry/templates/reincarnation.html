🔄 Reincarnation Cases

Ian Stevenson, former chairman of psychiatry at the University of Virginia, spent 40 years investigating children who claimed memories of previous lives. His methodology involved independent corroboration of claimed memories against verifiable historical facts, with interviewer blinding where possible.

Stevenson documented over 3,000 cases, finding highest case density in cultures with reincarnation belief (India, Sri Lanka, Myanmar, Turkey, Lebanon) but also Western cases. He identified patterns including birthmarks corresponding to claimed wounds in previous personality, unusual phobias, and behavior anomalies.

Jim Tucker (UVA) continues this research. Critics argue cultural expectation shapes reporting, that verification may be post-hoc, and that informant accuracy is difficult to assess retroactively.
