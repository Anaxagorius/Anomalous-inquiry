👁️ Remote Viewing

Remote viewing is a structured practice for acquiring information about a distant or unseen target using ostensibly extrasensory means. It emerged from classified U.S. government research in the 1970s under project names SCANATE, GONDOLA WISH, and eventually STARGATE.

Physicists Russell Targ and Harold Puthoff at SRI International developed formalized protocols. Ingo Swann contributed "Coordinate Remote Viewing" (CRV), a staged protocol for novice viewers. The CIA-commissioned AIR review (1995) concluded positive effects existed but that the program lacked operational usefulness.

Independent replication attempts have produced varied results. The Jahn-Dunne remote perception studies at PEAR reported above-chance correspondence using binary scoring on standardized judging forms.
