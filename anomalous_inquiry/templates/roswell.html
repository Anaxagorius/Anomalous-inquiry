🌵 Roswell (1947)

In July 1947, debris was recovered from a ranch approximately 75 miles north of Roswell, New Mexico. The Roswell Army Air Field initially announced the recovery of a "flying disc," then quickly retracted the statement, claiming it was a weather balloon. In 1994, the Air Force attributed the debris to Project Mogul — a classified balloon array for monitoring Soviet nuclear tests.

Multiple witnesses over subsequent decades claimed to have seen unusual materials and, in some cases, non-human bodies. Stanton Friedman, Kevin Randle, and Donald Schmitt conducted extensive witness interviews. The 1995 GAO report found that most Roswell-era RAAF outgoing messages had been destroyed.

The case remains contested. Skeptics argue the confusion is explained by Project Mogul secrecy; advocates argue the subsequent cover narrative itself contains inconsistencies.
