🎯 Trance States

Trance denotes a heterogeneous category of altered consciousness including deep hypnotic states, meditative absorption (jhana), shamanic journey states, and ritual possession. Common features include narrowed attentional focus, reduced executive monitoring, enhanced suggestibility, and amnesia upon emergence.

Ethnographic research (Eliade, 1951; Harner, 1980) documents trance-based information acquisition across cultures, typically involving percussive acoustic stimulation at 4–7 Hz (theta) inducing auditory driving. Neuroimaging studies of deep hypnosis and meditation show reduced default mode network activity and altered prefrontal-subcortical connectivity.

The overlap with psi research includes attempts to use trance states to enhance ESP performance, medium trance protocols, and cases where trance-derived information was subsequently verified against external records.
