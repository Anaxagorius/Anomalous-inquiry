🔆 Visionary Experiences

Visionary experiences include entheogen-induced states, spontaneous mystical experiences, and near-death-adjacent visions. Common phenomenological features across cultures include dissolution of self-other boundaries, encounter with entities or presences, and a sense of accessing fundamental reality.

Rick Strassman's DMT studies (1990–1995) at UNM documented entity encounters in a substantial minority of participants, with detailed phenomenological consistency across subjects. Recent psilocybin research (Johns Hopkins, Imperial College London) has produced evidence of lasting positive psychological change from mystical-type experiences.

The intersection with anomalous research includes veridical information obtained during visionary states, cross-cultural entity consistencies suggesting a shared phenomenological space, and philosophical questions about the ontological status of visionary content.
